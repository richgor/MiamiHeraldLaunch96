Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

THE QUEST FOR AN IDEAL COMMUNITY IS AS OLD AS HISTORY. THIS SERIES LOOKS AT THREE SEARCHES FOR UTOPIA:

  • Today: Weary of the isolation of modern life, a Broward group is hatching a residential subdivision that would feature a shared ``living room,'' shared meals and a real sense of community. 1A.

  • Saturday: Twenty-five years ago, ``a post-apocalypse city'' was founded in Arizona to thwart an ecological catastrophe -- urban sprawl. What it's like today. Living & Arts.

  • Sunday: Architects of the New Urbanism want to make our suburbs more like traditional towns. A new development in Jupiter will be the South Florida prototype. Home.
