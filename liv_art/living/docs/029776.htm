Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

In bathroom, kids learn privacy -- or the facts of life

Q. My 2 1/2-year-old daughter is in the bathroom with me a lot, and I don't know how to explain the menstrual cycle to her. I don't want to scare her with the sight of blood, so I try to keep her out, but then she cries.
-- Priscilla
Lee, San
Francisco


A.

Some Child Life readers were disturbed about telling a young child anything about the menstrual cycle.

``There is no way to explain to a 2 1/2-year-old that there is blood coming out of you when you haven't been wounded,'' says Bonnie, a reader from Miami.

Many other mothers, though, say they found that offering a simple explanation worked well.

``I calmly told them that this was my extra blood that helped me grow a baby inside my body when the time was right,'' says Sallianne Hines of Litchfield, Minn., a mother of three. ``I explained that if a baby was growing, it would need this blood. But if there was no baby, this blood just came out.''

Many parents who take this approach with their children do so to teach sex education in a natural progression.

Keep the explanations simple and truthful, recommends Karen Shanor, a clinical psychologist in Washington, D.C.

``Even a 2 1/2-year-old can understand that blood goes all around the body and delivers food to every part so it can grow,'' Shanor says. ``Then, you can say a mommy has blood in her uterus in case there's a baby there that the blood needs to feed. If there's no baby there, you don't need the blood, so it just comes out.''

Shanor also advises teaching a child that everyone is entitled to privacy in the bathroom.

More tips:

  • If your child's questions catches you off guard, say, ``That's a good question, and I'm not sure. But I will find out.'' Then, be sure to come back with an answer.

  • When faced with a question about reproduction, many adults laugh as a nervous reflex. Don't.

  • Answer just what's asked. If you're not sure what is being asked, ask your child. Give information in small bits, then wait for the next question.
