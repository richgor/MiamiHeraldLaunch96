TRENDS TO HELP YOU COPE WITH CHANGING '90s

A sampling of what Faith Popcorn views as some of the sizzling new social trends in Clicking, 16 Trends to Future Fit Your Life, Your Work, Your Business.

  • Anchoring: ``Reaching back to our spiritual roots, taking what was comforting from the past in order to be securely anchored in the future.'' Examples: Soaring church attendance, the popularity of angels, yoga classes, bestsellers like The Celestine Prophecy.

  • FemaleThink: ``A new set of business and social values, encouraging us to shift marketing consciousness from the traditional goal-oriented, hierarchal models to the more caring, sharing familial ones.'' Example: Saturn car dealers with child-care areas, The Body Shop's no-animal-testing policy.

  • Mancipation: ``A NewThink that goes beyond being `strictly business' and warmly embraces the freedom of being an individual.'' Examples: Unisex fragrances like CK, male skirts, a Discover magazine article in which the male writer explored the medical possibilities of breast-feeding his own children.

  • Icon toppling: ``A new socioquake has transformed mainstream America and the world, forcing us to question our monuments of business/government, the long accepted `pillars of society.' '' Examples: AT&T and massive corporate layoffs, Hard Copy and its cousins airing society's dirtiest lingerie.


  • Pleasure Revenge: ``Consumers, tired of all the rules and regulations, want to cut loose and have secret bacchanals with a bevy of forbidden fruits.'' Examples: Hebrew National hot dog sales are up, the red meat renaissance, the cigar craze, the spread of coffee shops.
