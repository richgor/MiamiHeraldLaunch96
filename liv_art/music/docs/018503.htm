Philharmonic plans Brahms Fest in June

The Florida Philharmonic is extending its season, originally scheduled to end this month, into June for a three-day Brahms Festival.

``We have enough orchestral services to put on a small festival within our budget,'' says Philharmonic music director James Judd, ``so we'll try to raise money with this festival.''

June 3, a host of soloists will perform Brahms chamber music at Miami's Gusman Center. June 5 and 7, Judd will conduct the Philharmonic and chorus plus vocal soloists to be announced in performances of Brahms' German Requiem at Broward's Bailey Hall, then Gusman Center.

Most of the soloists will donate their services, and the performers in the chamber series are lustrous: The duo-piano team Lisitsa and Kuznetsoff will play Brahms' Variations on a theme of Haydn in the two-piano version, and pianist Joseph Kalichstein, violinist Elissa Lee Kokkonen and principal horn Andrew Lewinter perform the E-flat Major Horn Trio, six piano pieces, Op. 118, and cellist William De Rosa will tackle the E minor Cello Sonata.

So why Brahms? ``Next year is the 100th anniversary of his death, and though we're doing a little of his music next season, I thought since we have the festival within budget now, why not anticipate his anniversary and do something special?'' Judd said.

Tickets are priced from $15, and students may purchase two tickets at $5 each on the evening of performances after 7 p.m. by presenting a valid full-time student I.D. Call 930-1812, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. weekdays, in Dade, Broward and Palm Beach.
JAMES ROOS
