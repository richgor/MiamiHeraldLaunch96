Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Diet guru still has pure passion for food

By CANDACE A. WEDLAN
Los Angeles Times

Fitness guru Richard Simmons, 47, sat perched at the island counter in his big, cozy kitchen, where he shared his low-fat cookies, his love for his four Dalmatians -- ``They become your children, and it's so hard because you're never supposed to give dogs table food'' -- and his passion for food. ``I'm an ex-fatty who still thinks like a fatty but doesn't look like one anymore.''

Q. Just how much do you love food?

A. I am a compulsive eater, and I always will be. I sold pralines -- those luscious New Orleans delicacies -- on the street corner when I was 7. I was raised in the middle of all these unbelievable restaurants. I will always love anything fried. I'm talking about cement from a bench deep-fried -- I would eat that. I can eat breakfast at 7 and eat another complete breakfast at 8. It's because I just adore food. I'll always love fried chicken, biscuits and gravy. I was raised that way. It was God, family and food was third. My love for food led to obesity, which led to eating disorders.

Q. A day never goes by that you don't exercise?

A. Never. Today, I'll do three hours of exercise.

Q. What's the minimum you'll work out?

A. An hour every day, and I have to do it in the morning. I've got to get out of bed at 4:30, get on the floor and do my sit-ups. Afterward, I do my stretching, and then I have to get down and do as many push-ups as I can -- and I hate those push-ups -- and I have to stretch my legs. I do it all to music.

Q. What are your meals like when you stick to a diet?

A. Breakfast, I'll have a bagel with fat-free cream cheese, and I go for fruit instead of juice. I'll peel an orange or grapefruit. And vitamins and a couple of large glasses of water. And I try not to eat between meals. When I'm home I open the fridge 20 times during the day -- bong, bong, Tupperware tops will be popping up -- just for viewing like a museum. And dinner: breast of chicken, a baked potato and vegetables. Whether I'm home or when I travel, I can always order a big salad with oil and vinegar on the side. I don't even look at the menu. I just know every place is going to have salad or chicken. I eat red meat once a week, and I watch the portions. Every Saturday night, I have dessert, which could be pecan pie if I'm in the South or cheesecake in New York.

Q. So you don't have to give up your passion for food?

A. Yeah. There's some people who grow their own tofu and sprouts. I'm not that kind of person. You know, I got on a plane, and there was a fitness celebrity. Seven-thirty in the morning, and she was eating a cold yam wrapped in tinfoil. And I said to myself, ``I could just never do that.''
