CLASSICAL MUSIC

  • Aspiring composers: Lukas Foss, one of America's more distinguished composers, is coming to town for the annual May in Miami Festival, a shared venture between Florida International University and the New World Symphony. He'll be a featured composer at three concerts slated for Wednesday at 1, 4 and 8 p.m. in FIU's Graham Center Ballroom. The concerts will also spotlight aspiring composers from the Americas and Asia. The 8 p.m. program features Foss and FIU composers Fredrick Kaufman, Orlando Jacinto García and Jon Christopher Nelson. Call (305) 348-2896 for information about the FIU events.

  • Opera redux: So Verdi's A Masked Ball has come and gone and you thought the opera season was over, right? Wrong. Beginning May 10, Handel's Julius Caesar, the same florid Baroque period piece that vaulted Beverly Sills to overnight fame as a coloratura soprano with the New York City Opera, will receive four performances -- two in Miami Beach, two in Fort Lauderdale -- with singers of the Florida Grand Opera's Young Artist program.

    This is the first time in its 12-year history that the Young Artist program will offer productions in both Dade and Broward. Jennifer Swanson and Darci Bultema will alternate as Cleopatra -- the role that brought Sills renown. Claudia Zahn, who will soon make her New York debut directing Donizetti's Lucrezia Borgia for Opera Manhattan, will stage the FGO production.

    Performances are 8 p.m. Friday and 2 p.m. May 12 at Miami Beach's Colony Theatre, and 8 p.m. May 14 and 16 at the intimate Amaturo Theatre in the Broward Center for the Performing Arts. Tickets cost $15 for all performances. Call (305) 854-7890 in Dade, (800) 741-1010 elsewhere in South Florida.
