Published Friday, May 3, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Asian film fest . . . soccer extravaganza . . . local rockers roll

TODAY

  • If you're hooked on the movies of director John Woo and martial arts whiz Jackie Chan, then you might want to check out some of their compatriots' work at the second annual Hollywood Asian Film Festival, which features films from Taiwan, Hong Kong, China and the United States on Friday and Saturday. Moonlight Boy from Taiwan will be screened at 7:30 tonight, and The Happy Chef from Hong Kong will be shown at 9:30 at the Hollywood Central Performing Arts Center, 1770 Monroe St. Tickets cost $5 per movie. A $10 ticket will include a reception featuring Asian food starting at 6 p.m. Friday, plus admission to the first movie. Call 929-8175. On Saturday, the festival moves to the AMC Sheridan 12 Theatres, 4999 Sheridan St., Hollywood, where Maya Lin: A Strong, Clear Vision from the United States will be shown at 7:30 p.m., followed by Unwelcomed Lady from China at 9:30 p.m. Tickets cost $5 per movie. Call 987-4680.
  • Turn on to Florida's Best Kept Secrets, two nights of blistering modern rock sponsored by ASCAP and performed by local unknowns for industry fat cats. Tonight's lineup: Amanda Green, Joe Popp, Joey Sunshine, The Underbellies and Big Engine. Saturday: Orgasmic Bliss, I Don't Know, The Psychles, Superball, Pagan Saints. Showtime is 9 tonight and Saturday at Rose's Bar & Lounge, 754 Washington Ave., Miami Beach. Admission is free. Call 532-0228.

  • Goooooooooaaaaaaal. To call attention to the upcoming South Florida Olympic Soccer Competition, coming up in July, more than 100 volunteers will kick, push and prod a four-foot soccer ball from Jupiter to Miami. The 96-mile run started Thursday and continues at 8 a.m. today in Fort Lauderdale, winding south through Little Haiti to Miami Beach, across Biscayne Bay to Bayside Marketplace, over the Brickell Bridge through Little Havana and finally into Cocowalk at 5 p.m. today. A finish line bash with Brazilian dancers, Izzy the mascot and Peruvian soccer player Nene Cubillas, who will sign autographs, starts immediately after at 5 p.m. at Cocowalk, 3015 Grand Ave., Coconut Grove. Admission is free. Call 373-8633.

  • Steve Miller, the rocker who made ``I really love your peaches, wanna shake your tree'' a cult phrase, brings his psychedelic '70s anthems to the Sunrise Musical Theater this weekend. Hear college kegger favorites like Take the Money and Run, Jungle Love and Swingtown at 8 tonight and Saturday at Sunrise Musical Theatre, 5555 NW 95th Ave., Sunrise. Tickets are $32.75, $23.75. Call (954) 741-7300.

    SATURDAY

  • South Florida isn't exactly known for its mountains, unless you count Mt. Trashmore, which we'd rather not. But that doesn't mean we don't know a thing or two about mountain biking. Witness the two-day Bud Light Mountain Bike Festival, a 7-to-10 mile yearly cycling competition being held this year along the bike trails of Oleta State Recreational Area in North Miami. There's also a Triathlon, Slalom Challenge, Kids Competitions, a Fun and Fitness Ride and a Restaurant and Bar Charity Ride to benefit The Children's Home Society. The Mountain Bike Expo starts at 9 a.m. Sunday at Oleta State Recreational Area, 3400 NE 163rd St., North Miami. Registration fees are $20 on race day, $15 in advance. There is a $3.25 park admission fee. Registration and other events start at 3 p.m. Saturday and continue that day with a barbecue and live music at 7 p.m. The festival winds down at 3 p.m. Sunday with a party and free buffet at Shooters, 3501 NE 163rd St., North Miami. Prizes include $2,000 cash, a Diamondback mountain bike, Air South tickets to next year's festival in Atlanta and lots of sports gear. To compete, you must bring a bike and a helmet. For a complete schedule and prices, call (407) 241-3801.
  • An antique car show, rock and roll music by Majic 102.7 radio (WMXJ 102.7 FM), contests, hourly raffles, an Elvis impersonator and animals from five shelters will be available for adoption at the Nifty Fifty Pet Adoptathon. Hours are 11 a.m.-11 p.m. Saturday at 2070 Griffin Rd., Dania. Call 989-3977.

    SUNDAY

  • Schmooze with Andy Garcia, Tom Arnold, Danny Glover, Arsenio Hall, Edwin Moses and Olympic athletes including Katarina Witt, at the glitzy charity brunch and auction for the South Florida Inner City Games. Items up for bid include a green BMW roadster, Hollywood memorabilia and walk-on parts in upcoming films. The event runs from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. Sunday at Planet Hollywood, 3390 Mary St., Coconut Grove. Tickets are $100. Call 673-0021.
