Interactive programs let kids play with 2 classics



By ROY BASSAVE
Herald Staff Writer

Goldilocks and the Three Bears and Little Red Riding Hood are two new interactive adaptations of the famous Brothers Grimm stories, from Packard Bell Interactive.

These animated storybooks can help develop an early love for reading and an array of other important skills.

Designed for children ages 4-9, the CD-ROMs have hundreds of animated areas allowing your child to interact by using the computer mouse. Each includes an original musical score with sing-along features.

To provide a jump on preschool skills, the CD-ROMs have skill-building games and activities that teach literacy, counting, creativity and more. Games and activities such as coloring and dot-to-dots are designed to help build strong creative abilities and vivid imaginations. Children can print out black and white outlines of any page and create their own coloring books. Or they can color the ``live'' animations on the screen and print them out in color, if you have a color printer. The games and skill-building activities can keep kids engaged for hours.

The CD will also play on your home or car audio CD player; you can hear the story and soundtrack. The program comes with a bound copy of the original storybook, so kids can hear the stories from Mom and Dad at bedtime -- no computer required.

Other titles from Packard Bell Interactive are The Little Engine, Dinosaur in the Garden and Milly Fitzwilly's Mouse Center.



Goldilocks and the Three Bears or Little Red Riding Hood -- An ``A+ Learning Series'' Interactive Storybook

PC minimum system requirements: 386/33Mhz or better, 8MB of RAM, Super VGA card, color monitor, double-speed CD-ROM drive, Windows 3.1 or higher.

Macintosh system requirements: LC III or better, 8MB of RAM, color monitor, double-speed CD-ROM drive, System 7.0.1 or higher.

Estimated selling price, manufacturer: $49.99 each CD-ROM; Packard Bell Interactive.
