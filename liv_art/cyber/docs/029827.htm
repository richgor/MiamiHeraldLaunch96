Dinotopia explores land of humans, dinosaurs

By ROY BASSAVE
Herald Staff Writer

If you enjoy the writings of Jules Verne and J.R.R. Tolkien, you'll enjoy the magic of Dinotopia: Living the Adventure CD-ROM, which is based on James Gurney's 1992 book Dinotopia: A World Apart .

This CD-ROM version offers a superb excursion into a fabulous realm where humans and dinosaurs live together in harmony. The game lures players into a journey of discovery in a majestic new world.

Dinotopia combines special effects puppetry with enriched 3-D graphics animation and blue-screen technology featuring live actors with nonviolent action.

Test your skills through interaction with dinosaurs and humans. Experience new worlds, learn a new language, create musical melodies, play card games, solve intricate puzzles and navigate a submarine through an underwater world.

Plot: 17-year-old twins, Nathan and Constance, are separated after their ship sinks during a storm. Nathan finds himself washed up on a beach, but Constance is nowhere to be found.

As Nathan wanders into the rain forest, he discovers a civilization of living dinosaurs and humans. Making his way to the Dinotopian city of Volcaneum, Nathan finds his sister's locket being used as a prize in an intricate card game. Nathan joins the game, wins the locket and begins his quest for Constance.

Dinotopia works better under real DOS; when running under Windows 95 you might find in rare cases that your PC locks up. If it does, click on your MS-DOS icon for the game and go to the Properties folder, then check where it says ``DOS will not detect Windows.'' This should work.

If you need game hints, get Dinotopia: The Official Strategy Guide ($16.99) from Prima Publishing; for more information call (800) 294-0022. Web address: http://www.turner.com/dinotopia

DINOTOPIA: LIVING THE ADVENTURE

PC minimum system requirements: an IBM or 100 percent compatible 66Mhz 486, 8MB RAM, DOS 5.0 or later, double-speed CD-ROM, SVGA monitor, 16-bit Soundblaster or 100 percent compatible sound card, and a mouse. Works under Windows 95.

Estimated selling price, manufacturer: $50; CD-ROM with illustrated book, Dinotopia: A Land Apart From Time is under $60; Turner Interactive.
