Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Ethnic-couched comedy . . . Harlem dancers . . . Try Faith

TODAY

  • ``Of all the people that got fired from the post office and came back to shoot the post office up, how many of them were black or Mexican?'' asks comedian Carlos Mencia. ``None. White people, when you snap, you go too far.'' Mencia, host of HBO's Loco Slam specials, performs his ethnic-couched comedy at the Comedy Zone this weekend. Showtimes are 8, 10 and midnight tonight, Saturday and 10 p.m. Sunday at the Comedy Zone, 1121 Washington Ave., Miami Beach. Tickets are $15. Call 672-4788.

  • She carried you in her womb for nine months, the least you can do is take her out once a year, right? Now, stand up straight and treat mom to a Mothers' Serenade from 7 to 10 tonight on Sunset Drive and 59th Avenue, South Miami. The streetside concert features live performances by Enrique Chia, the Joe Michael Dancers, Imagen Peruana Dancers and the South Miami Elementary Choir. All mothers will receive a complimentary gift from the Indoor Gardener Florist and restaurants such as Chilango's Mexican Grill, Christy's, Mozart Stube, the Argentina Grill and others will provide food and drink. [At last, mom will applaud your taste. Call 665-7065.

  • If you missed last year's Faith Healer, the Carbonell award-winning drama about a traveling Irish charlatan and the people whose lives he touches, the luck of the Irish is with ye. The New World Repertory Company revives its production at 8 tonight through May 19. Showtimes are 8 p.m. Thursday, Friday and Saturday and 2 p.m. Saturday and Sunday at the New World School of the Arts, Louise O. Gerrits Theater, 25 NE Second St., Miami. Tickets are $16 for adults; $8 students/senior citizens. Call 237-7635.

  • New Orleans rockers The Radiators perform their soul-drenched rock with Grateful Dead knockoffs Crazy Fingers and Iko-Iko. Doors open at 8 tonight at The Club at 245 22nd St. (formerly the Club Nu building) Miami Beach. Tickets are $18. Call Ticketmaster or 532-4340.

    SATURDAY

  • Society Ties, the Young Professional Group of the Leukemia Society of America, invites you to visit Boogie Wonderland at their ``Slip Into the '70s'' fund-raiser. The polyester-friendly party includes a champagne reception and hors d'oeuvres followed by all the mindless disco melodies you can stomach. It starts at 7 p.m. Saturday at Bash, 655 Washington Ave., Miami Beach. Admission is $20 for nonmembers; $15 for members. Call 538-2274.

  • We've done your Mother's Day gift shopping for you (No need to thank us): Take her to the 11th annual Mother's Day Arts, Crafts and Gifts Show, which promises more than 130 exhibits and three stages of music by the oldies' group the Fabulons, Tina Edwards and Southern Guns. Clowns, puppet shows, magic demonstrations and face painting will also be available for children. Hours are 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Saturday and Sunday, along Main Street at Northwest 67th Avenue, Miami Lakes. Call 817-4198.

  • The Caribbean Performing Arts Ensemble, a troupe of 100 singers, dancers and actors ages 9 to 25, presents Catch the Spirit of Peace. The show embraces efforts to educate audiences about drugs, Caribbean culture, family values and AIDS awareness through song and dance. Showtime is 8 p.m. Saturday at Bailey Concert Hall, Broward Community College, 3501 SW Davie Rd., Davie and 4 and 8 p.m. Sunday at the Gusman Center for the Performing Arts, 174 E. Flagler St., Miami. Tickets are $20-$25 for adults; $13 children. Call 954-963-7350.

  • Never mind Sweatin' to the Oldies, gear up for The Greater Miami Race for the Cure, a marathon walk and run to benefit the YWCA and breast cancer research and treatment programs. Events include a 5K Coed Run, 5K Coed Walk and one-mile Fun Run. Registration on race day starts at 7:30 a.m. Saturday; the walk starts at 8 a.m at Bayfront Park, 305 Biscayne Blvd., Miami. NBC-6 medical reporter Ileana Bravo will host an awards ceremony at 10 a.m., followed by live music, appearances by Y-100 DJs, prizes, refreshments and other activities. Two women teams are encouraged to sign up for the ``In the Pink'' program for $100. In the Pink participants will receive a pretty-in-pink T-shirt and goodie bag. Race registration packets are available at J.C. Penney's and Pier One; registration fee is $20. Call 977-YWCA.

    REST OF THE WEEK

  • It's a dance thing: The world-renowned Dance Theatre of Harlem performs two programs of different ballets, including Signs and Wonders, choreographed to traditional African music and The Prodigal Son, by George Balanchine. Signs and Wonders, The Prodigal Son and Dougla will be performed at 8 p.m. Thursday and 8 p.m. May 18. Concerto in F, Medea and Firebird will be performed at 8 p.m. May 17 and 2 p.m. May 18 at the Jackie Gleason Theater, 1700 Washington Ave., Miami Beach. Tickets are $15 to $50. Call Ticketmaster.
