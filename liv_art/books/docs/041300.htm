AUTHOR! AUTHOR!

PLS. RUSH TO ALBERT IN FRANCE: Ernest J. Gaines, with his wife Dianne, was honored at the Salon du Livre.

GOOD NEWS
FOR GAINES:

Ernest J. Gaines, whose 1993 novel, A Lesson Before Dying, won the National Book Critics Circle Award and was a finalist for the fiction Pulitzer Prize, has been named a Chevalier of France's Order of Arts and Letters.

The award, established in 1957, was presented to Gaines by Minister of Culture Philippe Douste-Blazy during the Salon du Livre, France's foremost publishing event, which this year honored the U.S. literary tradition. Past winners include Susan Sontag, William Styron, Saul Bellow, Ralph Ellison, Stephen King, Francine du Plessix Gray, Mary McCarthy and Surfside's late Nobel laureate, Isaac Bashevis Singer.

Gaines is married to the former Dianne Saulney Smith, a former assistant Dade County attorney and the first African-American on the Florida Bar's board of governors. Gaines was born in Oscar, La., and is writer in residence at the University of Southwestern Louisiana in Lafayette. He has spent this spring teaching creative writing in France.
IN THE GREEN

And speaking of Stephen King:

The Green Mile, his new serialized novel, is selling much faster than expected. ``Four times the normal pace of a Stephen King paperback,'' says Alex Holtz, senior vice president of Signet, the Penguin USA imprint publishing the book. ``We're reprinting as fast as we can.''

Mile went on sale March 25 with 2.6 million copies in print; now, after seven printings, there are 3.1 million copies. So far, The Two Dead Girls and The Mouse on the Mile are only available segments. Coffey's Hands is due May 24, to be followed by The Bad Death of Eduard Delacroix on June 24; Night Journey on July 29 and Coffey on the Mile on Aug. 26. Best news: The segments retail for $2.99 each.
WE'VE GOT EYE STRAIN
ALREADY

Big news for late summer and fall from Alfred A. Knopf:

Watch for Servant of the Bones by Anne Rice (August); The Last Thing He Wanted, Joan Didion's first novel in 12 years (September); In the Beginning by Karen Armstrong (September); Golf Dreams: Writings on Golf by John Updike (September); The Dangerous Old Woman by Clarissa Pinkola Estes (October); A Regular Guy by Mona Simpson (October) and a not-yet-titled novel from Michael Crichton (November).
