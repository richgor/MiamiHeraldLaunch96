Published Friday, May 3, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Books that celebrate the parent-kid bond

Mother's Day is coming soon, and Father's Day is not far behind. In honor of the two holidays, we've assembled a list of books that truly celebrate the parent-child relationship (mostly mothers, in this case) -- the warmth, the tenderness, the caring. These are not new books, but they're ones worth owning and keeping, books that will never lose their appeal, or their importance.

  • Are You My Mother? P.D. Eastman. Random House. $7.99 (Spanish paperback, $9.95).

  • Guess How Much I Love You? Sam McBratney. Illustrated by Anita Jeram. Candlewick Press. $14.95.

  • Just Me and My Mom. Mercer Mayer. Golden Books, Western Publishing Co. $2.25.

  • Just Me and My Dad. Mercer Mayer. Golden Books, Western Publishing Co. $2.25.

  • Love You Forever. Robert Munsch. Illustrated by Sheila McGraw. Firefly Books. $4.95 (paper).

  • Momma, Do You Love Me? Barbara M. Joosse. Illustrated by Barbara Lavallee. Chronicle. $13.95.

  • Momma, If You Had a Wish. Jeanne Modesitt. Illustrated by Robin Spowart. Green Tiger Press, $14.

  • A Mother for Choco. Keiko Kasza. Putnam. $14.95.

  • Mommies. Dian Curtis Regan. Illustrated by Mary Morgan. Cartwheel. $5.95.

  • The Runaway Bunny. Margaret Wise Brown. Illustrated by Clement Hurd. HarperCollins. $13 (paper $4.95)


  • Where's My Mom? Leon Rosselson. Illustrated by Priscilla Lamont. Candlewick Press. $13.95 ($5.99 paper).

    -- BETH DUNLOP
