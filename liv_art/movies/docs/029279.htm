Published Saturday, May 4, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Costume may bust starlet's movie career

Knight-Ridder News Service

Call it the Curse of the Costume, but cinematic history tells us that whenever an actress dons movie action-wear, her career is placed in jeopardy larger than any faced in the movie that requires it.

It's conceivable that Barb Wire's Pamela Anderson Lee is already such an icon that nothing can stop her march to world domination. And Jane Fonda eventually rebounded from the bust of Barbarella, if you'll excuse the expression. But all too often, the fate of the breastplated or spandex-wrapped heroine is, as they tend to say in movies like these, worse than death. Consider:

  • Helen Slater -- Blond, beautiful Slater was considered a major discovery when she won the coveted role of Supergirl in 1984. But the film stopped her ascent to stardom like Kryptonite.

  • Tanya Roberts -- Having kicked behinds and taken names on television's Charlie's Angels, she was considered the perfect choice to wear the loincloth in 1984's Sheena, based on the pulp and comic exploits of the Queen of the Jungle. Instead, she was lost in the jungle.

  • Sandahl Bergman -- The muscular, seductive Bergman made a big impression in All That Jazz, but as the pulp fiction female barbarian in 1985's Red Sonja, she drew no blood.

  • Lori Ann Petty -- Petty was coming along nicely as a character actress, thanks, then she got in the postpunk gear of the comic's hot-tempered Tank Girl. It, and Petty, tanked.
