Published Tuesday, April 23, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Class unimpressed by perverse Substitute


By JACKIE POTTS
Herald Staff Writer

In The Substitute, Tom Berenger plays a hard-as-nails high school teacher who subscribes to the Rambo ethic of teaching. When his unruly students misbehave, he makes them write ``I'm sorry'' 100 times on the blackboard. If that doesn't work, he throws books or cans of soda at them. And if they're really bad, he breaks out the automatic weapons.

Ludicrously conceived and laughably executed, The Substitute is a perverse action film about an out-of-work mercenary (Berenger) posing as a substitute teacher to avenge an attack on his teacher-girlfriend (Diane Venora).

The movie was shot at the historic, gracefully arched Miami High School, which has been rechristened Columbus High and transformed into a hellish, graffiti-laden institution populated by every type of racial movie stereotype. The students pass through metal detectors and barbed-wire-topped fences to get to class. Fights frequently break out on the grounds, and teachers are afraid to walk to their cars after school.

As their pistol-packing savior, Berenger is the mysterious ``Mr. Smith,'' a man with an ugly scar on one cheek and a laughable armory of tough-guy lines. During one of his first days at the blackboard, he growls, ``I am the warrior chief . . . Don't f--- with my universe.''

He soon identifies the school's toughest gang -- the K.O.D. or Kings of Destruction -- and uncovers an intricate web of drug smuggling and corruption that apparently ranges from downtown Miami to the Miccosukee Indian Village.

The head of the K.O.D.'s drug ring is the principal, a smooth-talking, expensively dressed ex-cop (Ernie Hudson), who thinks nothing of unloading bags of cocaine in the school basement on parent-teacher night.

Luckily, Mr. Smith knows how to handle these situations. He simply calls in his former mercenary pals, hauling a truckload of spy gadgets and heavy artillery. High school was never so, ahem, educational -- So, you say the dog ate your homework? Hasta la vista, baby.
