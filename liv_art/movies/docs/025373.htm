Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

FRANK & OLLIE: Disney animators Frank Thomas and Ollie Johnston started their careers together in the 1930s and remain inseparable pals today.

The story of Disney animators is as rich as their enduring work

By RENE RODRIGUEZ
Herald Movie Critic

The popularity of Walt Disney animation is at an all-time high, which makes it a perfect time for Frank & Ollie, a documentary about two of Disney's original animating team (or the ``Nine Old Men,'' as Walt himself affectionately called them).

The movie tells the story of Frank Thomas and Ollie Johnston, who first went to work at the Disney studios in the 1930s. Throughout their long careers, the pair worked on some of the company's most beloved classics, including Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, Pinocchio, Bambi, Lady and the Tramp, The Jungle Book and Robin Hood. Hearing the pair talk about making these films is fascinating, particularly since many of them were such groundbreaking efforts in their time -- and many have never been equaled.

Most people think of animators as skilled artists, but Frank & Ollie reveals that the best in the craft were also terrific actors: The only way to get a sketch to take on a personality that transcends paper and ink is to imbue it with some of your own, and Thomas and Johnston show how much of themselves went into each of their cartoon characters. The men's memories remain razor-sharp, and their anecdotes provide a stimulating -- and often very funny -- glimpse into the creative process.

Frank & Ollie also traces the lifelong friendship between these two men, who became best buddies in college, moved into neighboring houses after marrying their respective wives, and today remain inseparable. Watching them complete each other's sentences and thoughts, you get to watch two artistic minds working in perfect unison, and the effect is as amazing as anything in the Disney vaults. Frank & Ollie is a treat for any animation lover, but it's equally rewarding to anyone interested in plain old human beings.

***

(PG)
