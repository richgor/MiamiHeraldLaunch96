SEEN 3 WAYS: Chazz Palminteri is in Mulholland, Faithful and Diabolique.

ALL THAT CHAZZ

When Chazz Palminteri steps out of the sleek Buick Roadmaster that serves as transportation for the stylish rogue cops of Mulholland Falls, he will find himself in contention for the all-time screen saturation record.

Palminteri can be seen in two other current movies, playing Sharon's Stone's sadistic lover in Diabolique and the hitman hired to take out Cher in Faithful.

So, is Palminteri the new Michael Caine, distinguished by his very ubiquity?

``Ahh, I knew somebody was going to say that,'' says Palminteri, expelling a large sigh into the telephone.

``What can I tell ya? I like to work,'' he says, sounding almost as weary as Moses looking into the valley. He must mean acting, not interviewing, but there is a reason Palminteri has come out of nowhere to become one of the sought-after character actors in film.

``Well, it's Hollywood, you know,'' says Palminteri. ``In this town, they tend to send you things they immediately think of you for, which means that I get a lot of cops and heavies. But I don't mind that if they're something more than that. What you're really looking for is something three-dimensional, something you can really act.''

-- Knight-Ridder
