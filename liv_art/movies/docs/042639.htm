Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

THE CRAFT, A THRILLER ABOUT HIGH SCHOOL GIRLS WHO DABBLE IN THE SUPERNATURAL, WAS THE NO. 1 FILM OVER THE WEEKEND, BEATING OUT NEW FILMS STARRING SHARON STONE, DAMON WAYANS AND FRIENDS STAR DAVID SCHWIMMER.

Starring Neve Campbell of TV's Party of Five, ``The Craft'' earned an estimated $7 million in its premiere weekend, industry sources said Sunday.

The Truth About Cats and Dogs was second with $6 million, followed by The Quest, with $4.1 million.

The top 10 movies are based on industry estimates of ticket sales Friday through Sunday.

1. The Craft, $7 million.

2. The Truth About Cats and Dogs, $6 million.

3. The Quest, $4.1 million.

4. Primal Fear, $3.75 million.

5. The Great White Hype, $3.3 million.

6. Last Dance, $2.7 million.

7. The Birdcage, $2.5 million.

8. James and the Giant Peach, $2.4 million.

9. The Pallbearer, $2.35 million.

10. Mulholland Falls, $2.27 million.
