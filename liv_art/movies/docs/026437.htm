HOW TO MAKE AN AMERICAN QUILT
*** An enthusiastically old-fashioned rumination on love, told from the female point of view. A young bride-to-be (Winona Ryder) spends a summer at her grandmother's house, where she meets a handsome suitor and starts having doubts about her upcoming marriage. She turns to her grandmother's friends for advice, and each tells a story about her own romance. Director Jocelyn Moorhouse (Proof ) embraces melodrama and weepy sentiment but never lets the movie dissolve into schmaltz -- its tone is warm and gentle, and the huge cast turns in unselfish performances. -- Rene Rodriguez (PG-13 vulgar language, sexual situations.) (1995, MCA/Universal, 117 minutes; available on video disc.)


WHITE MAN'S BURDEN
** John Travolta and Harry Belafonte star in this film set in a contemporary world where traditional race roles are reversed. It's a good gimmick -- but that's all you get, a gimmick. -- Rene Rodriguez (R; strong language, violence.) (1995, HBO, 89 minutes.)

MONEY TRAIN
** This disjointed mess will test the patience of even the most devout fans of Wesley Snipes and Woody Harrelson, whose chemistry made White Men Can't Jump such a hoot. Here, the pair play foster brothers and New York cops who, by film's end, are aboard a runaway subway loaded with transit authority receipts, debating whether to steal the cash. Before then, however, the story derails across so many different story tracks, you begin to wonder if there was ever a script. -- Rene Rodriguez (R; vulgar language, violence, sexual situations.) (1995, Columbia TriStar, 110 minutes; available on video disc.)

TOM AND HUCK
** A literary classic is reduced to an after-school special in this movie starring Jonathan Taylor Thomas of Home Improvement fame. Though touted as ``bad boys,'' this Tom and Huck could easily qualify for the church choir. Pretty bland, despite a murder and one foul-mouthed villain. -- Christine Dolen (PG; vulgar language, violence.) (1995, Disney, $19.99, 92 minutes.)

RECENT MOVIES

Fatally Yours (1996, Monarch, $92.95, 90 minutes). George Lazenby, Rick Rossovich, Roddy McDowall. Horror tale with Lazenby as a young husband who impulsively buys a rundown house and soon learns of its eerie past.

The Immortals (1995, Evergreen, 92 minutes). Eric Roberts, Tia Carrere, Tony Curtis. An elaborate heist unites eight strangers in simultaneous assaults on targets all over a city.

Watch Me (1995, Triboro, $92.95, 90 minutes). Jennifer Burton. A woman with a mysterious past gets caught up in her landlord's voyeuristic games.

Wild Side (1995, Evergreen, 96 minutes). Christopher Walken, Joan Chen. A beautiful young woman leads a double life: bank executive by day and high-priced call girl at night.

FOR CHILDREN

The Littlest Pet Shop (1996, LIVE, $12.98 each, 45 minutes each). Three tapes of episodes from the syndicated children's series that follows the adventures of the inhabitants of a miniature pet shop: Wrecking Havoc, Scaredy Dog and Treasure of Sierra Pet Shop .

MUSIC AND DANCE

Del Amitri -- Let's Go Home (1996, Polygram, $19.95, 60 minutes). The pop group in concert. Songs include Roll to Me .

More Abba Gold (1996, Polygram, $19.95, 60 minutes). The '70s group is seen in a series of videos. Songs include The Day Before You Came and Head Over Heels .

SPORTS

Back On Top -- Cats!: The Official 1996 NCAA Championship Video (1996, CBS, $19.98, 50 minutes). A look at the Kentucky Wildcats win of the recent NCAA men's basketball tournament.

MADE FOR TELEVISION

Mystery Science Theater 3000 (1992-94, Rhino, $19.95 each, 97 minutes). The first three episodes of a planned continuing release of episodes from the Comedy Central series: Amazing Colossal Man, Mitchell and The Cave Dwellers .

Sharon's Secret (1995, MCA/Universal, 91 minutes). Mel Harris, Candace Cameron. Made-for-USA thriller with Harris as a psychiatrist trying to help a troubled young girl who has been framed for the murder of her parents.
