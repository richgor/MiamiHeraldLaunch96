Published Friday, April 26, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Lesbians, body parts and all hell break loose in frantic Naked Killer

By RENE RODRIGUEZ
Herald Movie Critic

Just when you think you've seen it all, along comes a movie like Naked Killer to prove you wrong. This Hong Kong import might be the best serial-killing kung-fu-fighting gunslinging softcore-lesbian-dominatrix movie ever made: If you can't find something here to offend you, you're just not paying attention.

It's the story of Kitty (Chingmy Yau), a young and cheerful man-killer who becomes the apprentice of Sister Cindy (Kelly Yao), a resourceful Black Widow-type who executes rapists and murderers with her flying knives, head-crushing dropkicks -- and, when all else fails, her arsenal of guns.

Kitty proves such a talented student, she grabs the attention of Princess (Carrie Ng) and her girl-toy Baby (Svenwara Madoka), two castrating, man-hating lesbians who decide to wipe out their new competition (they can't stand anyone stealing their tabloid headlines).

But Princess soon develops a crush on Kitty, which leaves Baby fuming with jealousy. Meanwhile, Kitty fawns over Tinam (Simon Yam), an impotent cop who, after meeting Kitty, now wakes up each morning with a ``priapism.'' All the lovey-dovey stuff begins to annoy Sister Cindy (``Killer can't fall in love! Once she is in love, she can't kill with barbarity!'') It's only a matter of time before all proverbial hell breaks loose.

Naked Killer more than lives up to its title: There's a lot of killing in it, and there's a lot of naked flesh, too (some of that flesh isn't always alive, either; one of the movie's setpieces involves a search for a severed male part mixed up in a tray of breakfast sausages.) Like most Hong Kong films, the story moves like lightning, maybe a little too quickly: The plot rarely slows down for anything resembling characterization, and all the bodacious leather-clad murderesses are sometimes hard to tell apart.

Director Clarence Fok revs the action up to near-hysterical levels: One gunfight, set in a parking garage where Sister Cindy and Kitty battle an army of men, is so frantically edited it registers on a near-subliminal level. The speed delivers a giddy rush, though it'd be even better if you could actually follow what was going on.

The movie also has a twisted sense of humor that gleefully ignores any vestige of taste: When Sister Cindy first recruits Kitty, she locks her in a basement with a drooling rapist to test her mettle. Later, Kitty gets revenge by locking Cindy in the same cellar with two madmen; when Cindy disposes of both without so much as mussing her hair, Kitty gushes, ``I admire you so much!''

OK, so it's all pretty dumb. But it's also kinetic and wildly outrageous -- and, in the end, even romantic and sentimental. Naked Killer doesn't always make sense, but it's certainly got attitude: These chicks could teach even Sharon Stone a few new tricks with that icepick of hers.


** 1/2
NAKED KILLER
(unrated)

Cast: Simon Yam, Chingmy Yau, Carrie Ng, Kelly Yao, Svenwara Madoka.

Director: Clarence Fok.

Producer: Wong Jing.

Screenwriter: Wong Jing.

Cinematographer: William Yim.

Music: Lowell Lo.

A Rimm Films release. Running time: 85 minutes. Vulgar language, violence, gore, sexual situations, nudity, adult themes. In Cantonese with English and Chinese subtitles. In Dade only: Alliance.
