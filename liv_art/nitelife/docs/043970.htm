Published Friday, April 26, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

FOR WEEKEND

MANNY HERNANDEZ / For The Herald
REGGAE SOUNDS: The music of Broken Sound livens up Mombasa Bay Wednesday through Friday nights.

  • Mombasa Bay: 3051 NE 32nd Ave., Fort Lauderdale; 565-7441. Hours: 11:30 a.m. to 2 a.m. Sunday through Friday. 11:30 a.m. to 3 a.m. Saturday. Cover: Varies

    At Mombasa Bay, it's tough to tell the locals from the tourists. Everyone looks (and acts) like they're on vacation.

    On Sundays, the club's busiest night (ladies night), it's standing room only after 9 p.m., with the indoor restaurant packed with people jamming to house band RPI. A separate, but equally raucous, party takes place on the outdoor dock, brimming with folks showing off their weekend suntans, overused pick-up lines and snazzy boats.

    Under new ownership since January, the restaurant/bar has been infused with a much-needed dose of energy. And the music likely has something to do with the upsurge. Mombasa is the only bar in the neighborhood that offers reggae nightly. The lineup includes: Tisham (with Lee Milo) on Tuesdays (also ladies night), Broken Sound Wednesday through Friday and RPI Saturday through Monday -- all beginning at 9:30 p.m.

    Rum runners, raspberry coladas and margaritas are the favorites at the island-themed Mombasa Bay, along with Caribbean-influenced treats such as calypso chicken, conch fritters and bay shrimp. If you ever wanted to go to Jamaica, take a trip to Mombasa Bay -- in Fort Lauderdale.
