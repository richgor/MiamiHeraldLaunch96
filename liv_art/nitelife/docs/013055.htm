ON A ROLL: Ann Zinn, left, and David Lasseter can play pool, bowl or rock at Gary's.

  • Gary's Sports Bar and Grille: 5325 S. University Dr., Davie; 954-434-9680. Open 11 a.m. to 2 a.m. Sunday through Wednesday, 11 a.m. to 4 a.m. Thursday through Saturday. No cover.


    Gary's has most of what you would expect from a sports bar -- pool tables, satellite television, dozens of domestic beers, live music, huge bars -- but what makes it unusual is that it's tucked inside a large bowling alley (University Bowl). So if you get a midnight craving for beer and bowling, you can do both here.

    On Saturdays, crowds come to hearnationally known artists (granted, some are oldies), such as Survivor, Atlanta Rhythm Section, Flock of Seagulls.

    Other nightly lineups include karaoke on Mondays (bar tabs a prize), battle of the local bands on Wednesdays (four local bands compete), ladies night on Thursdays (ladies drink free from 9 p.m. to 11 p.m.) and live music on Fridays (usually, local rock and alternative bands) and Saturdays.

    A smooth pair of legs can be a lucrative asset here. On Thursday nights, the woman who wins takes home $300. To make the evening appeal to both sexes, Gary's hot legs contest is followed by a male revue.

    If Davie is not in your usual destination, Gary's Sports Bar & Grille has a second spot at 2200 N. Federal Hwy. in Pompano Beach (954-782-6340). The Pompano Beach location also offers bowling, but venue varies from the Davie one.
    -- FELICIA MICHELE HABER
