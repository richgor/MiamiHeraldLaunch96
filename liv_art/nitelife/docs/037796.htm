Published Friday, May 3, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

  • Felicia Michele Haber's weekly guide to upcoming events in Broward and North Dade isn't meant to be all-inclusive. For

    • more details, see listings, starting on this page. Friday: Kick off the weekend at Hollywood's newest one-stop party shop: Dave & Buster's; looooong wait for dinner; opt for munchies in adult-oriented arcade; virtual reality; hoops; pool; interactive games; bring lots of cash, it goes fast here. What's on tap? At BW-3 Grill & Pub, 24 imported and domestic beers; also, wings with 11 sauces -- try them all; Bahama Bob & Gimme 5 play. Gossip about Chuck and Di at The Laughing Onion, a Brit hangout.

    Saturday: Put on your best threads for Bermuda Bar in North Miami Beach; ladies with great outfits get $100 cash; free chocolate-covered strawberries and champagne all night. Got a big mouth? Shoot it off at Comedy Sportz; improvisational troupe; audience participation; not for the timid. Token night at River Rock Cafe in Fort Lauderdale; buy one drink, get a token for the next.

    Sunday: Bring your laundry money to Baja Beach Club in Fort Lauderdale; a quarter buys you a long-neck from 8 to 11 p.m.; women 18 and up welcome; sorry guys, men must be at least 21. Pretend you're on safari and sip exotic java from Kenya at ZanZbar on Las Olas; coffees brewed at your table; for people-watching vantage, sit outside. Live reggae party at Mombasa Bay; packed with tourists and locals; you pay $2 to sit inside, free if you prefer fresh air; a great place to dock your boat.

    Monday: Jump-start the work week at Java Joe's in Fort Lauderdale; caffeine, jazz, cappuccinos; denim rules. Don't miss your cue at Hot Shots in Sunrise; plenty of billiard tables; satellite TV; darts; leave designer duds at home. For jazz fans, Sha-Shaty performs at O'Hara's Pub on Las Olas Boulevard; sit inside or out; downtown Lauderdale crowd.

    Tuesday: E-mail your buddies at Cybernation in Fort Lauderdale; screen your dates; cappuccino and computer chitchat. Couch potatoes can veg at Playoffs Sports Grill. Billiards and video games if you can leave the bar stool at Norman's Billiard Club & Eatery in Miami, (19705 NW Second Ave.). What wine really goes best with your wild mushroom pasta? Learn while you drink at Oliver's' Wine Bar in Lighthouse Point; more than 70 wines stocked; romantic; date scene.

    Wednesday: Break up the week at Christopher's; ladies get discounted drinks; men flock here in droves; unintelligible pickup lines. For friendlier crowds hang out with the Irish; live tunes at Maguire's Hill 16; Gaelic games on satellite TV; Guinness to cool your jets. Get a Cajun kick at Evangeline on Fort Lauderdale beach; live jazz; catfish and jambalaya; sit outdoors and catch the street show.


    Thursday: Stop singing in the shower; test your talents at open mike night at Chickee Bar; laid-back crowd won't boo; outdoor pool; Jacuzzi. For gay scene, there's a smooth buns contest at The Copa; Nikki Adams hosts; big bucks ($250) to winner; tonight, 18 and older welcome.
