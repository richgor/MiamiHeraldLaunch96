Published Friday, April 26, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

FOR WEEKEND

MANNY HERNANDEZ /
For The Herald
DRESS IN BLACK: That's the favored wardrobe for revelers, some gathered by an art sculpture in a Kitchen Club patio.

  • The Kitchen Club, 3701 NE Second Ave, Miami. From 11:30 p.m. to 5:30 a.m. Fridays and Saturdays (DJ and dancing). Cover: $7; beer, $1. Call 754-0777.

    If you want to get out of the house, go to The Kitchen.

    For years, the club has been a gathering spot for South Florida youth, where they can rebel without a cause.

    The club has called Miami Beach and the Grove its home, but is now found in the heart of the Miami Design District in the former Fire and Ice space.

    Kitchen DJs churn out the latest in industrial, New Wave, retro and progressive. So you might hear some Ministry and a little nine inch nails, along with Jane's Addiction and the Ramones.

    The garb of choice is something in black -- be it T-shirts and jeans, long coats or dresses. If you lose your companions, good luck finding them. The club's interior is just as dark as the clothes patrons wear.

    The dance floor, in a section appropriately enough dubbed The Dark Room, is in the back of the club. Strobe lights there might help you find that missing friend -- or discover a new one.
    JOHNNY DIAZ
