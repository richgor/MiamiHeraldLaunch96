Published Friday, April 26, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

  • Tara's weekly guide to some of the more spirited upcoming events on the Beach is not meant to be all-inclusive. For

    • comprehensive club listings, see facing page.

    Friday: Too many clubs, too little time: Liquid (urban industrial; celeb cameos); Salvation (the Beach's newest club; tonight, New York Undercover, a straight party with guest DJs from NYC); Groove Jet (young and relentless; love the outdoor Crystal Lounge); Amnesia (disco mall; like Club Med for trendoids; tonight, Noche Latina); Circus (loud and reckless, the way you like it); Pacha (home to throngs of savvy young Latins); Les Bains (Fellini-esque, Euro-y; tonight, the college-y Danceteria); Bash (ever-popular; every club should have a tiki hut dance patio like theirs); Kremlin (with billiards room; gay); the Space (reminds us of a disco gym, in a good way); RSC (progressive and alternative); Temptations (caliente fun with live salsa acts); Bar 609 (lounging and dancing; cool music with words); Lua (like two clubs in one; clandestine, albeit loud, cocktailing and dancing); Club Follia (inside the trendy Italian restaurant); Warsaw (a South Beach institution; gay). Lounges and bars include: Bar None (where Sly Stallone hangs his artwork; enough said); Berlin Bar (aesthetically brilliant; perfect for martini time; tonight, Lupe and her Europals); Swirl (high camp, gay-friendly); Brandt's Break (hip pool hall); Society Hill (another hip pool option, with loud bands); Chili Pepper, featuring the prerequisite loud bands; Rose's (unpretentious, with varied blues, jazz and rock acts); South Beach Pub (live rock 'n' roll and funk bands); Twist (chatty cocktail banter on two floors; gay); the Tudor (the in spot for models and such; Ray Rodriguez hosts); and Ocean Drive's most civilized choice, Speakeasy at Les Deux Fontaines. At The Edge Theatre at 405 Española Way: David Mamet's Sexual Perversity in Chicago and Harvey Fierstein's On Tidy Endings (through May 5); RSVP at 531-6083.

    Saturday: Set the alarm: Rose's hosts an afternoon Mad Matinee of five bands, from 3 p.m. Tonight's pick: ``Blowout: A Hair Raiser for AIDS'' at Amnesia, from 10 p.m., with actresses Marcia Cross and Kathleen Robertson, model Tyson Beckford and vocalists Treach, Lina Santiago and Nadine Renee. Synaesthesia retro dance party in the Red Room of Virtua Cafe. Big nights for the gay-friendly dance clubs Salvation, Warsaw (Playground 18-and-older night, plus RuPaul in concert tonight; 531-4555) and Kremlin (the latter geared to the girls).

    Sunday: Gospel Brunch with Maryel Epps at Astor Place; seatings at noon and 2 p.m.; Sunday Afro Noons at Swirl by Nicky & Sherry of Afro-Maintenance (big points for wearing your 'fro); and tea dance mayhem at Amnesia. Fresh: Tea dance with Albita at Yuca on the Beach, from 6 p.m. The best nocturnal choices include Top Secret espionage music night at 821, hosted by Andrew Price Summers; the Gothic-themed Church at Groove Jet (dress code tip: black lipstick and a cape always a good idea); laid-back acid jazz at Hercules at Lua; and boyworld fun at X-Tra Large, at Liquid (Marvella presents Girlesque at 2 a.m.). Retro nights at Cameo (disco) and Bash (assorted, with emphasis on the '80s). Tonight at Rose's: Nil Lara.

    Monday: New: Back door Bamby at Lua, with DJ Gigi. Cozy Mondays at Berlin Bar, hosted by Brigitte Andrade (tonight: diva Maryel Epps). Drag at its dizzying best: Check out Kitty Meow, Steven Gomez and Santiago ``Go Go Snack'' Pazo's supper club hit at Caffe Torino. More drag: Bridgette Buttercup and Taffy Lynn host at Swirl. Foodies should book at Ernie, Larry and Shakira's bi-weekly Chef's Table night with Chef Johnny Vinczencz at Astor Place (coming April 29: your own Queen o' the Night dons apron and chef chapeau for a '60s Flower Power-themed meal; RSVP at 534-CHEF). Fat Black Pussycat at Liquid is too important to miss, even if you have a day job. At Society Hill, open mike night (``just like in your parents' garage,'' our invite reads). More open mike at Rose's.

    Tuesday: New: Jazz by candlelight at hip eatery Lure on Lincoln Road, and Gay Stand-Up night at the Comedy Zone. Also new: Betty Ford Bingo at Swirl, hosted by Marvella. Music smorgasbord: Funk and hip-hop at Phat-Phree, at Bar None; classic R&B at Peppermint Lounge, at Lua; '80s disco at Follia, for Tommy Pooch's night; bluegrass R&B at the Strand, with Fleet Starbuck Blues Band; European underground at Bash; and '90s melodies for moderns by Monsy and Q-baa, at Chili Pepper.

    Wednesday: New 1) Blondes Doooo Shakespeare at the Comedy Zone, produced by Sherry Faith's Method Actor's Group, featuring a cameo by Adora; 2) For a Better World, a hip-hop and reggae night, at Society Hill; 3) Secret, a dinner-show event for women only, at Caffe Torino, from 8:30 p.m.; 4) Foundation at Groove Jet, serving up grassroots jazz, funk and hip-hop. Chanteuse Lola performs at Vecchia Brera (in the Betsy Ross Hotel), from 8:30 p.m. Just when you thought it was safe to be single, Caffe Torino unleashes Shelley Novak's South Beach Singled Out; showtime: 10 p.m. At Virtua Cafe, Voodoo Lounge, with surf, twist and rare grooves by DJ Shannon and Ken; plus: hand-rolled cigars and martinis! Scratch 'n' Dent night at Swirl, where Bridgette Buttercup is let loose at the turntable. Worldbeat night at Lua. Seventies disco night at the Space.

    Thursday: Claudia Heimes hosts Brazilian Nights at Lua. JaMiyla and friends celebrate Ulypian Thursdays at the Marlin Bar, from 6 p.m. The notorious foam night has returned to Amnesia; dress down. Soul 'n' more at Mark Leventhal's Funkadelica, at Bar None.
