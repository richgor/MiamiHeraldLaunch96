Published Saturday, May 4, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Despite critical raves, Fox pulls the plug on Profit

By RICHARD HUFF
New York Daily News

Critical raves weren't enough to save Fox's new dark drama, Profit.

The network has yanked the show for the remainder of the May sweeps -- a quarterly ratings period used by local stations to set future advertising rates -- and the series' four unaired episodes probably will have to wait for summer to be burned off for good.

Fox also announced that starting June 3, a new drama, L.A. Firefighters, would air in Profit's 9 p.m. Monday berth. Until then, the network has scheduled a movie and a couple of specials to air in the time period through the sweeps.

``The show was not performing well in the ratings,'' said a Fox spokesman.

That's an understatement. Last week, Profit, which stars Adrian Pasdar as a ruthless businessman -- a venal New Age J.R. Ewing, so to speak -- was the lowest-rated program for the four major networks. The series has been in the Nielsen cellar since its debut April 8, despite all the praise it has received.

L.A. Firefighters is an ensemble drama starring Christine Elise (ER ).

Also on June 3, Fox will debut The Last Frontier, a sitcom about three single guys and their beautiful new roommate. It will air at 8:30 p.m.

As a result, Melrose Place, now airing from 8 p.m. to 9 p.m. on Mondays, will come off temporarily. (MP's a keeper.)
