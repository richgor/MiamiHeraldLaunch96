FAMILY TV PROGRAMMING

Here's a week's worth of the best TV programming for children and families. Each capsule includes a recommendation on appropriate audiences -- preschool, grade-school or the whole family.


FRIDAY
Gullah Gullah Island Natalie and Shaina have a tea party with Shaina's imaginary friend, Miss Ella Mae Breadsticks. Elsewhere, James and Vanessa meet driftwood artist Everett Mayo on the beach, and he shows them how to make their own works of art. Songs include Have You Any Tea?, Loop De Loo, The ImagiNation and Aiken Drum. Preschool; Grade-school. NIK at 1:30 p.m.


SATURDAY
Wide World of Sports for Kids On the day of the Kentucky Derby (airing later this afternoon on ABC), ``A Day at the Races,'' with host Maria Sansone, goes behind the scenes of thoroughbred racing, from the predawn world of the backstretch to the high-energy excitement of post time. Grade-school; Family. ABC, channels 10 and 25 at 12:30 p.m.

Movie ``Bon Voyage, Charlie Brown (and Don't Come Back!)'' In this 1980 animated adventure, originally made for television, the Peanuts gang travels on an exchange program to France, where Charlie Brown, Linus, Peppermint Patty, Lucy and Snoopy find adventure, romance, history and some valuable lessons. Preschool; Grade-school; Family. TBS at 2:05 p.m.

Space Cases In ``Prisoner of Luff,'' an escaped convict switches places with Catalina (Jewel Staite) in order to hijack the Christa, leaving Catalina trapped on a prison world to face a possible death sentence. Grade-school. NIK at 8:30 p.m.


SUNDAY
The Mystery Files of Shelby Woo In ``To Catch a Fish,'' Shelby (Irene Ng) and her pals (Adam Busch, Preslaysa Edwards) search for a dolphin that's missing from the Cocoa Beach marine lab, a case that's been shoved aside by the activities of a local smuggling ring. Shelby takes on the neglected crime and winds up solving both mysteries. Grade-school. NIK at 6 p.m.

Nature ``The Call of the Kakadu'' travels to Kakadu National Park in northern Australia, a harsh wilderness that spends six months being pummeled by violent thunderstorms and monsoon rains, then six more enduring scorching drought and fires. The setting for the movie Crocodile Dundee, the land of evergreen forests and lily-framed billabongs is home to a variety of exotic wildlife, including wallabies, cockatoos, giant saltwater crocodiles, dingoes, snakes and dragonlike lizards. Grade-school; Family. PBS, Channel 2 at 8 p.m.


MONDAY
Timmy the Tooth The puppet series returns with 10 new episodes that run through May 17. Imaginative Timmy and his best friend, Brush Brush, seek adventures around the globe. Preschool; Grade-school. NIK at 1 p.m.

Spellbinder Wearing a power suit, Paul (Zbych Trofimiuk) is able to open a doorway to his own universe -- unaware that Ashka (Heather Mitchell) is lurking nearby, eager to follow him. Riana (Gosia Piotrowka) distracts Ashka so Paul can slip through, but when Paul sees Ashka menacing his friend, he reaches through and pulls Riana into his world. Grade-school; Family. DIS at 7:30 p.m.


TUESDAY
The Adventures of Mole The first of two new one-hour animated specials based on Kenneth Grahame's The Wind in the Willows. In the springtime, Mole goes in search of adventure. At a river, he meets Rat, who introduces him to the famous Mr. Toad; the three of them then take a hair-raising caravan trip. Later on, Mole goes into the Wild Wood hoping to meet Badger, but instead finds trouble. Preschool; Grade-school; Family. DIS at 7 p.m.

The Prince and the Pauper In Disney's 1990, half-hour version of the Mark Twain classic, Mickey (voice by Wayne Allwine) takes the title roles as two look-alikes: a pampered prince who longs to explore the outside world; and a peasant who dreams of making ends meet. When the two trade places, hilarity ensues. Roy Dotrice (Beauty and the Beast ) narrates. Preschool; Grade-school; Family. DIS at 8 p.m.


WEDNESDAY
Reading Rainbow In Tooth-Gnasher Superflash by Daniel Pinkwater, read by Victoria Jackson, the Popsnorkles' quest for a new car leads them to an amazing test drive. Inspired, LeVar Burton decides to learn all about automobiles during a day at a service station. He also offers a glance at cars of the future, including the solar-powered Sunraycar. Preschool; Grade-school. PBS, Channel 2 at 11:30 a.m.

Faerie Tale Theatre Novelist (The Seven Per Cent Solution ) and director (Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan ) Nicholas Meyer wrote and directed this version of The Pied Piper of Hamelin. Eric Idle stars as a piper who is hired to rid a town of its rats, but when the town leaders withhold his payment, he decides to lure their children away instead. It's one of the best-known fairy tales, but probably not for very little children. Grade-school; Family. DIS at 7 p.m.


THURSDAY
Gullah Gullah Island In ``Yes I Can,'' Shaina agonizes over not being able to tie her shoelaces, while James is embarrassed by his inability to whistle. The kids eventually find out that the only way to learn something is to believe in yourself and to practice. Soon, Shaina is doing tie-dye, and James is leading a whistling parade. Preschool; Grade-school. NIK at 1:30 p.m.

Biography Although Christopher Columbus gets all the credit, many historians believe that it was Norse explorer Leif Ericson who was the first European to set foot in the New World. According to legend, Ericson was on his way from Norway to Greenland around 1000 A.D. when a storm drove his ship west, where he landed in a place many now believe was Newfoundland or New England. Grade-school; Family. A&E at 8 p.m.
