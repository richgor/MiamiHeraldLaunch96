Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

May Lonesome Dove stories rest in peace

  • Dead Man's Walk, part 1, 9 p.m. Sunday; part 2, 8 p.m. Monday; channels 10 and 25

    Dead Man's Walk is the fourth miniseries made from Larry McMurtry's Lonesome Dove stories, and, with any luck, the last.

    The originals, 1989's Lonesome Dove and Return to Lonesome Dove, deftly explored the characters who populated the Western expansion.

    But last fall's Streets of Laredo was a shameless attempt to cash in on its popularity, as is this latest installment.

    Written by McMurtry and Diana Ossana, it's about the early years of Lonesome Dove heroes Gus McCrae and Woodrow Call, the crusty Texas Rangers first played by Robert Duvall and Tommy Lee Jones.

    The story begins as the painfully green Call (Jonny Lee Miller) and McCrae (David Arquette) join an expedition led by erstwhile pirate Caleb Cobb (F. Murray Abraham) to capture Santa Fe. They encounter gruesome injury, starvation, fire and the vengeance of a Comanche warrior and the Mexican Army.


    The whole crew moves from one misadventure to the next with no apparent reason. It's hard to tell the heroes apart (luckily, one has a mustache). And Jennifer Garner as young Clara is an insult to Anjelica Huston, who originated the role.

    Dead Man's Walk has one great character -- who's not unveiled till the last 10 minutes. By then, most viewers will have deserted this desiccated piece of beef jerky.
    --ROBIN DOUGHERTY
