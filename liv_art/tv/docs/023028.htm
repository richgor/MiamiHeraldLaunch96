Published Monday, April 29, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Nick at Nite expands to new channel


  • TV Land sneak preview, 8 p.m. to 5:30 a.m. Tuesday, Nickelodeon cable channel
    By FRAZIER MOORE
    Associated Press

    NEW YORK -- Load up your VCR tonight, hit ``record'' and let 'er rip. Some keepers are coming down the wire.

    We're talking the Beatles on Ed Sullivan. The very first Gunsmoke (from 1955). The teleplay that led to the Twilight Zone series.

    You'll get the premiere episodes of two of television's finest -- Hill Street Blues and St. Elsewhere -- and a segment of one of TV's all-time, undisputed worst: My Mother, the Car. And more.

    You're about to visit TV Land.

    Cable's Nick at Nite, which long ago dared to join the two words ``classic'' and ``television,'' is branching out with more of this stuff. A new companion channel, Nick at Nite's TV Land, will celebrate four decades of TV programming and the pop culture that went with it. Tonight, TV Land signs on.

    Whether it's included on your menu of basic services is in the hands of your local cable provider, and, sadly, you may not be among the lucky 4 million cable homes getting TV Land from the get-go.

    But if not, despair not. For 9 1/2 hours, at least, TV Land will be available for sampling during the window normally occupied by Nick at Nite on the widely available Nickelodeon channel. That's from TV Land's official sign-on at 8 p.m., all the way through to 5:30 a.m. Tuesday.

    What's the difference between TV Land and Nick at Nite, the reruns-crazy service that since 1985 has pulled Nickelodeon's late shift?

    First of all, TV Land is around-the-clock classic TV. And while Nick at Nite is devoted exclusively to sitcoms, TV Land will harbor all genres, beginning with comedies, dramas, westerns and variety.

    ``TV Land is not just about a series or two,'' says Rich Cronin, president of the two networks. ``It's everything TV! Obsessed with TV! It's about the language of television. The music. The graphics. Test patterns. Even classic commercials.''

    Yes, that's right: Until October 1997, TV Land will air ``retromercials'' in place of national advertising. Look out for Speedy Alka-Seltzer, the fast-talking Federal Express guy, Life cereal's Mikey and hundreds more commercials of old.

    Here is the lineup for tonight:

    8 p.m. Love, American Style, featuring a half-hour segment that served as the pilot episode for the sitcom Happy Days.

    8:30 p.m. The Addams Family (premiere episode).

    9 p.m. That Girl (pilot).

    9:30 p.m. Hogan's Heroes (pilot).

    10 p.m. The Ed Sullivan Show from 1964, including the Beatles' first appearance on American television.

    10:30 p.m. The Sonny & Cher Comedy Hour with guests Ronald Reagan and the Jackson 5.

    11 p.m. Hill Street Blues (premiere).

    Midnight Time Element, the precursor to The Twilight Zone series.

    1 a.m. St. Elsewhere (premiere).

    2 a.m. My Mother, the Car (pilot).

    2:30 a.m. Petticoat Junction

    3 a.m. Green Acres (premiere).

    3:30 a.m. Gunsmoke (pilot).

    4 a.m. Mannix (pilot).

    5 a.m. Honey West, from the 1965-66 series about a sexy sleuth played by Anne Francis.

    5:30 a.m. Signoff.
