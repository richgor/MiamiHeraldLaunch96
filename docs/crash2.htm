Posted Saturday, May 11, 1996

2nd sea lion caught snacking by locks is Sea World-bound

Associated Press

SEATTLE -- A second sea lion suspected of overindulging in steelhead at the Ballard Locks has been caught and shipped to Tacoma's Point Defiance Zoo and Aquarium, bound eventually for a new home at Sea World of Florida.

The huge, bewhiskered critter, identified as No. 45, is one of five targeted for capture. It was caught Thursday night. Another -- No. 225 -- was caught Monday, according to a news release issued jointly by state, federal and zoo officials.

It is not clear when the animals will be shipped to Orlando. It will save time and money for Sea World to take as many sea lions as possible at one time.

Earlier this year, after a change in the Marine Mammal Protection Act, the state got federal permission to kill the five most voracious sea lions among those that gobble steelhead migrating through the locks.

The plan was approved after years of unsuccessful attempts to deter the animals with firecrackers, rubber bullets and air horns.

But it drew an outcry and a lawsuit from environmental groups, who blame the man-made locks for the problem.

In April, Sea World volunteered to provide a home for the all-too-efficient anglers -- Nos. 41, 45, 87, 225 and 17.

The Humane Society of the United States, based in Washington, D.C., says neither Washington state nor the federal agency has the authority to send the animals to Sea World. A society official suggested the state simply hold the animals at Point Defiance until the steelhead spawning run ends later this month.
