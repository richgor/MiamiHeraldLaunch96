IN THE AMERICAS

From Herald Wire Services

MEXICO
8 defendants charged
in serial killings

CIUDAD JUAREZ -- Eight alleged gang members arrested for investigation in the suspected serial killings of 17 young women have been charged with murder and conspiracy in seven of the slayings.

Police found nine victims last year in the desert on the outskirts of this city along the U.S. border, and eight more were found in the past six weeks. Some were raped and strangled, and at least one was stabbed.

Arturo Chavez, attorney general for the state of Chihuahua, said detectives found evidence linking at least seven killings to the defendants, who are believed to be members of a gang called The Rebels.

CUBA
Anti-cholera vaccine
used in human testing

HAVANA -- An anti-cholera vaccine developed through genetic engineering is being tested on humans, Gustavo Sierra, head of Cuba's Program of Vaccine and Research and Development, said Thursday.

Sierra said the initial tests have shown promising results and researchers hope to achieve a vaccine that can be effective with a single dose.

He said the Cuban vaccine is prepared with attenuated live bacteria. Cuban officials had announced last year that they hoped to have a cholera vaccine on the market by the end of 1995 but Sierra did not explain the apparent delay.

ETC.

  • DOMINICAN REPUBLIC -- Former U.S. President Carter ended a two-day visit Thursday, saying he had received assurances from President Joaquin Balaguer that the May 16 presidential election will be a model of fairness, although he felt the number of election observers was too small. Carter said the three main candidates had expressed confidence in the election procedures.
  • HONDURAS -- Foreign Minister Delmer Urbizo Panting said Thursday that a meeting of the commission studying Honduras-El Salvador border problems ended without achieving progress on the issues.
