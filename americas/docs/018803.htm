IN THE AMERICAS

From Herald Wire Services

COLOMBIA

In new blow to Samper, finance minister resigns

BOGOTA -- In the latest blow to his scandal-plagued administration, President Ernesto Samper accepted the resignation of his finance minister Monday.

Guillermo Perry submitted his resignation over the weekend, saying his departure would enable him to better defend the president against charges he took money from the Cali drug cartel to finance his 1994 campaign.

Samper named former Agriculture Minister Jose Antonio Ocampo, head of the government's economic planning office, to replace Perry.

BRAZIL

Mudslides kill 32 in Recife shantytowns

SAO PAULO -- Torrential rain dislodged tons of mud Monday outside Recife, killing at least 32 people and burying makeshift hillside huts. Hundreds of rescuers searched for survivors.

Most of deaths were in the hillside shantytowns on the outskirts of the city, according to the Recife Fire Department.

In middle-class districts, homes were heavily damaged by falling trees.

  • CUBA -- Col. Daniel Alarcon Ramirez, a former comrade-in-arms of Ernesto ``Che'' Guevara and ex-bodyguard to President Fidel Castro, has asked for political asylum in France, exile sources in Paris say.
  • GUYANA -- Strong performances in the rice and sugar markets along with the resumption of gold mining has pushed the economy to a better-than-expected 6.6 growth for the first quarter of 1996.
