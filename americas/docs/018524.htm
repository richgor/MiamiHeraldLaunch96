Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN THE AMERICAS

From Herald Staff and Wire Reports

GUATEMALA

U.S.: Nun's kidnapping story considered `hoax'

U.S. Embassy officials in Guatemala considered an American nun's account of being kidnapped, raped and tortured by military personnel in 1989 as a ``hoax,'' but investigated anyway for fear of bad publicity, according to State Department documents released Monday in Washington.

Despite a lack of firm evidence, the U.S. government has ``no reason not to believe'' Sister Dianna Ortiz, State Department spokesman Nicholas Burns said.

The documents were among 20,000 released by the State Department on the suspected murders and tortures of 18 Americans dating to 1984.

Government, rebels sign pact aimed at poor

Rebel and government negotiators seeking to end a three-decade civil war signed a key agreement in Mexico City guaranteeing more financial support and political power for Guatemala's rural poor.

It was the first major accord between the two sides in the conflict since March 1995.

CUBA

Aviation agency delays report on shootdown

The International Civil Aviation Organization announced in Montreal that it has postponed until early June its report on the downing Feb. 24 of two U.S.-registered civilian planes by Cuban warplanes over the Florida Straits. Four pilots of the volunteer group Brothers to the Rescue died in that incident.

A spokesman for the aviation organization told The Herald that the report needs to be submitted to the United Nations Security Council before it can be released to the public.
