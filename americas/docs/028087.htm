Published Tuesday, April 23, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN THE AMERICAS



From Herald Wire Services


GUATEMALA
Nun protesting abuses
to eat only bread, water
A nun who says she was raped and tortured in Guatemala said Monday she will eat only bread and water until the government releases information it has on her case and other human-rights violations in the Central American nation.

For 22 days, Sister Dianna Ortiz, an Ursuline nun from Grants, N.M., has conducted a silent vigil in a park across the street from the White House in an effort to gain access to information the government has on her 6-year-old case.



HONDURAS
2 ex-Haitian officials
received as refugees
SAN PEDRO SULA -- Two former Haitian officials accused of widespread human rights violations arrived in Honduras from the Dominican Republic as political refugees Monday.

Former Port-au-Prince Mayor Franck Romain and Lt. Gen. Joseph Michel Francois, former head of the national police, arrived in this city 125 miles north of the capital, Tegucigalpa, in a private twin-engine plane.



ETC.

  • JAMAICA -- A performance review of Cuban-trained doctors in Jamaica has led the Medical Council here to repeal a 20-year-old rule allowing doctors educated in Cuba to practice without taking Jamaican bar exams.
  • VENEZUELA -- The bolivar plunged nearly 42 percent Monday after the government lifted foreign exchange controls. Prices immediately shot up for foods, goods and services. In Washington D.C., world financial leaders approved a package of proposals designed to better handle the next Mexican-style financial crisis as as they assembled a $3 billion rescue package to support Venezuela.
  • MEXICO -- Top anti-drug cops from across the hemisphere are meeting in Mexico City, beginning today, to talk shop. The changing leadership in the Latin American drug trade is likely to be a hot topic.
