IN THE AMERICAS

From Herald Wire Services PUERTO RICO FBI arrests politician after federal indictment

SAN JUAN -- The FBI said it arrested a local ruling party senator Wednesday after a federal grand jury handed down an indictment against him.

The charges against Sen. Nicolas Nogueras of the New Progressive Party were not immediately available.

`We just arrested him,'' FBI spokeswoman Sara Lema said after local television showed agents ushering Nogueras from his house in the Condado area of San Juan. The island Senate's Ethics Committee recommended in a report Friday that Nogueras be expelled for allegedly violating the local tax code, government ethics law and a law that limits a legislator's outside income.

MEXICO
U.S. plans to provide
copters for drug war
The United States plans to send several dozen Huey transport helicopters to Mexico in a few months as part of an effort to help the Mexican military combat the illegal drug trade.

Jan Lodal, principal deputy U.S. undersecretary of defense for policy, said in Washington Wednesday that agreement has been reached on the method of supplying the helicopters, which are Army surplus. The exact numbers and timing are yet to be worked out, he said.

The eventual total will be in the range of a ``few dozen,'' he said. They will be given free of charge.

ETC.

  • CUBA -- Granma, the official Communist Party newspaper, reported Wednesday that the current Cuban sugar harvest has produced 4 million tons of sugar, surpassing the 3.3 million produced last year. The goal is to achieve 4.5 million tons before the harvest ends next month.
  • ARGENTINA -- Chilean President Eduardo Frei arrived Wednesday on his first official visit to Argentina. He was welcomed by President Carlos Menem in the northern city of La Rioja, Menem's home town.
