IN THE AMERICAS

IN THE AMERICAS
PERU
More than 30 killed
as bus plunges off cliff

LIMA -- A crowded passenger bus plunged off a cliff in southern Peru, leaving 31 people dead and 20 injured, according to news reports Sunday.

The bus reportedly left the city of Tacna, near the Chilean border, at about 4 p.m. Saturday, carrying merchants northeast toward the Andes Mountains and the city of Ilave. The bus crashed about two hours later. The cause was not immediately apparent.

PUERTO RICO
Plane lands safely after
possible bomb threat

SAN JUAN -- A pilot who thought he heard a bomb threat over his earphones landed his commercial plane safely in San Juan Sunday, without injury to the four crew members and 31 passengers.

Explosive-sniffing dogs and a specially trained Puerto Rico police bomb squad combed the plane from nose to tail but found nothing, said Juan Rivera Cancel, the commander of the airport canine corps.

``The pilot picked up a a radio transmission over a secure frequency and thought it to be a bomb threat,'' said Kathleen Bergen, a spokeswoman for the FAA in Atlanta.
The plane was on a commercial flight from the Dominican Republic.
