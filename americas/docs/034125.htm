Published Thursday, May 2, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN THE AMERICAS

From Herald Wire Services

HAITI

Hundreds protest plans to privatize businesses

PORT-AU-PRINCE -- Several hundred people protested Wednesday against President Rene Preval's plan to privatize state-owned businesses

``Haiti is not for sale, neither wholesale nor retail,'' declared a placard on 1 of 30 vehicles that took part in the May Day march.

Seventeen grass-roots organizations had called on thousands of workers to join their demonstration. But only about 600 people marched from Notre Dame Cathedral to the National Palace, chanting slogans against privatization, the government and the World Bank.

DOMINICAN REPUBLIC

Government takes over third-largest bank

SANTO DOMINGO -- The government has taken over the country's third-largest commercial bank to avoid depositors withdrawing their money as a planned sale of the bank goes through.

The Central Bank announced Tuesday it had acquired 65 percent of the shares of Banco del Comercio, under an agreement signed with its holding company, Grupo Bancomercio.

Grupo Bancomercio is in the final stage of negotiating a sale of the majority of the bank's shares to Spain's Banco Central Hispanico. But talks had broken off in recent days, worrying financial authorities.

CUBA

European Union takes first step on embargo

The European Union will ask the World Trade Organization to intervene after the United States passed the Helms-Burton law calling for punishment of foreign companies that do business with Cuba.

The EU announced Tuesday it would seek WTO consultations, the first step in solving disputes in the body, but that the move was intended to encourage Washington to reconsider the law.

The 15-nation EU said it would not press the matter if the United States responded positively.

ETC.

  • EL SALVADOR -- President Clinton announced Wednesday the nomination of Anne W. Patterson of Arkansas, a career foreign service officer, as the next U.S. ambassador to El Salvador. Patterson is currently principal deputy assistant secretary of state for inter-American affairs.
