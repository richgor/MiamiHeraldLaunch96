Published Tuesday, April 23, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

European Union ponders retaliation against States

Group protests U.S. sanctions on Cuba

LUXEMBOURG -- (AP) -- The European Union is considering retaliation against the United States to protest new U.S. sanctions against Cuba that could harm EU companies doing business with Havana.

The 15 EU foreign ministers meeting here said they were studying options to counter the so-called Helms-Burton Act, ``including possible retaliatory measures.'' They did not elaborate.

Such a move could provoke a new trans-Atlantic trade conflict once the sanctions are put into effect. The EU ministers also said they may bring the dispute before the Geneva-based World Trade Organization.

They said the act ``goes against international law and harms the rights and interests of the European Union in the trade and investment sectors.''

The Helms-Burton Act allows Americans to sue foreigners who profit from properties confiscated by the Castro government. The law came in the wake of the downing of two Cuban-American civilian planes by Cuban fighters Feb. 24. It is aimed at discouraging international investment.

Many U.S. trading partners have protested the act, saying the United States has no right to impose penalties on other countries' businesses for trading with Cuba.
