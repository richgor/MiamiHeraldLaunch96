Published Saturday, April 27, 1996, in the Miami Herald.


Associated Press
SEES `OPPORTUNITY OF THE CENTURY': William Perry.


Defense chief hails `new era' of cooperation in hemisphere

By DON BOHNING
Herald Staff Writer

Hailing our hemisphere as ``the least militarized region of the world,'' U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry told a security symposium in Miami on Friday that his predecessors had looked south ``and saw security problems. I look south and see security partners.''

There is, Perry said, ``a new era of peaceful and democratic cooperation among and between all nations in our hemisphere'' where all countries ``save one [Cuba] have chosen democracy. . . . This is presenting the opportunity of the century . . . an opportunity to secure a future of peace, stability and security for all our nations.''

Perry's remarks came at a closing luncheon of the third Latin America Strategy Symposium co-sponsored by the Panama-based U.S. Military's Southern Command and the National Defense University in Washington.

It brought together more than 100 ranking security officials and specialists from the United States, Latin America and the Caribbean for a three-day discussion on the theme ``Adapting to a New Security Environment.''

Much of the discussion focused on new roles for security forces and the threats facing them, including drug trafficking, transnational criminal gangs, peacekeeping efforts and the consolidation of democracy.

As an example of the new spirit of cooperation among the hemisphere's security forces, Perry cited this week's ``productive'' visit to Washington by Gen. Enrique Cervantes, Mexico's defense minister.

The visit was in return for one Perry made to Mexico in November.

``In just six months since that visit, the United States and Mexico have launched a new, mutually beneficial relationship between our two militaries. We are working together to solve common challenges such as disaster relief and narco-trafficking.''

As another example, Perry cited Paraguay, where earlier this week ``the democratically elected president faced the threat of a military coup. Neighboring states, the United States and the Organization of American States swung into action to support the president. Democracy prevailed in Paraguay.

``This shows how sovereign nations of the hemisphere are working together today, partners in peace, prosperity and democracy,'' Perry said.
