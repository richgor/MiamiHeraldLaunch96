Published Friday, May 3, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Attorney general of Colombia is ordered arrested

BOGOTA, Colombia -- (AP) -- Orlando Vasquez, the government's chief investigator of public officials, was ordered arrested Thursday on charges that he took money from the Cali drug cartel.

Vasquez, the attorney general, is the latest high-ranking target in Colombia's unfolding drug corruption scandal, which threatens to unseat President Ernesto Samper.

Vasquez's arrest was ordered by Prosecutor General Alfonso Valdivieso, who has been leading an investigation of drug cartel influence in government. A spokesman for Valdivieso said Vasquez's whereabouts were not known.

The arrest order comes as another major blow to Samper, who is defending himself before a congressional panel against allegations that he won election in 1994 with millions of dollars in contributions from the Cali Cartel, the world's largest drug trafficking organization.

Samper has denied any knowledge of the drug money donations. His lawyer has said that campaign aides apparently solicited cartel money and then stole it.

The 53-year-old Vasquez, who has been in charge of investigating administrative wrongdoing since August 1994, has also served as interior minister and ambassador to Chile.

Prosecutors allege that a cartel front company gave tens of thousands of dollars to Vasquez's failed Senate re-election campaign in 1994. They also say the cartel paid the bill when Vasquez stayed at Cali's Intercontinental Hotel twice in 1993. He faces charges of illegal enrichment.

The United States suspended Vasquez's visa last month because of allegations of ties to drug traffickers.

Also Thursday, Rep. Francisco Canossa resigned from the panel looking into drug corruption in Samper's campaign, denouncing the investigation as a sham.
