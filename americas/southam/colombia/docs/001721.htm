Published Monday, May 6, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Rebels kill 16 in Colombia

BOGOTA, Colombia -- (AP) -- Leftist rebels killed at least 16 people Sunday in one of the country's most violent regions.

RCN radio reported police as saying the victims, including at least two children, were killed in two separate attacks.

The massacre took place in Turbo, a fishing town on the Caribbean coast. The town is located in Uraba, a banana-growing area near the northern border with Panama, where several mass killings have occurred in the past year.

Guerrillas, paramilitary groups and the army are vying for control in Uraba. Authorities on Sunday blamed the latest massacre on the leftist Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia.
