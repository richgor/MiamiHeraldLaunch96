IN THE NEWS

Deputy minister quits over tax

OTTAWA -- Canadian Deputy Prime Minister Sheila Copps resigned from parliament Wednesday in a controversy over the government's change in the national value-added tax.

Her resignation marks the third loss in the ruling Liberal Party ranks in the past 10 days because of proposed changes to the national 7 percent goods and services tax.

Two Toronto-area members of parliament left the Liberal Party last week, accusing Chretien of not living up to his 1993 electoral pledge to get rid of the GST. Copps promised on the campaign trail to resign if the Liberal government didn't ``scrap and abolish'' the GST.

In a news conference last week, Copps said the remark was ``shooting from the lip'' under the stress of the campaign.

Chretien promises vote on gay rights

OTTAWA -- Prime Minister Jean Chretien has confirmed there will be a free vote on gay-rights legislation that some Liberal MPs are determined to vote against.

The Liberals are planning to amend the Canadian Human Rights Act to prevent discrimination on the basis of sexual orientation.

Justice Minister Allan Rock introduced the amendment Monday. It would add sexual orientation to the list of prohibited grounds for discrimination along with race, religion, age, sex, marital status and disability.

Reform MP Bob Ringma has drawn criticism for his remarks that it should be OK to fire blacks or homosexuals if they're hurting a business. He later apologized for the statements.

Montrealers line up to win Winnipeg trip

WINNIPEG -- Several hundred Montrealers have responded to a promotion offering a free weekend in Winnipeg.

The city of Winnipeg ran an advertisement in The Montreal Gazette touting Winnipeg's attributes and offering two Montrealers a chance to win a weekend to the city some call Winter-peg.

The campaign was prompted by an earlier promotion in the Gazette that belittled the Manitoba capital.

The Gazette ads told Montrealers if they didn't feel good about Montreal after reading the newspaper's series on that city, they should spend a week in Winnipeg.

IN BUSINESS

Wanted: New leader for wheat board

EDMONTON -- The embattled Canadian Wheat Board is looking for a new commissioner after its freshest face resigned over the agency's contentious marketing monopoly.

Ken Beswick stepped down Tuesday after locking horns with other commissioners over the monopoly.

It's been the cause of a rebellion by a growing number of farmers who want the freedom to market their grain on their own in an effort to get better prices.

Stocks rise amid lifted bank issues

TORONTO -- Canadian stocks rose as expectations that interest rates will decline lifted bank issues and an asset sale helped Newbridge Networks Corp.'s stock, analysts said.

Lower oil and gas shares tempered the index's gains, as crude oil and gas oil prices dropped for a second day on Washington's decision to sell U.S. $227 million of oil reserves to curb rising gas prices.

Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce, Royal Bank of Canada and Bank of Montreal led the Toronto Stock Exchange 300 Composite Index up 4.63 points to 5151.10.

The U.S. dollar closed Wednesday at C$1.3610. The Canadian dollar closed Wednesday at U.S. 73.48 cents.
