Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

THE TOP TEN

Here are the nation's 10 biggest black-owned industrial and service companies, as compiled by Black Enterprise magazine. To qualify, a company must have been fully operational in 1995 and at least 51 percent black-owned. It must manufacture or own the product it sells or provide industrial or consumer services. This list includes the company, headquarters city, line of business and 1995 revenues.

1. TLC Beatrice International Holdings Inc., New York, food processing and distribution, $2.1 billion.

2. Johnson Publishing Co. Inc., Chicago, publishing, broadcasting, television production, cosmetics, hair care, $316.2 million.

3. Philadelphia Coca-Cola Bottling Co., Philadelphia, soft drink bottling, $315 million.

4. H.J. Russell & Co., Atlanta, construction, property management, airport concessions, real estate development, $172.8 million.

5. Pulsar Data Systems Inc., Lanham, Md., systems integration, office automation, computer reseller, $165.1 million.

6. Uniworld Group Inc., New York, advertising, public relations, event marketing, television programming, $133.7 million.

7. Burrell Communications Group, Chicago, advertising, public relations, consumer promotions, entertainment, $127.9 million.

8. Anderson-Dubose Co., Solon, Ohio, food distributor, $119.50 million.

9. Granite Broadcasting Corp., New York, network television affiliates, $119.47 million.

10. BET Holdings, Washington, D.C., cable television network, magazine publishing, $115 million.
