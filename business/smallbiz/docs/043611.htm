Published Friday, April 26, 1996, in the Miami Herald.


Cellular phone firm connects

Let's Talk wins Chamber's entrepreneurial award

By ALINA MATAS
Herald Business Writer

Let's Talk Cellular, a Miami company that sells cellular phones, pagers and related paraphernalia in mall kiosks, Thursday won the 1996 Cutting Edge Award from the Greater Miami Chamber of Commerce.

The company won from among 13 finalists, deemed by judges as stand-outs because of their innovation, niche marketing, growth, employee empowerment and customer service. The firms were recognized at a luncheon at the Biltmore Hotel Country Club in Coral Gables.

The winner impressed the judges ``with the overall niche strategy they were creating,'' said Larry Harris, chairman of last year's winner, Pollo Tropical, and one of this year's judges. ``It's a very competitive market they're in, but they've focused on the mall niche, which they've done very well, and they've improved from the year before.''

Let's Talk Cellular was a finalist last year, when the chamber introduced the award to recognize and encourage entrepreneurial excellence.

Founded by two college friends in the back of a van in 1989, Let's Talk Cellular now runs 27 mall kiosks that sign up cellular phone users and sell phones, pagers and other wireless devices. Its revenues increased from $1.5 million in 1993 to about $8.3 million in 1995.

``Last year was great just to be honored,'' said Nick Molina, the company's 28-year-old president and chief executive. ``And this year it shows that we improved. We're proud.''

``It makes you want to live up to the standards that got you here,'' said Chairman Brett Beveridge, 31.

The chamber also recognized two runners-up: The Vincam Group, based in Coral Gables, and the PMG Pharmed Group of Miami.

Vincam provides human resources management services, such as training, employee manuals and administrative help, to employers in Florida, Georgia and Michigan. Its revenues have increased from $138.1 million in 1993 to about $239 million in 1995.

PMG Pharmed distributes medical and pharmaceutical products in Florida and Central America. Its 1995 revenues were about $44 million, compared with $31 million in 1993.
