Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Sushi craze helps lift Benihana profits 60%

Benihana keeps cashing in on the sushi craze. The Miami company that has added sushi bars at 20 of the 46 Japanese restaurants it owns or licenses, recorded a 60 percent increase in profits for the year ended March 31.

Benihana's annual revenues also increased 11 percent. Same store sales for the year -- and the guest count -- were up about 8 percent.

Benihana President Joel Schwartz attributed the profitable year to the popularity of the sushi bars, seven of which were added last year, and the upgrading of existing restaurants, which cost $3 million.

Last year, the company also introduced its first Benihana Grill in Sacramento, Calif., ``an intimate version of our traditional restaurant allowing penetration of markets that were not feasible for free-standing Benihana restaurants,'' Schwartz said in a statement.

Two more Benihana Grills are planned for the current year.

Catalina Lighting

Despite a dip in sales, earnings increased 67 percent at Catalina Lighting, a Miami-based manufacturer and distributor of lighting products. Lower inventory levels, leading to lower interest expenses, led to the higher gross profits, the company said.

ADT Ltd.

The adoption of an accounting rule pertaining to long-lived assets led to a $379.1 million first-quarter loss at ADT Ltd. of Boca Raton.

The writedown of certain assets to their estimated fair values resulted in a $410.1 million non-cash charge against earnings during the three months ended March 31. Had it not been for the accounting rule, ADT would have posted a profit of $31 million, or 14 percent higher than the same quarter last year. First-quarter sales were 5 percent lower than a year ago, but only because ADT sold two European business units that accounted for $54.5 million in the first quarter of 1995.

ADT is the largest supplier of electronic security services in North America and the second-largest provider of vehicle auction services in the United States.

Hamilton Bank

Miami-based Hamilton Bank reported record earnings for the first quarter of 1996, ended March 31. Net income at the privately held bank reached $2.53 million, an increase of 12 percent compared to the same three-month period of 1995.

Chairman and Chief Executive Eduardo A. Masferrer attributed higher earnings to continued growth in trade financing activities.

Hamilton Bank has five Florida branches and regional advisory offices in Europe and Latin America.
