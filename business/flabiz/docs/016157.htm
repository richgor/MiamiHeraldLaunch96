IN FLORIDA

Losses grow for Peoples Telephone

From Herald Staff and Wire Reports

Peoples Telephone Co. said its net loss increased by about $1.2 million while its revenues declined by 15.4 percent in the quarter ended March 31.

The Miami-based company, an operator of pay telephones, said its results were affected by a switch to AT&T as its national operator service provider and a decrease in its installed base of prison-inmate telephone lines.

E. Craig Sanders, newly appointed chief executive, said he intends to focus on improving service, ``internal growth, alignment of accountability, and pushing decision making out closer to the customer.''

TresCom International revenues rise 55%

TresCom International of Fort Lauderdale reported a 55 percent increase in first-quarter revenue Wednesday and said it cut its losses for the period by more than half.

TresCom, a provider of international long-distance telephone service, had lost $7.6 million in the first quarter of 1995 and cut it to $3.4 million. Company President Wesley O'Brien said the increases in revenue and service usage illustrate the pent-up demand for alternative long-distance carriers abroad.

ProxyMed holds public offering

ProxyMed of Fort Lauderdale announced that Wednesday's public offering of 2 million shares was priced at $6.62 per share. An underwriting group led by Commonwealth Associates conducted the sale. Of those shares, 1.9 million were offered by ProxyMed, with the remainder offered by a selling stockholder.

ProxyMed is a healthcare information technology firm. The company's shares rose 75 cents Wednesday to close at $8.

Brazilian firm rolls out news plane

The newest airplane model built by Brazilian manufacturer Embraer will make its first U.S. appearance today in Fort Lauderdale.

The 50-seat, twin-engine Embraer EMB-145, flown in Wednesday from Brazil, will begin a 13-city tour in Fort Lauderdale at Embraer's U.S. headquarters, which houses marketing, training, parts and technical service offices.

The plane sells for about $15 million. Embraer spokesman Idan Sims said the manufacturer has orders from BWIA as well as several Australian and Brazilian carriers, and expects to display the plane to U.S. regional airlines during the month-long tour.

Embraer employs about 75 people in Fort Lauderdale.
