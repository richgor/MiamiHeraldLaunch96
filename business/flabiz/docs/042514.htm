Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Republic Industries' CFO resigns

By JAMES MCNAIR
Herald Business Writer

Republic Industries, Wayne Huizenga's latest foray into the Fortune 500, announced the resignation of its chief financial officer Thursday -- one day before the company's annual meeting of shareholders.

The Fort Lauderdale company said Gregory K. Fairbanks resigned for personal reasons. It wouldn't elaborate.

Fairbanks, 42, could not be reached for comment Thursday.

Fairbanks has served as Republic's executive vice president and chief financial officer since Huizenga bought a
controlling stake in the company last August. The Fairbanks-Huizenga association dates back to 1980. He was Huizenga's CFO at both Blockbuster Entertainment and Waste Management.

Huizenga, Republic's chairman and chief executive, praised Fairbanks' contributions to the company.

``We will miss Greg, but respect his decision,'' Huizenga said in a statement. ``We appreciate his willingness to remain with the company through the first of July to assist us in making an orderly transition of his responsibilities.''

The resignation is certain to raise shareholders' eyebrows, if not prompt questions at this morning's annual meeting. But Republic shareholders are a very contented lot: Since Huizenga took over, its common shares have grown tenfold in value.

According to Republic's proxy statement, Fairbanks owns 300,000 Republic shares and has options to buy 34,000 more for $3.87 1/2 apiece.

The resignation was announced after the market closed Thursday. Republic shares gained $1.62 1/2 to close at $37.87 1/2.
