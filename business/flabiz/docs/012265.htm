Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Salem managers counter Posner's bid for firm

Dow Jones News Service

A group of Salem Corp. managers has countered Miami financier Victor Posner's bid to take over the company by offering to buy the maker of industrial furnaces for about $41 million, or $22 a share.

The buyout offer comes a month after Posner, who owns about 49 percent of Salem's common shares, offered to buy the remaining shares for $20 each.

Posner is trying to take the Pittsburgh-based company private to circumvent a federal court order in December 1993 that bars him from being an officer or director of a public firm.

Salem stock rose 4 percent, or 87 1/2 cents, to close at $22.25 Thursday on the American Stock Exchange.

The management group, led by A.A. Fornataro, president, and Donald Hoylman, vice president, said Salem's acceptance of its offer would end Posner's relationship with the company.


Salem said it welcomed the offer and will respond as soon as it concludes its study.

In April, Salem said its board was not prepared to recommend Posner's roughly $36 million-bid for the company to its shareholders ``in its current form.''

Donald Glazer, a representative for Posner, described the management buyout offer as ``very strange.''

``We'd like to see what the conditions of this offer are first and to see whether this is just more stalling on behalf of the company or if it's a serious offer,' Glazer said.
