Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Capital Factors plans IPO

Capital Factors Holding Inc., a Fort Lauderdale-based subsidiary of Capital Bank, has filed a registration statement with the Securities and Exchange Commission for a proposed initial public offering of 2 million shares of Capital Factors Holding Inc. common stock, which will represent about 17 percent of the outstanding shares.

Oppenheimer & Co. will manage the underwriting group. An over-allotment option could increase the size of the offering to 2.3 million shares of common stock, the company said. Net proceeds from the proposed offering are expected to be used to reduce outstanding debt.

Capital Factors provides receivables-based commercial financing and related fee-based credit, collection and management information services.

Brazilian firm rolls out new plane

The newest airplane model built by Brazilian manufacturer Embraer made its first U.S. appearance this week in Fort Lauderdale.

The 50-seat, twin-engine Embraer EMB-145 began a 13-city tour in Fort Lauderdale at Embraer's U.S. headquarters, which houses marketing, training, parts and technical service offices and employs about 75 people.

The plane sells for about $15 million. Embraer spokesman Idan Sims said the manufacturer has orders from BWIA as well as several Australian and Brazilian carriers.
