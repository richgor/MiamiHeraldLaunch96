Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Precision Response files to take company public

By ALINA MATAS
Herald Business Writer

Precision Response Corp., a Miami Lakes company that has grown into one of the country's top telemarketing firms, has decided to sell stock to the public to finance further growth.

The company, which answers customer-service calls for large corporations, has filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission for an initial public offering.

The company wants to offer up to 3.45 million shares of stock to repay debt, make earnings distributions to its shareholders and fund expansion.

Precision executives declined to comment on the offering, citing SEC regulations.

Precision's revenues have increased from $16.2 million in 1991 to $30.2 million in 1995. Net income has fluctuated, from $1.8 million in 1991 to $1.5 million in 1995. The balance on a $15 million, three-year credit line and on various installment loans stood at a total of $6.8 million.

Founder and Chief Executive Mark Gordon and President David Epstein are the major shareholders.

Gordon founded the company in 1982 as Florida Fulfillment, mailing brochures and other promotional information on behalf of corporate clients. It had only two telephones.

Gordon bought out two of the company's partners and steered Florida Fulfillment into telephones and marketing. In 1991, Gordon and co-owner Epstein changed the firm's name to Precision Response.

Today, the company ranks among the country's top 20 telemarketing firms. In its six telephone call centers, more than 2,000 employees answer customer-service calls for clients such as AT&T, Taco Bell, Anheuser-Busch and British Airways.

The bulk of the business, 76.5 percent of 1995 revenues, came from its five largest clients.

The share price of the public offering will be determined in late June, according to a spokeswoman for Dain Bosworth, the underwriter.
