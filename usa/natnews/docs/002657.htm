Garth Williams, fanciful illustrator of Charlotte's Web

New York Times Service

Garth Williams, the artist who illustrated E.B. White's Stuart Little and Charlotte's Web and scores of other children's classics, died Wednesday in Guanajuato, Mexico. He was 84.

Generations of children picture their favorite fictional characters as drawn by Williams: that dapper mouse Stuart Little; the kindhearted spider Charlotte and Wilbur the pig; and bears, dogs, kittens, crickets, elves, fairies and children in books by Laura Ingalls Wilder, George Selden, Charlotte Zolotow, Else H. Minarik and oth
ers. Williams also wrote the text for seven books.

He said he used his illustrations to try to ``awaken something of importance . . . humor, responsibility, respect for others, interest in the world at large.''

Williams' father was a cartoonist, his mother a landscape painter. He first intended to become an architect, then switched to painting and sculpture. He graduated from the Royal Academy of Art in London.

Williams also illustrated Laura Ingalls Wilder's Little House on the Prairie and its sequels. In addition, he illustrated books by Margaret Wise Brown, Russell Hoban and Randall Jarrell. Among his most popular books were those written by George Selden, beginning with The Cricket in Times Square.

In 1958, he wrote and illustrated The Rabbits' Wedding, which became the subject of controversy because it dealt with a marriage between a white rabbit and a black rabbit.

For the past 40 years, Williams lived in a hacienda he built in Guanajuato and in his home in San Antonio.

He is survived by his wife Leticia, five daughters and a son.
