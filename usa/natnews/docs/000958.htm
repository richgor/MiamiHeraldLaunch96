Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Cleveland judge ends court-ordered busing

CLEVELAND -- (AP) -- A federal judge lifted part of a desegregation order Wednesday, ending nearly two decades of court-ordered busing in Cleveland's school system.

U.S. Senior Circuit Judge Robert B. Krupansky found that Ohio's biggest school district had followed a plan that was supposed to achieve racial balance, largely through cross-town busing.

James Hardiman, the lawyer for the group of black students and their parents who sued the district in 1973, said he had not seen the ruling yet. ``But it is disappointing if the court has sanctioned segregation,'' he said.

Rick Ellis, spokesman for the 72,000-student school district, said the ruling will give the district more flexibility in assigning students to schools, beginning with the upcoming school year.
