Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

AURAND HARRIS

Playwright for children's theater

Los Angeles Times

Aurand Harris, a prolific playwright for children's theater, best known for his 1963 classic Androcles and the Lion, died Monday in New York. He was 80.

Harris penned more than 50 published plays beginning with Once Upon a Clothesline in 1945, which broke from the fairy tales traditionally featured in children's theater and instead profiled two characters dressed in yellow jumpsuits to resemble clothespins.

Among his most popular plays are Steal Away Home, Yankee Doodle and The Arkansaw Bear.

He often directed his own plays and was invited to China in 1988 to direct his version of Rags to Riches in Shanghai.

Harris also edited several anthologies, among them Plays Children Love ; and Short Plays of Theatre Classics.

He taught at Grace Church School in Manhattan and at New York University.

A book called The Theatre of Aurand Harris: His Career, His Theories, His Plays is scheduled for publication in June.
