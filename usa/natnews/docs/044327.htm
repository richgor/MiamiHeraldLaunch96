Published Monday, May 6, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN THE NATION

Two 16-year-olds accused of cybercrimes

SOUTH BRUNSWICK -- Two boys posed as billing representatives for an online service and stole at least 15 credit card numbers to buy $15,000 worth of merchandise, from computer equipment to cymbals, police said.

A dozen other South Brunswick teenagers are being investigated, police said.

The two 16-year-olds were charged with 39 counts of possession of stolen property, theft and attempted fraud. They were released to the custody of their parents pending a Family Court hearing.

MICHIGAN

An Olympic reward for selling cookies

RIVER ROUGE -- A ninth-grader at River Rouge High School sold 3,520 boxes of Girl Scout cookies and, as a reward, the Girl Scout Council will pay for her trip to the Olympic Games in Atlanta.

L'Kisha Brantley, one of 40 Girl Scouts in the nation chosen to make the trip, also set a cookie sale record for the Detroit metropolitan area Girl Scout Council.

ETC

  • CALIFORNIA -- San Francisco Mayor Willie Brown wants to be known as the mayor with the open door and plans to make himself available to the public on Saturdays, starting in June. But visitors will have to talk fast, because they'll only get 10 minutes apiece.
  • OHIO -- The mother of a teenage inmate killed at a state prison has filed a $100 million lawsuit claiming that authorities did not do enough to protect her son. Damico Watkins, 17, was stabbed to death April 25 in a disturbance in the juvenile unit of Madison Correctional Institution in London, 25 miles west of Columbus.
