Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

LOCAL 4B

WILLIAM BRENNEN

Former bank official and mayoral aide

Associated Press

NEW YORK -- William S. Brennen, a deputy bank superintendent for Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller and a top aide to Mayors Abraham Beame and Edward Koch, died Wednesday. He was 72.

Brennen backed Rockefeller's run for governor in 1958, and the governor rewarded him by naming him First Deputy Superintendent of Banks.

In 1963, Brennen was named a senior vice president of Greenwich Savings Bank. He became its president in 1969 and chairman in 1971.

Beame asked Brennen to set up the New York City Industrial Development Agency, and he served as its volunteer chairman for 12 years, overseeing $500 million in loans to city businesses.

In 1983, Koch asked Brennen to overhaul the city's economic development agencies and he served as commissioners over two separate agencies, Business Development and International Business Development.

He left the city in 1986 to practice law. At the time of his death, Brennen was a partner in the law firm of Brennen & Stack.
