CONNECTICUT

One Bass taketh, 2nd giveth -- $20 million

NEW HAVEN -- A member of the billionaire Bass family of Texas is donating $20 million to Yale University, one year after his brother withdrew the same amount in a dispute with the school.

The new donation from Robert M. Bass, a 1971 Yale graduate, and his wife, Anne, will help renovate the Ivy League school's aging residential colleges, where most undergraduates live and eat for three of their four years at Yale.

Lee M. Bass, a 1979 alumnus, donated $20 million in 1991 to establish a program in Western civilization, but Yale returned the sum last year. Bass complained that the university kept him in the dark about what was happening with the program, which was never set up.

MASSACHUSETTS

Traffic chief quits; more tickets revealed

BOSTON -- The city's new traffic and parking chief's embarrassing string of traffic and parking violations finally got too long, and he's out of his job after only eight days.

William Luster resigned From the job he had started May 1, as it was revealed that he had failed to tell city officials about two recent parking tickets, including one for blocking a handicapped ramp while he was in town on his job interview.

Last week, it was revealed that the Registry of Motor Vehicles had sent him to a driver re-education program because of five speeding violations, three citations for causing accidents and two arrests for driving with a suspended license.
