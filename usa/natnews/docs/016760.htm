Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

DEATHS ELSEWHERE

ELSEWHERE
Evans, Daniel F. 74, a former chairman and president of L.S. Ayres department stores; in Indianapolis. He served as president, chairman and chief executive officer from 1965 to 1976.

Kallinger, Joseph, a shoemaker who killed his 14-year-old son and got another young son to help him murder a nurse; in prison in Cresson, Pa. Kallinger, who said he murdered his son and a 10-year-old neighbor two decades ago on orders from God, suffered a heart attack after he choked on his own vomit. A book, The Shoemaker, by Flora Rheta Schreiber, was based on Kallinger's career. He was denied royalties.

Maikovskis, Boleslavs, 92, who fled the United States when threatened with deportation to the Soviet Union for Nazi war crimes; in Muenster, Germany. To avoid a Soviet death sentence, Maikovskis, a Latvian native, escaped to Germany in 1987. He went on trial there three years later but was freed in 1994 after a court ruled his heart was too weak for further proceedings. Maikovskis denied charges that he ordered the massacre of more than 170 civilians as commander of a police force that collaborated with the Nazis in occupied Latvia.

Romero, Celedonio, 83, a Spanish guitarist; after a fall at the end of a concert in San Diego, where he lived since 1958. Romero, considered one of the great masters of the classical guitar, also had formed the Romero Quartet with his three sons, who are also soloists. His many concerts include a special recital for Pope John Paul II at the Vatican.
