IN THE NATION

Anti-abortion leader disputes news report

WASHINGTON -- Ralph Reed, the executive director of the Christian Coalition, tried to dispel any impression Saturday that he was out of step with other leaders of the anti-abortion movement, angrily dismissing as ``totally inaccurate'' a news article saying he would accept changes in the party's platform.

In a statement, the Christian Coalition said Saturday that the New York Times article ``inaccurately states'' that Reed ``would be willing to accept rape and incest exceptions as a possible change in the wording of the pro-life plank of the Republican Party's platform.''

Reed said the Christian Coalition favored an exception to an abortion ban only if the mother's life was endangered.

CALIFORNIA

Parents are celebrating birth of Siamese twins

LOMA LINDA -- Parents of Siamese twins joined at the abdomen are celebrating the girls' birth, having overcome the initial shock of their children's medical condition.

Jeff and Michelle Roderick, both 29, say they are beaming with pride and still getting acquainted with the girls, Shawna Leilani and Janelle Kiana.

The girls were delivered by Caesarean section last week at Loma Linda University Medical Center. Facing each other and sharing liver tissue, they weighed a combined 11 pounds, 4.7 ounces at birth. Doctors hope to attempt a separation in a few months.

Michelle Roderick said she was upset when she first learned in December that the twins were joined. The couple decided against abortion, and the girls were born with a good prognosis for successful separation. ``We've been double-blessed with these girls,'' the mother said.
