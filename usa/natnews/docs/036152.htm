Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Copley, William N., 77, a surrealist painter known for his images of men in starchy Edwardian suits and naked women; in Sugar

Loaf Key, Fla. His cartoonlike figures with their black outlines and colorful backgrounds presaged pop art and drew from American folk art. Copley was orphaned as an infant and adopted by Ira C. Copley, a newspaper tycoon. The adopted Copley worked briefly as a reporter for The San Diego Tribune, one of Copley's papers. Harris, Aurand, 80, a prolific playwright for children's theater best known for his 1963 classic Androcles and the Lion; in New York. He penned more than 50 published plays beginning with Once Upon a Clothesline in 1945. Among his most popular plays are Steal Away Home, Yankee Doodle and The Arkansaw Bear. Harris also edited several anthologies, among them Plays Children Love ; and Short Plays of Theatre Classics. A book called The Theatre of Aurand Harris: His Career, His Theories, His Plays is scheduled for publication in June.

Rouilard, Richard, 43, the former editor of the Advocate, the nation's oldest major homosexual magazine; of AIDS in West Hollywood, Calif. As editor from 1990 to 1992, Rouilard increased circulation of the Advocate from 60,000 to 150,000. He was praised for raising the journalistic standards of the magazine, which examined such issues as police brutality, homosexual suicide and the military ban on gays.
