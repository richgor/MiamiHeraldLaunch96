Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

N.C. rematch: Helms vs. Gantt

RALEIGH, N.C. -- (AP) -- Former Charlotte Mayor Harvey Gantt swept aside political newcomer Charlie Sanders in Tuesday's racially charged Democratic primary, setting up a rematch with Republican Sen. Jesse Helms.

With 49 percent of precincts reporting, Gantt had 143,049 votes, or 53 percent; Sanders had 111,168 votes, or 41 percent; and Ralph McKinney of Durham had 13,888 votes or 5 percent.

The Democratic Senate race pitted Sanders, the former chief executive of pharmaceutical giant Glaxo, against the first black mayor of Charlotte in an often bitter campaign.

Sanders campaigned as the only Democrat who could beat Helms in November. Gantt lost to Helms 52 percent to 48 percent in the 1990 general election.

Gantt accused Sanders of playing the race card by implying that he has an edge against Helms because he is white in a state where blacks make up just 20 percent of registered voters.

Helms, who is seeking a fifth term, faced no primary opposition but has already run attack ads accusing both Democrats of being liberals who support racial preferences in hiring and health benefits for gay partners.
