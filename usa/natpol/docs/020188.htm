Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Candidates get free time on four networks

Knight-Ridder News Service

All major television networks except ABC have now agreed to offer free time for presidential candidates to state their views this fall, and ABC is expected to announce a plan today.

NBC and CNN announced plans Tuesday, following CBS' announcement Monday that it would make time available within its newscasts.

NBC, like CBS, said the candidates would be given the time on its news programs, ranging from Today to the Nightly News to Dateline. CNN, like Fox Television, agreed to provide the time during the peak viewing hours, between 8 and 11 p.m.

The networks have been prodded by a public-interest coalition that includes Walter Cronkite, and then by Rupert Murdoch, the head of Fox, who announced in February that he would offer free time.

Cronkite and others argue that free time for candidates to seriously discuss issues will help to reduce the effect of big money and corrosive negative advertising.
