Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Triple-murder suspect arrested in N. Carolina

Associated Press

JACKSONVILLE -- An auto mechanic wanted in the slayings of three members of a Ukrainian family here was arrested Tuesday after running out of gas in North Carolina.

Dentcho ``Danny'' Denev, 38, who came to the United States five years ago from Bulgaria, was arrested in Sampson County, N.C., said Harry Reagan, a spokesman for the Jacksonville Sheriff's Office.

When authorities in North Carolina checked his auto tag, they learned he was wanted in Florida, Reagan said. Sampson County is about 400 miles north of Jacksonville on Interstate 95.

``There was no resistance,'' said Sampson County Sheriff Buck McCullen. ``This guy had given out of gas and had gone to a service station to obtain gas and had caught a ride back. When he got back . . . he was arrested at that point.''

McCullen said the suspect showed officers a .40-caliber Glock pistol that authorities think may have been the weapon in the slayings. The pistol was under the front seat, the sheriff said.

Denev is charged in a Florida warrant with three counts of first-degree murder and one count of aggravated battery.

Killed early Monday were his former girlfriend, Irina Leonova, 37; her brother, Sergey Zadnepryanets, 34, and her father, Yuri Zadnepryanets, 62. All the victims were Ukrainian Jews from Kiev.

Leonova's 8-year-old son, Kiril, was listed in serious condition Tuesday at University Medical Center.

Reagan said he did not know if Denev planned to fight extradition back to Jacksonville.

The shootings occurred about 12:30 a.m. Monday at the Williamsburg Commons apartments. All the victims died of multiple gunshot wounds.

Denev had an ``on-again, off-again'' relationship with the woman and a prior record of domestic violence, said Lt. David Gueterman, a police homicide detective, said Monday.

Gueterman said the initial target may have been the woman's brother, who often tried to protect her from Denev and repeatedly warned him to stay away.
