Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Judge: Pedophile can't keep kids' pictures

Associated Press

CRESTVIEW -- A convicted pedophile has lost his court battle to have thousands of sexually explicit photographs of his victims and other youngsters returned to him.

Judge Keith Brace ruled that John Gay, 30, did not prove that he was legally entitled to possess the 7,500 photographs, many of which show nude boys.

Inspectors at the Okaloosa Correctional Institution had seized the pictures claiming they were pornographic and violated prison rules.

Gay claimed they were evidence for his pending appeals and accused officials of also seizing a photograph of his deceased mother.

Gay's civil complaint gained attention when Gov. Lawton Chiles cited his case as an example of frivolous lawsuits filed by prison inmates.

Gay vigorously denied that charge, saying he was trying to prove his innocence.

A former employee of a photo processing store, Gay was sentenced in 1990 to two life terms for sexual battery on children under 12 plus 210 years on related charges.

In the ruling signed last week, the judge warned Gay that he would face court sanctions if he files another lawsuit on the same issue.

Meanwhile, he denied the state's request that Gay be ordered to pay the state's legal fees.

The judge also ordered the destruction of any sexually explicit photographs, but ruled Gay can mail any nonoffensive pictures of children to a friend or relative. Otherwise, they will be destroyed as well.

``This puts to rest an issue that's been dogging the prison and the taxpayers for five years,'' Assistant Attorney General David Frank said this week. ``It's outrageous that this case has consumed this kind of time and money, but that's what it takes.''

Chiles signed a bill last month intended to discourage frivolous inmate lawsuits. The lawsuits cost taxpayers an estimated $2.3 million annually.

About 1,200 cases are still pending statewide.
