Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

FOR KEYS 5B

A ROUNDUP OF NEWS IN THE SUNSHINE STATE

PANHANDLE
Bomb found, exploded

A homemade PVC pipe bomb was detonated safely in the woods after Panama City Mall security officers found the explosive in their office in a zippered blue notebook confiscated from two teenagers.

No book ban

The Goosebumps series of ``soft-core horror'' novels for young readers will remain in Bay County elementary schools, the School Board decided, rejecting a request by a third-grade student's parents to ban the books.

CENTRAL FLORIDA
Save the drive-in

Ocoee residents, campaigning to keep a nearby drive-in movie theater open, raised enough money to light the big screen for two nights this weekend. One of the films is The Blues Brothers.

Airport train

A transportation company struck a deal with the state Department of Transportation to develop a high-speed magnetic train for freight and passenger use at the Space Center Executive Airport in Titusville.

TALLAHASSEE
Appeal rejected

Serial killer Aileen Wuornos, a hitchhiking prostitute convicted and sentenced to die for murdering a half-dozen men, lost her sixth appeal filed with the state Supreme Court.
