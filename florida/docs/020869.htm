Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Oil lease reimbursement sought

Associated Press

TALLAHASSEE -- Coastal Petroleum is again pressing a claim for state restitution for a ban on oil drilling in Gulf Coast waters, where Coastal holds rights to royalties from any oil production.

Coastal's claim was rejected in a summary judgment in Leon County Circuit Court in 1993. But rather than dismiss the claim outright, the 1st District Court of Appeal ordered the court to hear arguments.

Leon Circuit Judge Philip Padovano began hearing an expected five days of testimony this week, to be followed by depositions and written arguments before he issues a ruling.

The controversy is over a 1976 agreement in which the state gave Coastal the right to collect 6.25 percent royalties on any oil production on about 2.5 million acres of tidal lands -- stretching roughly from Apalachicola to Naples and extending 4.3 miles out from shore.

Coastal President Phil Ware acknowledged under questioning Tuesday by Assistant Attorney General Robert Glogau that there had been no major oil discoveries on the lands.

But Coastal attorney Robert Angerer said, ``At the time the settlement was signed, Coastal anticipated leases would be signed on the lands.''

The state outlawed drilling in offshore waters in 1990, and Coastal claims its royalty interests were rendered worthless.
