Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Suspect: I killed ex-girlfriend, her family `out of jealous love'

Associated Press

JACKSONVILLE -- A jailed suspect charged with murdering his former girlfriend, her father and brother waived his initial court appearance Friday.

Dentcho ``Danny'' Denev, 38, was returned to Jacksonville on Thursday after being arrested in North Carolina. He is charged with first-degree murder for the shootings of Irina Leonova, 37, her brother Sergey Zadnepryanets, 34, and their father, Yuriy Zadnepryanets, 62.

While being escorted from the Jacksonville Sheriff's Office to the county jail Thursday, Denev attempted to explain his actions.

``It was jealous love. . . . It was love and hate mixed together,'' said Denev, who had dated Leonova but recently was spurned by her.

``It all happened so fast. I cannot remember what happened,'' he said of the shootings early Monday morning. ``Of course I'm sorry what happened.''

Denev also is charged with aggravated battery in the wounding of Leonova's 8-year-old son Kirill, who is in good condition in University Medical Center with a gunshot wound to the right shoulder.

Denev, a Bulgarian auto mechanic who came to the United States five years ago, said he needed help before the shootings.

``Where was everybody when I needed help?''

He was arrested in Sampson County, N.C., after a state trooper found him sleeping in his car Tuesday along Interstate 40. He said his car ran out of gas.

Public Defender Bill White, whose office was appointed to represent Denev, said his client was under a lot of stress when he spoke to reporters.

Prosecutors plan to take the case to a grand jury as early as next week to seek a first-degree murder indictment.
