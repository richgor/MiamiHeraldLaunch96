FLA. PAGE 6B

High-tech brain lab nets UF millions

GAINESVILLE -- (AP) -- Although groundbreaking for the University of Florida Brain Institute has yet to start, a $13.3 million federal grant will someday help fill the facility with high-tech equipment.

U.S. Rep. Bill Young, R-St. Petersburg, who shepherded the request through Congress, announced the Department of Defense/Veterans Affairs Medical Program award Friday.

Young said the investment will heavily outweigh the costs of treating brain diseases.

``The research that this money will help fund . . . will deal with issues like head and neck injuries, Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease -- so many diseases of the neurological system, the brain,'' said the chairman of the House Appropriations Committee, which approved the grant.

The equipment will include body scanners, computers and a 12-Tesla magnetic resonance imaging unit. Once the 200,000-square-foot center opens in 1998, faculty from both UF and the Gainesville Veterans Administration will make use of the facility.

Construction of the center, which will double as a training center for military health care professionals, is supported by an $18 million Defense Department award matched with another $26 million from the university's College of Medicine and Shands Hospital.
