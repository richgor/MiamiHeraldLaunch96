Published Monday, May 6, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Crusader for poor found murdered

Dying minister dialed 911

Associated Press

ROCKLEDGE -- A self-proclaimed minister and crusader for the disadvantaged who himself had been in trouble with the law has died of stab wounds.

Jack Anthony Ott, 41, called 911 from his home early Saturday, but could only mutter, ``Help, help'' before collapsing, police said. Ott died later Saturday at a Rockledge hospital.

Police sought clues to the Vietnam veteran's stabbing death Sunday. There were no suspects.

Ott was deeply religious and often would bring vagrants into his home for a meal or to help them back on their feet, said his brother-in-law, Ray Hall.

He began referring to himself as a reverend and started a church in his home that he called the Church of the Holy Spirit. In a column he wrote two years ago for the Florida Today newspaper, he said he had visions of angels.

``One day I was looking at my rosary and decided if I could find people who believed in God and would pray once a day to him, that God would love them and share his powers.''

Ott had a longstanding feud with his ex-wife over child custody, had a drug problem and had been charged many times with assault and battery, police said. Additionally, he went to prison several times in connection with stalking and beating his ex-wife.

``He was always doing something [to get himself in trouble], but he had a big heart,'' said Debbie Baker, a former girlfriend.
