Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Bulgarian sought in triple slaying

Associated Press

JACKSONVILLE -- (AP) -- A woman, her father and brother were shot to death early Monday, but her 8-year-old son survived. Police were seeking the woman's former boyfriend for questioning.

``It was a pretty gruesome scene,'' said Lt. David Gueterman, a homicide detective. He described the slayings as ``a crime of passion.''

The shootings occurred on both floors in a two-story townhouse at the Williamsburg Commons complex, and several shots were lodged in the apartment walls, he said.

Dentcho ``Danny'' Denev, 38, an auto mechanic who came to the United States five years ago from Bulgaria, was wanted for questioning.

Killed were Irina Leonova, 37; her brother, Sergey Zadnepryanets, 34; and their father, Yuriy Zadnepryanets, 62. All of the victims were Ukrainians from Kiev.

When called to the apartment, police found the brother's body outside and the bodies of the woman and her father inside. All died from gunshot wounds.

Leonova was a dentist in Kiev and was working as a dental assistant in Jacksonville.

Her son, Kirill Leonova, was in serious condition at University Medical Center with a gunshot wound to the right chest, a hospital spokesman said.

Denev had an ``on-again, off-again'' relationship with the woman and a prior record of domestic violence, Gueterman said. He said he hadn't determined if the previous incidents involved Denev's wife or the slain girlfriend.

``We believe he [Denev] went over there as an ongoing confrontation with the brother. He confronted the brother, and the shooting began,'' Gueterman said.
