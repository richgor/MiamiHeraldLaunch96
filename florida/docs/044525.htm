Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

FOR KEYS 5B

A ROUNDUP OF NEWS IN THE SUNSHINE STATE

PANHANDLE
Pollution claims settled

Champion International Paper Co. will pay $5 million to settle pollution claims with 2,000 Florida and Alabama landowners who claimed that dioxin and other pollutants from its Escambia County plant polluted Eleven Mile Creek and Perdido Bay.

Inmate turned down

A Crestview judge refused to release to convicted pedophile John Gay thousands of sexually explicit photographs of his victims and other youngsters that he said were evidence for his appeals.

NORTH FLORIDA
Protecting turtles

Apart from putting up a chain link fence, Volusia County is doing all it can to keep cars away from endangered sea turtles along 41 miles of beach, a lawyer for the county told a federal judge.

TALLAHASSEE
Restitution sought

Coastal Petroleum is again pressing a claim for state restitution for a ban on oil drilling in Gulf Coast waters, where Coastal holds rights to royalties from any oil production.

The Bailey case

Attorney F. Lee Bailey hopes to prove a former client used Canadian rather than U.S. drug profits to buy stock over which Bailey and the government are wrangling, but government attorneys say the national origin of illegal proceeds make no difference.

END OF THE LINE
Seeing is believing

A 21-year-old Tampa driver was on his way to traffic court to face a charge of failing to wear his glasses while driving, but he didn't wear his glasses and ran into a car involved in an accident.
