Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Panels refuse to ban book by black students

Associated Press

FORT WALTON BEACH -- Two review panels have refused to ban a book of black-written poetry from middle schools after a parent contended it promotes violence and complained about expletives and a reference to abortion.

Steve Powers of nearby Valparaiso said he would carry his fight to the Okaloosa County School Board after a second panel turned down his request Tuesday to pull Poetry of Black America from school libraries.

A seven-member committee concluded the 552-page book published in 1973 by Harper & Row is a valuable resource that should remain on the shelves, where it has gathered more dust than readers during the past 21 years.

The panel included a cross section of school employees and School Board member Bert Moore. Earlier, another review panel at Lewis Middle School, where Powers' 14-year-old daughter is a student, refused to ban the book.

``I did not find it to be so offensive that I would want to say that the people at Lewis had gone too far,'' Moore said.

Powers agrees that the book, taken as a whole, has value and may be suitable for older students, but he maintains it is inappropriate for younger children.

The poems he complained about focus on the civil rights struggles of the 1950s and 1960s, but the book also chronicles black experiences in America during the past 150 years.

The Florida Panhandle paint and body worker, a father of two, said he would reserve further comment until the School Board considers the matter, possibly at its May 27 meeting.

He has been trying to generate support for a ban by passing out copies of passages he considers objectionable at churches in Valparaiso and adjacent Niceville.

The book also is in libraries at one other middle school and two high schools in Okaloosa County, according to a survey by Keith Kyker, educational media specialist at Lewis.

A sampling by Kyker of nearly 40 percent of Florida schools found the book available in 126 high schools, 75 middle schools and 38 elementary schools.
