Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Tearful teen breaks silence on gang slaying

Associated Press

KISSIMMEE -- The victim's parents watched in quiet disbelief as a primly dressed teenager pleaded guilty to being an accessory to murder for keeping silent about the gang killing of their 22-year-old son.

Rebecca and Larry Oliver Sr. sat in the courtroom's first row as they watched Mary Lineberger, 19, wipe her eyes with a tissue and offer her plea in such a soft voice the judge told her to speak up.

A waitress by day and stripper by night, she was the third young adult in as many days to admit a role in the shotgun killing of the Olivers' son.

Her crime was saying nothing about the slaying while the Olivers prayed through six nights for their son's safe return home.

Larry Oliver Jr. lay dead in an orange grove.

And not one of a group of seven friends called for help, despite watching the killing, cleaning up afterward, stripping the victim's shiny red pickup or destroying evidence, investigators said.

``It's so hard to say what I ask myself, what I think about when I see her,'' Larry Oliver Sr. said after watching Lineberger plead guilty Thursday. ``She went about her normal life for an entire week. She sat on the information and didn't say anything.

``The moral thing is to go forward and tell what you know. But there's a lot of morality this group didn't have. They didn't have the moral background our son had.''

The man accused of the killing, Jermaine ``Bugsy'' Gleason, is in jail in New York, where he had a court hearing Thursday to fight extradition to Osceola County.

A judge's decision was expected soon.

Gleason and most of his co-defendants were members of a youth gang known as Four-Play, records show. Members believed they were closer than family and pledged to live and die by the knife and gun.

The only way out of the gang was to die, one of the members said in a sworn statement.

The Four-Play members met through jobs at Walt Disney World or at topless bars in Orlando. Lineberger, 19, bridged both worlds.

Three others are expected to plead guilty in coming weeks.
