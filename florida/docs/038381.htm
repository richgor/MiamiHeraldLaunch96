Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

FOR KEYS 5B

A ROUNDUP OF NEWS IN THE SUNSHINE STATE

NORTH FLORIDA
3 shot to death

A woman, her father and brother were shot to death in a Jacksonville apartment and police were seeking the woman's former boyfriend for questioning; all victims were Ukrainians from Kiev.

Drug use rises

Marijuana use has increased dramatically, hallucinogenic drugs are growing in popularity and cigarette smoking is the highest ever reported among young people, according to a survey of nearly 200,000 American schoolchildren analyzed at the University of Florida.

CENTRAL FLORIDA
Imitating life

A computerized doll that cries is being used to give Osceola County middle and high school students a taste of life with an infant is one of the newest tactics against teen pregnancy.

GULF COAST
Developer seeks deal

A developer can build on uninhabited Buck Key -- site of a Calusa Indian village that mostly belongs to the J.N. ``Ding'' Darling National Wildlife Refuge -- but says he would rather make a deal so he can build on Captiva Island.

TALLAHASSEE
Breaking up HRS

In the Legislature's plan to break up the state Department of Health and Rehabilitative Services, not all health offices are moving to a newly created Department of Health. Offices for Alcohol, Drug Abuse and Mental Health are remaining at HRS' successor, a new Department of Children and Family Services.
