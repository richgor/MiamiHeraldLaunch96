FOR YOUR INFORMATION

A LOOK AT WHAT'S HAPPENING IN PALM BEACH COUNTY


T
he For Your Information column appears every Friday and Sunday in the Florida section of The Herald. You'll find classes, lectures, programs for seniors and children, and Palm Beach County community entertainment events.

Things to Do, a comprehensive list of entertainment events, is in The Herald's Weekend section every Friday.

THIS WEEK





TODAY

Art on the Square: A juried art show, food fest and entertainment will be featured from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. today at Oakbrook Square, PGA Boulevard at U.S. 1, North Palm Beach. Free. Call (954) 472-3755.

Israel Independence Day: Judaica, arts and crafts, children's activities, entertainment and more will be featured from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. at the Kaplan JCC, 3151 N. Military Tr., West Palm Beach. Admission $1. Call 689-7700.

Sex change: Neil Simon's The Odd Couple (female version) will be performed by PBCC Theatre Program students at 2 p.m. today at the Duncan Theatre, PBCC, Lake Worth. Tickets are $10. Call 439-8141.

Kid's chorus: An alfresco concert of songs arranged especially for the setting will be performed by kids aged 7-14 at 3 p.m. at the Gumbo Limbo Nature Center, Boca Raton. Cost is $1. Call 338-1473.

Vocal competition: The Vocal Competition Winner's Concert of the Palm Beach Opera will be offered at 4 p.m. at the Kravis Center, West Palm Beach. Some of the singers, aged 18-30, will be performing for the first time with a professional orchestra. Tickets are $15 at the door. Call 833-9584.

Life beyond the pail: Jane Martin's Jack and Jill, a study of love over time, will be performed through May 12 at the Lois Pope Theatre, Plaza del Mar, Manalapan. Shows will be at 8 p.m. Wednesday to Saturday, at 7 p.m. Sunday, and at 2 p.m. Wednesday and Sunday. Tickets are $24, $26 or $29. Call 585-3433.

Tale of Scott's men kilt by cold: Journals and letters left behind by Capt. Robert F. Scott inform screenplay writer Ted Tally's Terra Nova, an account of the explorer's doomed 1911 race to the South Pole, through May 19 at the Caldwell Theatre Company, 7873 N. Federal Hwy., Boca Raton. Shows will be at 8 p.m. Tuesday to Saturday, at 7 p.m. Sunday, and at 2 p.m. Wednesday, Saturday and Sunday; tickets are $29 or $37.50. Call 241-7432 or 930-6400.

The Elephant Man: The Lake Worth Playhouse will present the sad story of John Merrick through May 5 at the Playhouse, 713 Lake Ave. Shows are generally at 8 p.m. Wednesday to Saturday, with 2:30 p.m. matinees on Sunday. Tickets are $15. Call 586-6410.

Hanging around town: The end-of-year juried show of works by students will by shown through Friday at the Humanities Gallery, PBCC, Lake Worth. Hours are 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. Monday to Thursday, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Friday. Call 439-8142.

  • The National Association of Women Artists, South Florida Chapter, will present a Spring Exhibit through Saturday at the Jeannette Hare Art Gallery, Northwood University, 2600 N. Military Tr., West Palm Beach. Hours are 8 a.m. to 7 p.m. Monday to Friday, 9 a.m. to 1 p.m. Saturday. Call 478-5555.

  • A multimedia juried art exhibition will be shown through May 5 at the Rosen Museum Gallery, Levis JCC, Boca Raton. Hours are 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Sunday to Friday. Call 852-3285.

  • The April Spotlight Show of the Artists' Showplace Cooperative will be shown through May 7 at the ASC Gallery, 7665 W. Lake Worth Rd. Free. Regular hours are 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Tuesday to Saturday, noon to 5 p.m. Sunday. Call 966-4908.

  • The Platinum Everglades, 18 platinum and palladium photographic prints by Lake Worth artist Irving Herman, will be shown through May 9 at the Cultural Showcase Exhibition at Palm Beach International Airport. Free. Call 930-2787.

  • Twenty-six members of the Delray Beach Camera Club were each given one roll of film on one day in March. The resulting study of life in Delray Beach, in more than 50 selected enlargements, will be shown through May 14 at City Hall, 100 NW 1st Ave. Hours are 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday to Friday. Free. Call 243-7000 or 495-5214.

  • Brain Teasers, a collection of puzzles, games and activities, will be the featured exhibition through May 15 at the South Florida Science Museum, West Palm Beach. Hours are 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. daily except Friday, 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. PartnerShipEarth, an illustration of our planet's origin and history, will be shown in the Planetarium through June 30, at 2 p.m. daily, at 7 p.m. Friday, and at noon
  • Saturday and Sunday. Cost of $1.75 in addition to museumadmission; $5 for adults; $3 for kids 13-21; $2 for kids 4-12. Call 832-1988.

  • History and Memory: Paintings by Christopher Brown will be shown through May 19 at the Norton Museum of Art, 1451 S. Olive Ave., West Palm Beach. The retrospective exhibition of 22 large-scale paintings examines collective memory, narrative and dream states. Hours are 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Tuesday and Wednesday, 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. Thursday, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Friday, Saturday and Sunday, 1 to 5 p.m. Admission $5. Call 832-5194.

  • Edna's Nudes, black-and-white photographs set in natural environments, will be shown through May 20 at Palm Beach Photographic Museum and Workshops, 55 NE 2nd Ave., Delray Beach. Hours are 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., Tuesday to Saturday. Admission $3. Call 276-9797.

  • Hand-painted screens and panels by abstract sculptor Nancy Nieves will be shown through May 22 at the Sculpture Center at Esperante, 222 Lakeview Ave., downtown West Palm Beach. Free. Call 930-2787.

  • Stone Echoes: Original Prints by Francoise Gilot will be shown through May 23 at the Schmidt Center Gallery, FAU, Boca Raton. Hours are 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Tuesday to Friday, noon to 4 p.m. Saturday. Free. Call 367-2966.

  • Addison Mizner, Inc.: Factory Made Originals (1919-1931), a collection of archival documents, photographs, products and more, will be shown through May 24 at Old Town Hall, 71 N. Federal Hwy., Boca Raton. Call 395-6766.

  • Laurence Sisson Retrospective: Paintings of Maine and the Southwest will be shown through May 31 at the Eissey Campus Art Gallery, 3160 PGA Blvd., Palm Beach Gardens. Hours are 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. Tuesday to Thursday, and 6 to 8 p.m. Wednesday and Thursday. A free tour is offered at 2 p.m. Call 625-2440.

  • Prints from Museum Collections: Distinctive Intaglio by Joseph Demarais will be shown through May 31 at Ryals Gallery, One S. Ocean Blvd., Boca Raton. Call 393-2461.

  • Wonderful Wood, an exhibit of photographs by Rudy Umans and handcrafted creations by the Palm Beach County Woodturners, will be shown through June 5 at the Okeeheelee Nature Center, West Palm Beach. Hours are 1 to 4:45 p.m. Sunday and Tuesday to Friday, 8:15 a.m. to 4:45 p.m. Saturday. Free. Call 233-1400.

  • Folding Images: Japanese Screens from the Liza Hyde Collection will be shown through July 7 at the Morikami Museum, Delray Beach. Hours are 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Tuesday to Sunday. Admission $4.25 for adults, $2 for kids. Call 495-0233.

  • Palm Beach Panorama: Turn-of-the-Century Photographs by E.W. Hazard will be shown through July 31 at the Flagler Museum, Palm Beach. Admission $7. Call 655-2833.






    MONDAY



    Law Week: The Palm Beach County Bar Association will sponsor a variety of free informational events this week. Dial-A-Lawyer will be conducted from 9 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. through Thursday; call 687-2800. An Ask-A-Lawyer booth will be stationed at the food court of the Palm Beach Mall, from noon to 6 p.m. through Thursday, and from 2 to 4 p.m. Friday. A Small Claims Court Clinic will be conducted from 7:30 to 8:30 p.m. today at the Central Library, 3650 Summit Blvd., West Palm Beach; registration requested, call 687-2800.

    Monday Night Movie: The Mystery of Picasso will be screened at 7 p.m. at the Lois Pope Theatre, Plaza del Mar, Manalapan. Tickets are $7. Call 585-3433.

    Performing Arts Series: Dan Leonard will star as Ernie Pyle at 8 p.m. at the Caldwell Theatre Company, 7873 N. Federal Hwy., Boca Raton. Tickets are $10. Call 241-7432 or 930-6400.

    Jazz and more: The PBCC concert Band and the 12 O'Clock Jazz Ensemble will perform at 8 p.m. at the Duncan Theatre, PBCC, Lake Worth. Free. Call 439-8141.






    TUESDAY


    Law Week: A Wills and Estate Planning Clinic will be offered from 6 to 7 p.m., and a Condominium and Homeowner Association Clinic will be offered from 7:30 to 8:30 p.m., at the Central Library, 3650 Summit Blvd., West Palm Beach. Palm Beach County Bar Association registration requested; call 687-2800.

    History lecture: Juno Beach resident Dan Corbett will deliver ``Indian Key: First County Seat'' for the Florida History Center & Museum Lecture Series at 7 p.m. at the Juno Beach Town Center, 340 Ocean Dr. Free. Call 747-6639.

    Women boaters: A Coast Guard Auxiliary Boating Skills and Seamanship course particularly for women will be conducted from 7:30 to 9:30 p.m. on Tuesdays for six weeks at the Deerfield Beach Fire Station, second floor, Hillsboro Boulevard at Federal Highway. Nominal fee for books and materials. Call 392-6230 or (954) 481-6639.

    Faculty recital: Clarinetist Paul Green and pianist Heather Coltman will perform music of Robert Schumann, Messager, Berg, Carl Maria von Weber, and Barroso (Cronicas, a work for clarinet and tape) at 7:30 p.m. at The Harid Conservatory, 2285 Potomac Rd., Boca Raton. Tickets are $10. Call 997-8846.

    Choral concert; The PBCC Concert Chorus will perform a musical world tour at 8 p.m. at the Duncan Theatre, PBCC, Lake Worth. Free. Call 439-8141.






    WEDNESDAY


    Blood pressure session: The Palm Beach County Cooperative Extension Service will offer education and information about high blood pressure from 10 a.m. to noon, or from 6 to 8 p.m., at the CES Family and Consumer Science building, 559 N. Military Tr., West Palm Beach. Free. Registration required; call 233-1742.

    Parenting Series: Psychotherapist Hermine Till will conduct a series of discussions at 11 a.m. each Wednesday in May at the North County Regional Library, 11303 Campus Dr., Palm Beach Gardens. Discipline will be considered today. Preregistration required; call 626-6133.

    Law Week: An Elder Law Clinic will be offered from 6 to 7 p.m., and a Consumer Law Clinic from 7:30 to 8:30 p.m., at the Central Library, 3650 Summit Blvd., West Palm Beach. Preregistration for the Palm Beach County Bar Association presentations requested; call 687-2800.

    Masterworks: Music of Mozart, Bruch and Bartok will be featured at 8 p.m. at the Kravis Center, West Palm Beach. Tickets for the Florida Philharmonic Orchestra program, with violinist Sergiu Schwartz, are $15 to $60. Call 832-SHOW or 930-1812.






    THURSDAY


    Handbell choir: The Children's Library of the Society of the Four Arts will present the Royal Pines Sixth- Grade Hand Bell Choir at 3:30 p.m. at the Rovensky building, second floor, 2 Four Arts Plaza, Palm Beach. Free. Call 655-7226.






    FRIDAY


    Law Day luncheon: Judge Joseph Wapner of The People's Court will address the Palm Beach County Bar Association at noon at the Sheraton Hotel, West Palm Beach. Cost of $30 for nonmembers. Call 687-2800.

    Village Players: Moss Hart's Light Up the Sky will be performed at 8 p.m. today and Saturday, and at 2:30 p.m. Sunday, at the North Palm Beach Community Center, 1200 Prosperity Farms Rd. Tickets for the comedy are $7. Call 622-5156.

    One-man show: Kevin McCarthy will star in Give 'em Hell Harry, recounting the life and accomplishments of President Harry S. Truman, at 8 p.m. at the Eissey Campus Theatre, PBCC, Palm Beach Gardens. Tickets are $15 or $18. Call 625-2345. A post-performance reception will be offered; tickets are $10.






    SATURDAY


    Sea Turtle sign-up: Registration and ticketing for evening sea turtle watches beginning May 20 will be offered at 9 a.m. at Gumbo Limbo Nature Center, Boca Raton. Restrictions apply. Cost of $3 per ticket. Call 338-1473.
