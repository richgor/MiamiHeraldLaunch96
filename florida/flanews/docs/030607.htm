Published Monday, April 29, 1996, in the Miami Herald.


FLORIDA 2B

Ex-Death Row inmate dies in wreck

Associated Press

LEESBURG -- A former Death Row inmate who twice came within hours of being executed has been killed in a car accident.

Jerry Chatman, 59, died early Saturday after his car hit a guardrail. He may have suffered a heart attack before the accident, police said.

A self-described ``born hustler,'' Chatman was convicted in 1960 of raping of a Fruitland Park woman and was sentenced to death. But two former Lake County deputies later testified that footprints supposedly taken at the scene by other deputies were fabricated.

In 1972, the U.S. Supreme Court abolished the death penalty across the country and a federal court reversed Chatman's conviction, ruling it was based on tainted evidence.

But two years later, an appellate court reinstated the conviction, arguing the deputies' testimony couldn't be believed.

A year later, Chatman was sentenced to 40 years in prison. He was released in 1981, after serving 21 years and 13 days. He had maintained his innocence until the end.

However, eight years later Chatman was sentenced to five years in prison for selling cocaine.
