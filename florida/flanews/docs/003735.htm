The bill for Spaziano case

Some of $100,000 expenses questioned

Herald Staff and Wire Reports

SANFORD -- Attorneys who kept Joseph ``Crazy Joe'' Spaziano from Florida's electric chair have asked Seminole County to pay $100,000 for their expenses.

The bulk of the charges were for expert witnesses and investigative costs, but some charges are raising eyebrows.

``I've never seen anything like this,'' Assistant County Attorney Susan Dietrich said.

Under Florida law, the county is required to reimburse reasonable expenses to Orlando attorney James Russ and the Holland & Knight law firm, the state's largest. Fees for expert witnesses, photocopies and deposition costs are routinely granted in many cases.

Russ and the firm defended Spaziano, a former Outlaw motorcycle gang member, for free. Spaziano, 50, won a new trial Jan. 22 after the star witness in his 1976 murder trial for the slaying of Orlando clerk Laura Harberts admitted to being forced to lie on the stand.

Prosecutors are appealing. Spaziano remains in prison on a 1975 rape conviction.

Tampa lawyer Gregg Thomas and Russ submitted a 2 1/4-inch-thick summary of bills on April 4. The case cost about $400,000 in lawyer time plus other costs that aren't included in the county bill, Thomas said.

Thomas headed a team of several lawyers and paralegals who worked with Russ, a well-known Orlando criminal attorney.

More than half the bill is for two expert witnesses a psychologist and an expert on hypnosis.

But the among expenditures that been most discussed is $24.15 in food and drinks for a defense investigator who went to a Casselberry topless bar. Russ said he believed that the investigator went to the bar because one of the key witnesses in the case was a topless dancer who had worked in the bar.

Thomas contends defense lawyers expected to bear some of the costs of representing Spaziano and asked for only those costs ``plainly necessary'' to prepare the case.

``We wanted to be as conservative as possible,'' Thomas said. ``My feeling is we incurred these costs. If the judge decides to award them, fine. If not, our firm will absorb them.''

Seminole County officials are studying which items should be paid and which are questionable.

Defense attorneys will meet with county lawyers in about a week to negotiate the bill. Any disputed items will be decided by Seminole Circuit Judge O.H. Eaton Jr. at a May 17 hearing.
