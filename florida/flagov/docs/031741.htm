Bill extending time to sue for medical errors passes

By KAREN BRANCH
Herald Staff Writer

TALLAHASSEE -- For Elaina Valdes, a House vote Friday meant parents like her will have a better chance to sue when their infants are the victim of medical mistakes.

For her 8-year-old son Tony -- inspiration for the bill -- it held a different meaning. His mother, who fought for two legislative sessions, won't have to come to Tallahassee any more.

``I want her to stay home,'' the West Palm Beach boy said. ``We'll go once more to Tallahassee -- for Gov. Chiles to sign the bill.''

That's what is expected to happen. And when it does, the plan (CS/S 454) will give parents in Florida twice as long to sue when their infants have been hurt by a medical mistake. If a mistake occurs when a child is younger than 4, parents will have until the child's 8th birthday to file suit.

Florida law now sets that deadline to sue at four years after the medical mistake -- no matter the patient's age. Most other states allow longer when the patient is an infant.

In the Valdes' case, they missed the deadline by 14 days.

When Tony was a baby, his doctor misdiagnosed him as a hermaphrodite -- and sewed his testicles to his abdominal wall. Years later, Tony's descended testicle developed normally. His mother sued, believing that disproved an earlier diagnosis that his other testicle -- which had melded to his abdominal wall -- was shrunk because of a birth defect and not physician's error.

But it was too late.

``This law really benefits children under the age of 4,'' said Rep. Carlos Lacasa, R-Miami, who sponsored the bill with Sen. Roberto Casas, R-Hialeah. ``Some injuries do not manifest themselves until the child is a little older.''

Elaina Valdes was clearly happy that her two-year fight is over. It ended as the House voted 112-4 on Friday. She and Tony applauded together as they watched from the House gallery.

``It's for the children,'' she said. ``It's not going to help Tony, but at least his injuries were not in vain.''
