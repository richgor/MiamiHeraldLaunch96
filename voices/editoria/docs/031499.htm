Expediency beats principle

METRO OFFICER `WINS' But a $180,000 settlement may cost Dade -- and its taxpayers -- a lot more.

Such strong-arm tactics, it seems, were the way to go. By a 6-5 vote on Tuesday, commissioners agreed to pay the disgraced officer $180,000 in exchange for his retiring from the department -- at the rank of division chief. Three commissioners -- Maurice Ferre, Alex Penelas, and Arthur Teele -- who all have a hankering to be the county's executive mayor apparently did not have the stomach to cast a vote in this distasteful issue. They left the dais before the vote, and returned only after it was taken. Mr. Ferre then cast the meaningless fifth No vote. Was their absence fortuitous timing, or a lack of political backbone to take a principled stand? As always, it looks bad.

Metro Police Chief Fred Taylor made the settlement recommendation. To his credit, he offers no defense of Mr. Bencomo. He acknowledges, rather, that getting this person off the force -- for a price -- was preferable to a protracted legal wrangle. Mr. Bencomo would have collected a paycheck all the while.

Still, when expediency wins out over principle, it sends a sorry message -- to the upstanding officers, to those of less integrity, and to the county residents who end up paying for them.
