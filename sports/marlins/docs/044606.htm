Published Thursday, May 2, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

S. Florida stars may slip past Marlins

By GREGG DOYEL
Herald Sports Writer

The Marlins are interested in two of the top South Florida prospects eligible for the June draft -- but wondering if either will be available.

LHP Eddie Yarnall, a Louisiana State junior from St. Thomas Aquinas, and INF Mike Caruso, a Douglas High senior, are projected to go somewhere between the second and fifth rounds. The Marlins won't go for either with the No. 9 overall pick, but they don't pick again until the fourth round. They sent their second- and third-round picks to Toronto in compensation for free agents Devon White and Al Leiter.

Contrary to recent reports, Caruso will not sign with the University of Miami. Caruso, who hit .483 this year, did not take a required geometry class in high school and learned of the mix-up too late. Ten major-league teams have visited his home, his father said.

Yarnall, the 1995 Cape Cod League Co-Pitcher of the Year, is 6-1 with a 2.48 ERA for LSU. He has been invited to try out for the U.S. Olympic team.
RHP project Rob Dibble is getting closer to an assignment or simulated-game action, pitching coach Larry Rothschild said. Dibble's arm strength and work habits have impressed the Marlins, and as soon as he overcomes a few bad mechanical habits, he will be game-ready.

The Marlins have not ruled out moving Dibble directly into the big leagues if he continues to progress, then impresses in some simulated games. However, a stint at Triple A Charlotte might be more likely.

RHP Dave Weathers, who clawed his way onto the team this spring, has clawed up the bullpen pecking order and now is sharing set-up duties with Terry Mathews. Weathers, once the long reliever (read mop-up man), has the bullpen's lowest ERA (2.25 in 11 outings).
RHP Matt Mantei was on the roster for the season's first 18 days because baseball rules dictated it. He stayed a little longer because of injuries. He is still here because he's earned it.


Only two active Marlins relievers (Robb Nen and Weathers) have a better ERA than Mantei's 3.38 in 10 2/3 innings. His 17 strikeouts rank fourth on the team, ahead of starters Chris Hammond (13 in 23 1/3 innings) and Pat Rapp (13 in 34). This from a guy whose spring ERA was near 8.00.

``Back then, there were times I felt I didn't belong,'' Mantei said. ``But those times would go away. And now they're gone completely. I feel comfortable here.''

  • SS Kurt Abbott, off to a solid .282 start (.462 slugging), has opened his stance, a la 1B Greg Colbrunn -- who lockers next to him. Abbott said the idea was his.

  • The U.S. Army's Jarret Behrens, 22, of Coral Springs, threw out the first pitch Wednesday. He returns to Bosnia on Monday.
    The mother of baseball star Gary Sheffield's 10-year-old daughter will receive less than a third of the $170,000 per year in child support payments she sought, a Pinellas County judge ruled Tuesday.

    Sheffield was ordered to pay Davene Williams $3,500 per month in child support, and to provide his daughter, Ebony, with private school tuition, a college fund and medical expenses.

    The package, which totals nearly $50,000 per year, is what Sheffield volunteered to pay last November.
