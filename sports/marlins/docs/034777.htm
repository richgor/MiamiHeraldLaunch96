Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Bullpen shuffle offers flexibility

By GREGG DOYEL
Herald Sports Writer

With the exception of closer Robb Nen, the Marlins' bullpen has been turned upside down because of injuries, demotions and good outings from unexpected sources.

Former starter Chris Hammond is the long man and probable spot starter, should such a situation arise. Former long man Dave Weathers has pitched his way into a set-up committee with Terry Mathews and lefty specialist Yorkis Perez -- neither of whom was expected to have that role when the season began. The set-up role became available when Alejandro Peña and Jay Powell went on the disabled list.

Former mop-up man Matt Mantei also has pitched his way into middle-inning appearances.

The result is a bullpen with few clearly defined roles, although pitching coach Larry Rothschild likes the flexibility.

``It's a matter of who's throwing well at a given moment,'' he said. ``We have a guy who's on a roll, he'll get certain innings over a guy who isn't.''

  • Assuming no complications from throwing in a simulated game Wednesday, Powell likely will be sent to Triple A Charlotte, perhaps as soon as Friday, for one or two rehab outings before being activated -- possibly by the end of the weekend or early next week. He had a strained rib cage muscle.

    The logical choice to be optioned to Charlotte would be Mantei, although he has the bullpen's second-lowest ERA and has earned a spot on a roster originally handed to him by Rule V guidelines.
    Mum's the word

    The Marlins were instructed Monday not to talk about ejections with the media, a directive players said came from President Don Smiley as a result of a pair of bizarre ejections -- third baseman Terry Pendleton, who insisted the umpires got the wrong man; and equipment manager Mike Wallace -- this weekend at Colorado.

    The Marlins were asked not to speak about ejections that happened this weekend, or ever.
    40-man flexibility

    By moving Peña to the 60-day DL last week, the Marlins opened a spot on their 40-man roster. They still haven't filled it, giving them several options, including using it to claim a player off waivers.

    The available 40-man spot also would allow the Marlins to promote any player in their system to the major leagues. They would have to give him the last remaining 40-man spot.

    The spot could be used for Charlotte heavy hitters such as OF Darrell Whitmore or C Jerry Brooks, or CF Marquis Riley or even P Rob Dibble -- who will throw batting practice this week, and then throw in a simulated game.

  • The Marlins have Peña under a two-year contract, but can buy out the second year -- worth $750,000 -- for $50,000. Even if Peña (torn rotator cuff) is physically unable to pitch next season, the Marlins would have to buy out the 1997 year on his contract.
    Lineup moves

    Marlins Manager Rene Lachemann tinkered with his lineup, moving C Charles Johnson from seventh to eighth. Also, Jeff Conine was moved to fifth.

    ``I'm pulling all kinds of tricks out of my hat,'' Lachemann deadpanned.

  • Alex Arias replaced Kurt Abbott (dizziness) in the lineup and hit seventh.
