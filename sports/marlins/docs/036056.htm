Published Friday, April 26, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Infielders a big hit at Triple A

By GREGG DOYEL
Herald Sports Writer


The Marlins have one of the best double-play combinations in the minors at Triple A Charlotte, home to flashy shortstop Edgar Renteria and smooth second baseman Ralph Milliard.

The Marlins expected that. The hitting has been a bonus.

After a horrendous start, Milliard is hitting .342 with a home run. Renteria is hitting .324 with two
home runs and five doubles.

``Those two have really hit the ball with authority,'' said John Boles, Marlins vice president of player development. ``Everyone knows about their defense. Now maybe people know about their offense.''

What a relief for the Marlins. Neither Renteria nor Milliard hit above .095 this spring in major-league camp before being sent down.

Livan Hernandez, 0-3 with a 12.08 ERA and 11 walks in 12 2/3 innings at Charlotte, is proving to be more human than hero. The Marlins hope you will bear with him.

``When I see Livan I see a 21-year-old kid with a great arm but needing development,'' Boles said. ``The key is patience. It's hard for him, being under a microscope.

``He needs to get better command of his pitches. He's pitching behind to a lot of batters.''

The Marlins were player-friendly with their treatment of Charlotte closer Mario Brito. They could have kept Brito, who had four saves and a 1.80 ERA when they sold him earlier this week to a Japanese club, as insurance in case of catastrophe to their major-league bullpen. Or they could have dealt him to another club's Triple A team for a prospect or cash.

Instead, they asked if he would like to be sold to the Tokyo Giants, where he could make a six-figure salary. Brito accepted, replacing struggling American Jeff Manto. Everyone's happy. And players around the league undoubtedly will remember.

Brito's last act at Charlotte, by the way, was winning a game with a three-run home run.

  • The Marlins replaced Brito with reliever Euclides Rojas. With road roommates Hernandez and Rojas, Charlotte now leads the International League in Cuban defectors.

    Charlotte also activated 1993 Organization Pitcher of the Year Vic Darensbourg and is trying to acquire another reliever.

  • INF Chris Clapinski is writing a season-long diary for USA Today Baseball Weekly.

  • Single A Brevard County: C Matt Erwin (.379, 5 2Bs), RHP Mike Parisi (3-0, 2.36 ERA), Australian OF Glen Reeves (.339).

  • Single A Kane County: RHP Vic Hurtado (2-0, 2.16), 2B Amaury Garcia (.333, 7 extra-base hits, 5 steals), 3B Josh Booty (.164, 8 RBI), OF Jaime Jones (.179, 2 HRs), 1B Nate Rolison (.317, 3 HRs).

  • Double A Portland: OF Pookie Wilson (.350), 3B Dave Berg (.298), OF Chris Sheff (.321, eight extra-base hits).

  • Triple A Charlotte: OF Darrell Whitmore (.351, 3 HRs).
