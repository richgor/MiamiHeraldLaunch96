Published Friday, May 3, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Dawson in court in Denver today

By GREGG DOYEL
Herald Sports Writer

Andre Dawson will plead not guilty today in a Denver courtroom on charges he disturbed the peace in a 1995 incident in which a hotel valet claimed Dawson shoved him.

Dawson said he expects the valet to file a civil suit if the criminal suit ends in Dawson's being found guilty. He rejected a plea bargain.

``If I felt I was guilty, I could have done something about it,'' said Dawson, who likely would face no more than a fine if convicted. ``[But] it can be easy for a prosecutor to sway a jury on a disturbing the peace charge.''

Dawson expects the case to end today, but fears it could go well into the afternoon. He will be in court at 8 a.m.

``It's going to take a while,'' Dawson said.

The Marlins squashed rumors out of Cincinnati that the Reds were trying to trade left-hander Pete Schourek for Marlins right-hander John Burkett, saying the teams had had no contact.

LHP Al Leiter said he meant no disrespect when he grunted after striking out Phillies slugger Pete Incaviglia Wednesday night. ``I grunt after every pitch,'' Leiter said. ``I definitely didn't show him up. He can think that.''

He does. Incaviglia discoursed on the subject for some time after the game -- after blasting a 482-foot homer off Leiter. ``I was raised to show respect,'' Incaviglia said. ``I wasn't shown respect.''

  • When Marlins pitching coach Larry Rothschild called to get the bullpen warmed up Wednesday night, he found the phone disconnected. Bullpen coach Joe Breeden was summoned to sprint the request to warm up Dave Weathers from the dugout to the bullpen.

  • LF Jeff Conine's visit to an ophthalmologist Thursday revealed nothing new. He will take antibiotics and switch contacts to counter irritation.

  • RHP Matt Mantei (3.38 ERA, 17 strikeouts, 5 hits allowed in 10 2/3 innings) is working his way toward appearing in pressure situations, Rothschild said.
