Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Mourning undergoes surgery on finger

Heat center Alonzo Mourning underwent outpatient surgery Monday to repair a chronic dislocation problem on the ring finger of his left hand.

Mourning had a damaged ligament in the finger that caused it to dislocate several times, most recently during a playoff game against Chicago.

Mourning's finger will be in a splint for about three weeks, at which time he will begin rehabilitating. A full recovery is expected.

Mourning is a free agent but has said he likely will remain with the Heat. Because of salary cap reasons, the Heat likely will not re-sign Mourning until after it has formulated most of the rest of its roster -- probably in August.
BARRY JACKSON
