Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Tickets for Game 6 on sale today (10 a.m.)

By DAVID J. NEAL
Herald Sports Writer

Tickets for Game 6, scheduled for 7:35 p.m. Tuesday at Miami Arena, go on sale at 10 a.m. today at the Miami Arena box office and through Ticketmaster (358-5885 in Dade, 954-523-3309 in Broward and 407-966-3309 in Palm Beach).

About 3,000 seats are available -- Panther Pack ($15), goal zone ($30), sideline balcony ($34) and mezzanine ($40).
Sheppard scratched

The Panthers' leading playoff scorer, right wing Ray Sheppard, missed Game 4 with a bruised shoulder. Sheppard should be ready for Sunday's Game 5 in Philadelphia.

Also scratched for Game 4 was defenseman Gord Murphy, who played his first game in three weeks Tuesday.
Fans quiet at start

The Miami Arena crowd, obnoxious past a fault Tuesday, began Thursday much tamer.

The national anthem was not only audible, but fewer toy rats hit the ice and no golf balls, as was the case during Thursday's rendition.
Lindsay plays hurt

The right eye of Panthers left wing Bill Lindsay was barely open, but provided enough vision for him to play Thursday night.
Svehla breaks nose

The Panthers lost defenseman Robert Svehla to a broken nose suffered 2:23 into the game. As Svehla dived for a lost puck, the skate of Philadelphia center Eric Lindros unintentionally came back and caught him. Svehla also suffered a cut lip and lost some teeth.

  • Philadelphia sat tough guy left wing Shawn Antoski as Rob DiMaio returned to the lineup. DiMaio missed the last eight games of the season and the first nine Flyers playoff games with a sprained right knee.

  • In a strange if justifiable first in Florida's brief history, the Panthers and Flyers had to play the last 3.6 seconds of the first period before they could start the second.

    The Panthers' Brian Skrudland flipped the puck over the glass with 3.6 seconds left, but the clock kept running. The Flyers, a great face-off team, protested vehemently as the draw would have been in the Panthers' end. Skrudland tied up Lindros on the face-off and time expired.
