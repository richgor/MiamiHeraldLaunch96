Published Saturday, May 4, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Sheppard is fine: It was just the flu

By DAVID J. NEAL
Herald Sports Writer

PHILADELPHIA -- Panthers right wing Ray Sheppard denied his absence from Wednesday's practice and Thursday's morning skate had anything to do with a rapid heartbeat. Sheppard said he just felt dehydrated from the flu.

``I feel fine now, 100 percent,'' Sheppard said jovially. ``I can't complain. Life is good.''

Sheppard, Tom Fitzgerald and Johan Gar
penlov sat out an optional skate Friday.

``I said to them, `You know your bodies better than I do,' '' Panthers Coach Doug MacLean said.
Once a team gets a label around the league, it's harder to shake than a Sutter in a corner. The City of Philadelphia spent Friday moaning about the neutral-zone trap, which the Panthers are accused of playing whether they win games 6-2 or 2-0.

``We didn't play the trap once that I saw,'' MacLean said. ``If we played it, I didn't notice it.''
When Flyers center Eric Lindros talked with his brother Brett on Wednesday, this series was one thing they didn't discuss. Brett, the No. 9 overall pick in the 1994 draft, was forced to retire this week after suffering 10 concussions over four seasons.

``That would be bruising his heart,'' Eric said of talking about the playoffs. ``I think he wants to stay away from the game for a while.''
Flyers Coach Terry Murray saw late in the second period Thursday that Lindros, John LeClair and Dale Hawerchuk were going nowhere fast. So he moved Mikael Renberg back to the top line in an unsuccessful attempt to juice up the offense.

It didn't work. The reunited Legion of Doom had zero shots in the third period.

Hawerchuk was back with Lindros and LeClair in practice Friday. Renberg, still nursing a sore pelvic tendon, skipped practice. Murray said Hawerchuk likely will start Saturday night's game with Lindros and LeClair.

Herald wire services contributed to this report.
