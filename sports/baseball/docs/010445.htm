Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

A's Young hits 3 HRs; Pavlik moves to 5-0

OAKLAND, Calif. -- Ernie Young hit three home runs and Geronimo Berroa and Brent Gates had one apiece as the Athletics routed the Minnesota Twins, 15-5, Friday night in Oakland.

Young was 4-for-5 with six RBI and four runs scored, helping the A's to season-highs in hits (18) and runs.

Young connected off Frank Rodriguez (2-4) to start the first and hit his third of the season, a two-run shot off Greg Hansell, during a five-run fifth that included three homers, tying Oakland's team record.

He hit his third homer, a two-run shot off Latroy Hawkins, in the seventh as part of the A's second five-run inning of the night.

The three-homer night by Young was his first ever and the first for an Oakland player since Mark McGwire did it at Boston last June 11.

  • Rangers 6, Tigers 2
    TIGERS' WOES CONTINUE

    ARLINGTON, Texas -- Roger Pavlik allowed hits to six of the first 12 batters he faced, but settled down and stayed unbeaten.

    Pavlik (5-0) allowed two runs and nine hits in 6 2/3 innings, struck out four and walked two.

    The Rangers have won seven of their last nine games. Detroit lost for the 19th time in 22 games.

    Jose Lima (0-3) gave up five runs and seven hits in six-plus innings for the Tigers, who lowered their team ERA from 7.12 to 7.11.

    ``Right now we have to play the perfect game and it's not happening,'' Tigers Manager Buddy Bell said.

  • White Sox 5, Yankees 2
    TAPANI GETS WIN

    CHICAGO -- With David Cone out, possibly for the season, the Yankees said the rest of the team would have to step up. It didn't happen Friday night.

    Kevin Tapani (3-2) combined with two relievers on a three-hitter to lead the host Chicago White Sox over New York, 5-2. It was the Yankees' third loss in their last 11 games.

    Andy Pettitte (5-2) lasted just four innings, giving up five runs -- three earned -- and five hits. He walked four.

    Tapani allowed two hits in seven innings with four strikeouts and four walks.

  • Orioles 10, Brewers 7
    ALOMAR 4-FOR-6

    MILWAUKEE -- Jeffrey Hammonds started a three-run rally in the 12th inning with an RBI single off Ramon Garcia, and Baltimore, which had blown two-run leads in the ninth and 10th, held on for the victory.

    Roberto Alomar, who went 4-for-6, added an RBI single off Garcia (0-1), who entered the game in the 10th, and Rafael Palmeiro added another RBI single off Kevin Wickander.

    Jesse Orosco (2-0) got the victory with 1 2/3 innings of scoreless relief and Roger McDowell pitched the 12th for his first save. Garcia (0-1) gave up three earned runs and five hits in 1 2/3.

  • Red Sox 6, Blue Jays 5
    O'LEARY HR WINS IT IN 11TH

    TORONTO -- John Valentin tied the game with a two-out homer in the ninth and Troy O'Leary homered in the 11th to lift Boston.

    O'Leary's fifth homer of the season, a drive into the second deck in right off Giovanni Carrara (0-1), gave Boston its sixth win in eight games.

    Heathcliff Slocumb (1-1) pitched 1 2/3 innings and struck out three. He combined with Tom Gordon and Mike Stanton to strike out 17. Gordon struck out 12 in seven innings, allowing four runs and six hits.

    Ed Sprague hit his 10th homer of the season, off Stanton in the eighth to give Toronto a 5-4 lead, but Mike Timlin blew a save chance for the first time in six chances this year when he allowed Valentin's homer.

    Domingo Cedeno had the first four-hit game of his career, going four for five with two RBI.
    Late Thursday

  • Blue Jays 5, Rangers 2: At Arlington, Texas, John Olerud's two-run double in the sixth inning broke up a scoreless game and snapped Ken Hill's string of 19 shutout innings as Toronto halted Texas' five-game winning streak.

  • Royals 8, Angels 2: At Anaheim, Calif., rookie Sal Fasano hit his first major-league homer, a three-run shot in the fifth inning, lifting Kansas City to its fifth straight victory.

  • Orioles 6, White Sox 4: At Chicago, Cal Ripken, in an 0-for-16 slump, blooped a bases-loaded single to key a five-run first inning that boosted Baltimore.
