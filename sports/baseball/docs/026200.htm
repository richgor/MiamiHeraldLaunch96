BASEBALL TODAY

BASEBALL TODAY

  • TUESDAY'S STARS: Mark McGwire hit a three-run homer into the scaffolding in left-center as the Oakland Athletics won their second straight against Cleveland, defeating the Indians, 8-4. . . . Alex Fernandez (5-2) gave up five hits and walked three in eight innings to become the AL's third five-game winner and help the Chicago White Sox beat the Baltimore Orioles, 3-2.

  • STATS: Alex Fernandez of the Chicago White Sox is 9-1 with a 1.33 ERA (13 earned runs in 88 innings) in games he starts when the temperature is under 50 degrees. . . . Wade Boggs of the New York Yankees had his 200th three-hit game on Tuesday night against Detroit.

  • SLUMPS: Jim Abbott is winless in nine home starts since rejoining the California Angels last July 2

  • SURPRISES: Detroit's Alan Trammell, who has played in 2,250 games, made his first appearance as a starting second baseman on Tuesday night.

  • SHUTOUTS: Jose Parra pitched six innings and combined with three relieverson a six-hitter as the Minnesota Twins beat the Seattle Mariners, 2-0, Tuesday night for their first shutout since last Aug. 29.

  • STOPPERS: Dennis Eckersley of the St. Louis Cardinals hasn't walked a batter in 15 2/3 innings this season.

  • STRIKEOUTS: Boston's Roger Clemens has struck out 10 or more batters inthree consecutive games for the first time since 1987.

  • SWINGS: Montreal drew 13,236 to Olympic Stadium on Tuesday night, down from 46,893 on Monday when there was a bargain night promotion.

  • STEALS: Cleveland's Kenny Lofton leads the majors with 22 stolen bases. He's been caught stealing six times.

  • SIDELINED: Texas Rangers outfielder Juan Gonzalez left Tuesday night's game against the Toronto Blue Jays with a strained left quadriceps. Gonzalez pulled up lame while running out a ground ball in the second inning.

  • SCOUTING: Jose Canseco went 1-for-4 and Kevin Mitchell went 0-for-4 as they began their rehabilitation assignments Tuesday night with the Pawtucket Red Sox.
