Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Cone's surgery goes well; recovery time uncertain

From Herald Wire Services

David Cone had surgery Friday to repair an aneurysm in his right shoulder, but the New York Yankees wouldn't predict whether their ace will be able to pitch again this season.

But before the Yankees' game against the White Sox Friday night, Manager Joe Torre told his players that Cone might not be back until next year.

Doctors took a one-inch vein from Cone's upper left thigh and used it to repair two arteries in his right shoulder during the three-hour operation at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center, about three miles from Yankee Stadium.

Both doctors involved in the operation said Cone should be able to pitch again, but that they couldn't be more specific.

``We are optimistic, and knowing David, his return will probably be sooner rather than later,'' said Dr. Stuart Hershon, the Yankees team physician.

Doctors not involved have said that based on the recovery time of other pitchers who have had this type of surgery, it is unlikely that Cone would be able to pitch before late this season at the earliest.

Steve Soderstrom, currently pitching for Triple-A Phoenix, and Kevin Rogers, pitching in Pittsburgh's extended spring training, had similar operations in 1994 that ended their seasons.

Cone is expected to be hospitalized until the middle of next week.
