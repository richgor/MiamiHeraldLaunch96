Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

3 of a kind

Bulls' Jordan, Rodman, Pippen make 1st team

From Herald Wire Services

No wonder they won 72 regular-season games. For the first time in 13 years, three members of the same team -- Michael Jordan, Scottie Pippen and Dennis Rodman of the Chicago Bulls -- were named to the NBA's all-defensive team.

``It's what made us a good team this year even though our offense led the league,'' Coach Phil Jackson said. ``We got a lot of offense off our defense.''

They were joined on the first team by Seattle's Gary Payton and San Antonio's David Robinson. Payton earlier was selected the league's defensive player of the year.

Pippen received 55 of a possible 56 points in voting by 29 NBA coaches, who were not allowed to vote for their own players. Jordan received 53 points, Payton 48, Robinson 42 and Rodman 30.

Only one member of the Heat received points: Alonzo Mourning, with four.

All five first-teamers have made the team before. It is the seventh selection for Jordan and Rodman, fifth for Pippen, fourth for Robinson and third for Payton.
