Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

SEMIFINAL PREVIEW

EASTERN CONFERENCE
Magic (2)-Hawks (6)

  • Game 1: 8 p.m. Wednesday in Orlando (TNT).

    The Hawks were competitive with the Magic, winning the two games in Atlanta (124-91 and 96-84), but losing twice in Orlando (108-95 and 119-104). But the victories came with Andrew Lang at center, and the losses occurred with Christian Laettner at center. Laettner and Sean Rooks, acquired from Minnesota for Lang and Spud Webb, must be physical against Shaquille O'Neal, who averaged 22.3 points and 13.3 rebounds vs. Atlanta this year. Atlanta's success usually has ridden on the perimeter shooting of Mookie Blaylock and Steve Smith. If they're hitting their three-pointers, the series could be longer than most expect.

  • Prediction: Magic in five.

  • Injuries: None.
    WESTERN CONFERENCE
    Spurs (2)-Jazz (3)

  • Game 1: 7 tonight in San Antonio (TNT).

    San Antonio won three of four in the season series, and swept both meetings in Salt Lake City for the first time. But the Jazz did take one game in San Antonio (114-111 in overtime) and has a legitimate chance to pull an upset. Two keys for Utah: Can Jazz centers Felton Spencer and Greg Ostertag contain -- or at least pester -- David Robinson, who averaged 23.8 points against the Jazz this season? And can Utah's backcourt of John Stockton and Jeff Hornacek outplay the Spurs' underappreciated starting guards -- Avery Johnson and Vinny Del Negro?

  • Prediction: Spurs in six.

  • Injuries: Spurs -- G Doc Rivers (back spasms) is doubtful for Game 1. Jazz -- F David Benoit (knee) is questionable for Game 1.
    BARRY JACKSON
