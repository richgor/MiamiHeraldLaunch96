Borders become wider, give walls a finishing touch

Herald Home Furnishings Writer

Wallpaper borders are growing, to as wide as 12 to 20 inches.

``Big borders'' can give a room a regal flair. When paired with a smaller border, they can create a custom look. Used in place of crown moldings, big borders put a finishing touch on a wall.

Two of the newer collections of big borders are titled Maximus and Serenity, produced by Gloria Merrill Enterprises, a Long Island wall-covering company.

The theme of these large borders is drawn from the tropical rain forests of the Amazon, the exotic character of the Orient and the majestic beauty of Africa's wild animals.

Easy to hang, many borders are prepasted and strippable for no-fuss application and removal. Even the most novice do-it-yourselfer can be artistic with borders.

Here are some ideas for creating beautiful rooms with borders:

  • Use borders just below a ceiling instead of a crown molding.

  • Frame doors and windows for architectural detailing.

  • Create a chair rail by running borders around a room at waist level. For a wainscoting effect, hang coordinating wall coverings above and below the border.

  • Use oversize borders to ``hide'' unattractive soffits.

  • Create a headboard effect on a wall above a bed.

  • Focus interest on a fireplace with a border placed above the mantel or around the perimeter.

  • Decorate toy boxes, hampers and chests of drawers with borders.

  • ``Panel'' a door with borders.

  • Frame all four walls in borders for a picture-perfect room.

  • Glue borders to staircase risers, then coat with polyurethane.

  • Make coordinating book jackets with oversize borders.
