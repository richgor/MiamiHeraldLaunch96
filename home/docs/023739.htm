Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Artsy tiles: whimsical and unique

By JO WERNE
Herald Home Furnishings Writer

Terry Kaplan's career in ceramics began 18 years ago when she made jewelry. Six years ago, she ventured into art tile.

Katrina Wolf used to be a quilter, ``but that's not as economically viable as tile.'' Now she makes tile in quilt designs.

Kaplan, founder of Terry Tiles in Miami, and Wolf, owner of the Blue Moon Ceramic Tile Studio in Hollywood, were among a dozen or more artisans who exhibited their one-of-a-kind tiles at the recent International Tile & Stone Exposition at the Miami Beach Convention Center.

After looking at miles of practical floor and wall tiles, it was refreshing to see the whimsical tiles that evolve from an artist's imagination.

Kaplan's popular designs include ``The Key Largo Series,'' a pastel-colored design of beach activities, and ``Stars & Stripes,'' a vibrant primary-colored, whimsical design. Her ``Coffee Cup'' wall hanging is a trio of wildly colored, oversize cups, while her door knobs ($18 to $24) would add pizzazz to any kitchen cabinet. (Kaplan's tiles are distributed by Sunny McLean & Co. in the Miami Art and Design Village.)

Wolf, who distributes her own tile, does custom work that ranges from $4 to $84 per tile. Besides her tile motifs -- ``You hang them on the wall and never have to launder them'' -- she makes tiles in mola patterns after the colorful appliques done by the Cuna Indians in the San Blas Islands of Panama.

Surving Studios of Middletown, N.Y., a regular exhibitor at the tile show, showed finely crafted three-dimensional ``critters'' -- lizards, salamanders, sea turtles, tree frogs, geckos and snakes -- that make interesting borders and inserts for bathrooms and fountains. This year, the studio offered something for hot tub enclosures and bachelor baths -- female nudes that cost $100 each.

Shari Hatchett Bohlmann of SharDan International in Clackamas, Ore., is known as a romance artist who paints charming ``Rooms With a View'' scenes from around the world. Now she's laminating her paintings on a lightweight poly tile that can be hung as a painting or fashioned into a table top. A 3-foot-by-3-foot framed tile picture costs $840.

Sylvain Bongard, who lives and works in Portugal, assembles tiles into wall hangings, paints scenes on them, then dismantles them for firing in the kiln. A large wall plaque that could be used as a headboard costs $9,000. Bongard is represented by Rhonda Gelfman Szabo of North Miami Beach.
