Published Sunday, April 28, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Moon, Star glass featured in article

By ANITA GOLD
Knight-Ridder News Service

We have a collection of 29 pieces of old Moon and Star pattern glass, all in perfect condition, which belonged to our 96-year-old mother.

Pieces include two sizes of compotes with lids, spoon holders, cake dish, etc. Where can we find a buyer? We also have a table lamp in perfect condition, designed with a figure of Ann Morrow Lindbergh standing on top of the world with a full parachute with star cutouts. How can we learn more about it?

An article titled ``Heavenly Glass Collectible Moon and Star'' can be found in the February/ March issue of Glass Collector's Digest. The issue is available for $4.50 from Glass Collector's Digest, P.O. Box 553, Marietta, Ohio 45750-0553.

Also available is the definitive book on the subject, Mysteries of the Moon & Star by George & Linda Breeze. It pictures more than 200 pieces in color along with their prices, and is available for $33.50 postpaid from George Breeze, 4207 Fox Creek Dr., Mt. Vernon, Ill. 62864.

To check out or sell your Lindbergh lamp write the C.A.L./ N-X-211 (Charles Lindbergh) Collectors Society, 727 Youn-Kin Parkway, South Columbus, Ohio 43207-4788. Send $15 for an annual club membership and monthly newsletter.

I have a collection of 2,600 mice. I would love to hear about other mice collections. Or can you tell me if there's a mouse collectors club?

There are no mouse collectors clubs that I know of. Recently the Little Cheesers Collectors' Club for Little Cheeser mice figurines in North Tonawanda, N.Y., shut down its club and newsletter operation.

Where can I find information on tax deductions for donated antiques, and how much is allowed for such pieces? Also, how does one go about finding the proper places that accept such donations?

How to Speak Furniture With An Antique English Accent for $14.95, How to Speak Furniture With An Antique French Accent for $14.95, How to Speak Furniture With An Antique Victorian Accent for $12.95, and How To Speak Furniture With An Antique American Accent for $12.95 -- plus $4 postage for each book. If all five books are ordered, shipping is free along with a 10 percent book discount.

Where can I find information on certain Dept. 56 collector pieces?

Write The Village Press, the Oldest Publication for Dept. 56 Collectors, P.O. Box 556, Rockford, Ill. 61105-0556. Enclose $24 for a six-issue subscription, or $48 for 13 issues, or send $5 for a single copy of the publication.

I have a collection of nearly 200 pieces of milk glass. Where can I sell the collection as a whole or as individual pieces?

Write the National Milk Glass Collectors, 1113 Birchwood Dr., Garland, Texas 75043; enclose a self-addressed stamped envelope for a reply or membership information.

Is there a club for collectors of Czechoslovakian glass and china items?

Write the Czechoslovakian Collectors Guild International, P.O. Box 901395, Kansas City, Mo. 64190. Enclose a self-addressed stamped envelope for membership information.

Write Anita Gold, P.O. Box 597401, Chicago, Ill. 60659. Enclose a self-addressed, stamped envelope. Not all letters can be answered. Priority will be given to those that include a copy of the column.
