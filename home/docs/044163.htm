Published Sunday, April 28, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Some heirs use Sotheby's, some a garage sale

By JUDITH UNGAR
New York Times Service

Last week Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis' children sold more than 4,000 of her meticulously cataloged personal belongings at a four-day auction at Sotheby's.

When my children dispose of my things, it will probably be at a garage sale. But that doesn't mean my stuff doesn't have provenance, too.

  • Jackie: A Louis XVI ormolu-mounted mahogany bureau plat and cartonnier, signed E. Levasseur, J.M.E. (used by President Kennedy to sign the 1963 Nuclear Test Ban Treaty).

  • Me: Dining set (an engagement present from Grandpa Weiner to my parents in 1939, used for birthdays, seders and art and science projects). Bonus: a Johnson Wax tin, circa 1951 (open it to evoke my childhood).

  • Jackie: A Louis XV-style gilded and silver-plated three-light bouillotte lamp (among the furnishings in the White House and previously the Kennedys' Georgetown house).

  • Me: Matched pair of miniature lamps made of Popsicle sticks by a retired San Antonio man.

  • Jackie: A collection of miscellaneous woven baskets (about 14 pieces).

  • Me: A tightly coiled basket of Nairobi telephone wire; a bowl my husband and I watched a man decorate in Morocco by dipping a fingertip into black paint; a spiral of iridescent metal spikes from a nail gun, from a roofing job we put off till a raccoon stared at us from a ceiling hole.

  • Jackie: John Singer Sargent (Head of an Arab ); John Wootton (Lord Bateman's Arabian ); Robert Rauschenberg (Drawing for President of the U.S.A. With Dante ).

  • Me: Children, grandchildren and others (``Eat American,'' poster of a slice of chocolate layer cake and a can of diet soda; red-and-white wooden replica of classic Burma Shave sign, saying: ``Don't lose your head to gain a minute. You need your head, your brains are in it'').

  • Jackie: The Lesotho III diamond, Harry Winston (40.42-carat engagement ring from Aristotle Onassis).

  • Me: Plain gold band Wedding Ring II. (Ring I slithered off finger at Darlington Lake in New Jersey in 1967; still at silty bottom. I asked my husband to replace it. His reply: ``One ring per wife.'' I bought this one.)

  • Jackie: Simulated pearl necklace (triple strand of ``pearls'' with silver Art Deco-style clasp set with single rows of ``diamonds'').

  • Me: Add-a-Pearl necklace, with 52 tiny real pearls (also five yellow cards with pearls for adding).

  • Jackie: A Louis XV gilt-tooled red Morocco leather small casket, circa 1770 (owned by Marie Antoinette).

  • Me: Corona typewriter with wooden return bar, basic black ribbon, circa 1930 (owned by Daddy).

  • Jackie: A Chinese cloisonne enamel bird-form box.

  • Me: Wheaties box with Michael Jordan.

  • Jackie: Thank-yous from Robert Lowell, Truman Capote and Norman Mailer, inscribed in their books.

  • Me: I've thrown out thank-you notes so that I won't appear to be saving mail that makes me look good.

  • Jackie: A Victorian mahogany youth chair, probably French, second half of 19th Century (J.F.K. Jr.'s high chair in the White House); a set of Ben Hogan Power Thrust irons (with the bag inscribed ``J.F.K. Washington, D.C.''); three leather hunting saddles; gold-and-black enamel lighter, Dupont, Paris.

  • Me: The Square Egg Machine, a screw-top device for shaping warm boiled eggs into cold squares. Contents of top buffet drawer: 93 yarmulkes (velvet, suede, Bukharan, imprinted, crocheted); unopened cigarette pack (souvenir from an Armenian restaurant in Moscow); 31 incense sticks from India, last burned when my book group discussed A Passage to India.

  • Jackie: A 1992 BMW 325i four-door sedan (dark green metallic with tan interior, with indicated mileage of 10,032 miles believed to be correct).

  • Me: 1988 Nissan Sentra four-door compact (rusted gray with splotchy interior and New York City tow-pound marking on front passenger window, with 140,014 miles). No key needed to open trunk; just insert finger and wiggle.
