Published Sunday, April 28, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Merged companies to market doors

Jesus Martinez, founder of Glasco Industries, and Enrique ``Leo'' Pino, owner of Pino Windows, have merged their companies to form Vidco Industries.

They are specializing in selling DorAlum, a new line of South Florida Building Code-approved aluminum French doors, sidelights and picture windows. The partners also have the exclusive South Florida dealership for Work Right bathroom products, including tub and shower enclosures and swinging shower doors.

Vidco Industries' showroom and manufacturing plant are at 7040 NW 77th Ter., Miami. Hours are 7:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Monday through Friday, 8 a.m. to noon Saturday. For information, call 888-0077.

Roach population wins free execution

Would you trade your favorite cockroach for a free home treatment plus $500?

Combat is doing its annual search for America's five most roach-infested homes. Combat field scientist Austin Frishman will conduct roach-severity tests in each winner's home and treat the infestations.

People who have seemingly unsolveable roach problems and live in single-family residences can send a one-page descriptions to Combat, 55 Union St., San Francisco, Calif. 94111. Include name, address and phone number. Entries must be postmarked by May 6.

Art exhibition opens at gallery

Profiles Gallery at Puente & Pila Associates in Design will host a two-month exhibition by artist Sonia Joras beginning Friday.

Titled ``Symbolic Collages,'' the art pieces are based on the ideas of the human ecology movement.

A reception from 6 to 9 p.m. Friday links the opening with Coral Gables Gallery Night.

Profiles Gallery, 244 Valencia Ave., Coral Gables, is open from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday through Friday.
