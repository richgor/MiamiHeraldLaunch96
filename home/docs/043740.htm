Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

COMPOSING: PLS USE THIS VERSION. THX

Plants with colorful foliage can take full sun, others cannot. Horticultural consultant Derek Burch provided lists of plants suitable for sun or shade for the 1996 Color Conference. His suggestions are these:
SUN

  • Ground covers, low-growing

    Alternanthera, Joseph's Coat and others

    Artemisia, finely textured plant of silver-gray

    Caladium, tropical bulbs producing colorful, showy leaves

    Celosia, an annual with bright cockscomb-like flowers, short-lived

    Coleus, produced deeper colors in sun, fades in shade

    Liriope, some cultivars have variegated leaves

    Ocimum, purple-leaf basil

    Plectranthus, related to coleus; Oregano de Bruja likes dry spots

    Rhoeo, oyster plant

    Zebrina, wandering Jew; can also grow well beneath trees

  • Shrubs, specimen plants

    Acalypha, Copperleaf; other cultivars have varying colorations

    Agave, A. angustifolia is variegated; A. marginata has white leaf edges

    Canna, some have variegated leaves, or red undersides

    Codiaeum, croton, select from those intended for sun

    Heliconia, a few have red leaves, red veins or red undersides

    Musa, ornamental bananas may have same leaf coloring as heliconias

    Pittosporum, green/white variegation

    Schefflera arvoricola, green/yellow variegation

  • Trees

    Aleurites moluccana, silvery undersides of leaves

    Conocarpus erectus sericus, silver buttonwood, a native

    Erythrina variegata var. orientalis, powerful variegation

    Flacourtia, one patented, variegated form
    SHADE

  • Ground covers, low-growing

    Caladium, some for sun also available

    Calathea, peacock plants and relatives

    Fittonia, nerve plant, with pink or white veins

    Hypoestes, polka-dot plant, freckle-face, green and pink

    Kaempferia, peacock or dwarf ginger lily, dormant in winter

    Pilea, aluminum plant, P. cadieri, Burgundy and silver

    Pseuderanthemum and relatives, chocolate plant is one

    Sansevieria, green and cream

  • Shrubs, specimens

    Alpinia, green and white ginger

    Begonia, many have flowers in all but deep shade

    Bromeliads, wide range of colors

    Cordyline, ti plants

    Costus, variegated form or Burgundy leaves

    Codiaeum, croton, Norma and Petra among others

    Dieffenbachia, dumbcane

    Dracaena, corn plant and relatives with yellow, red stripes or edges

    Ferns, Pteris has white variegated forms

    Graptophyllum, caricature plant, reddish-purple, whitish

    Heliconia, reddish undersides, red veins

    Hoffmania, taffeta plant

    Sanchezia speciosa, cultivar Ellen is smaller form

    Zingiber, gingers
