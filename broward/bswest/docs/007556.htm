Grand Palms women get acquainted at lunch club

By ANNMARIE DODD
Herald Staff Writer

T
he Grand Palms ladies who lunch often play a game of bring 'n' brag as an icebreaker.

Doris Sobel organizes the community's luncheon club, a group that gathers about once a month to get a little culture lesson, to meet friends and to enjoy lunch. The club will meet at noon Friday at Fran's, the country club restaurant at 110 Grand Palms Dr. in Pembroke Pines.

``This meeting provides a good opportunity to get to know each other in a more meaningful and personal way,'' Sobel said.

Those attending are asked to bring a homemade handicraft or tell an interesting story or a good joke.

Grand Palms is a collection of 800 homes off Pines Boulevard just west of Interstate 75.

Newcomers and Grand Palms residents interested in joining the group can call Sobel at 433-3099.
Several Grand Palms residents have recently been named to the board of directors of the Grand Palms Community Association.

Homeowners joining developer Pat Segall on the board include: Ron Martin, a local accountant; Stan Lesser, president of Grand Palms Advisory Board; Allen Entin, a local lawyer who'll advise the board on legal issues; Peter Gullo, past president of Grand Palms Advisory Board; and Sy Gothlef, chairman of the homeowner board's finance committee.

The group discusses planning issues, area improvements and some business affairs at Grand Palms Golf & Country Club. For details, call the development's executive office at 437-8664.

  • The Chapel Trail Owners Association will meet at 7:30 p.m. Wednesday at Rose G. Price Park, 901 NW 208th Ave., Pembroke Pines. This is an umbrella group that tackles common issues affecting groups of smaller homeowner associations and manages common areas in the development. For details, call the Chapel Trail development office at 431-7111.

  • The Franklin Farms Homeowners Association will meet at 7 p.m. Wednesday in the community room at Miramar Police Department, 8915 Miramar Pkwy. Franklin Farms is a collection of 173 single-family homes near Douglas Road and Miramar Boulevard. Residents with questions about the meeting can call president Nancy Holloway at 435-9086.

  • Board members from the East Lake Townhomes Homeowner Association will meet at 7:30 p.m. Wednesday in the complex meeting room, 9732 NW 16th Ct., Pembroke Pines. The group's annual spring general meeting is scheduled for Wednesday. East Lake is made up of 332 townhouses on Taft Street between Douglas Road and Palm Avenue. Residents with questions can call the management office at 432-6888.

  • Trilogy residents will meet at 7:30 p.m. Wednesday at the Walter C. Young Resource Center, 901 NW 129th Ave., Pembroke Pines. Homeowners from the 486-home Miramar development -- also called Flamingo Estates -- meet once a month in Room 714 to discuss issues in their neighborhood and their city. For information, call the resource center at 437-0503.

  • Tapestry homeowners will meet at 7:30 p.m. Thursday at Rose G. Price Park, 901 NW 208th Ave., Pembroke Pines. Tapestry is a 100-home community off Northwest 186th Avenue in Chapel Trail. For details, call the Chapel Trail development office at 431-7111.
