Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.


SW HOMETOWN TYPE

POLICE REPORT

COOPER CITY

Cars scratched: In three separate incidents, car owners returned to their vehicles to find right-side scratches.

  • The first incident occurred April 4 at Pioneer Middle School, 5350 SW 90th Ave. Someone scratched the right side of a 1988 Dodge Ram Charger, causing about $800 in damage.

  • At 2:35 p.m. April 19, a man found scratches along the right side of his 1996 Toyota Camry, parked at Cooper City Hall, 9090 SW 50th Pl. The damage was estimated at $300.

  • Then, between 10 p.m. April 19 and 12:10 a.m. April 20, someone scratched the right side of a 1995 Honda. The damage to the car, which was parked in the 5800 block of Southwest 112th Way, was estimated at $300.

    Water meter stolen: Someone stole a water meter from a home in the 13100 block of Majestic Way between 5 p.m. April 19 and 7:30 a.m. April 24. The meter was valued at $125.

    License plate lifted: A license plate and its decal were taken off a car between 8 a.m. April 21 and 8:30 a.m. April 22. The car, a 1987 Pontiac, was parked in the 11600 block of South Budd Drive. The cost to replace the plate and decal is $6.

    MIRAMAR

    Interrupted burglary: A 17-year-old boy returned from school in time to find two men wearing socks over their hands and holding a white bag standing inside his home in the 6600 block of Arbor Drive about 1:10 p.m. Tuesday. The boy ran away, and so did the burglars, who discarded their socks in a neighbor's yard. No property was reported missing.

    Vandalism, theft: The owner of a home in the 6600 block of Southwest 19th Street is blaming an evicted tenant for stealing about $1,300 in appliances and causing extensive damage to the residence sometime between 8 a.m. Monday and 4 p.m. Tuesday. The owner told police a former tenant broke windows, bathroom tiles and a toilet bowl, and poured paint on the bathroom floor while stealing a refrigerator and a washing machine.

    Business burglary: A burglar pried open the front door to Sterile Recoveries, 11300 Interchange Cir., between 1 p.m. April 22 and 4 a.m. April 23 and took a facsimile machine and two cellular telephones. The equipment was valued at about $500.

    Appliances taken: A burglar took a refrigerator and stove from a house under construction in the 2200 block of Southwest 84th Terrace between 1 and 8 a.m. Tuesday. The appliances were valued at about $1,800.

    PEMBROKE PINES

    Woman hits police car: After being issued a traffic ticket, a woman put her car into reverse and drove into the police car parked behind her in the 1100 block of South University Drive about 6:25 a.m. April 26. No injuries were reported. The woman's car sustained about $600 in damage and the police car sustained about $800, police said.

    School burglary: A burglar took two videocassette recorders from portable classrooms at Pines Lakes Elementary School, 10300 Johnson St., between 3 and 7 p.m. April 26. The equipment was valued at a total of about $600.

    Car taken: A thief took a 1993 Honda Civic, valued at about $17,000, from the Pembroke Lakes Mall, 11401 Pines Blvd., between 12:55 and 10:10 p.m. April 26.

    Tools missing: A thief took about $1,800 from a Ford pickup while its owner was inside the Rose Auto Store at 9003 Taft St. about 12:45 p.m. April 27.

    Gun stolen: A thief took a .45-caliber handgun from a 1988 Sterling parked in the 300 block of North University Drive between 3 and 3:30 p.m. April 28. The value of the gun was not determined at the time of the report.
