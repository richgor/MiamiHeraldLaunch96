Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Strikeouts, top defense lead Marlins to win in Sunrise

By JIM VARSALLONE
Herald Writer

inning pitcher Joey Costanza struck out five batters in three innings to lead the Marlins over the Giants, 16-1, in Sunrise Little League Minors (ages 9-12) baseball April 26 at DCA Sports Complex.

Costanza, a left-hander, allowed no runs, no hits and three walks, improving to 7-0-1. He received solid defensive support, especially from second baseman Jeffrey Hittleman, who was awarded a game ball with four assists. Tommy Sfraga also got a game ball for his offensive heroics, which included a grand slam.

Andrew Collins and Costanza each hit two-run singles to ignite a four-run third inning, giving the Marlins (7-1-1) a 5-0 lead. Eric Abend's RBI double in the first put the Marlins ahead 1-0 in the first. First baseman Paul Jaeger snagged a line drive.

For the Giants (6-3), starting pitcher Jeremy Parrot struck out eight in three innings. Christopher DePelisi hit an RBI triple scoring Parrot, and Michael Sullivan made a good catch.

  • Anthony Bussiere hit two home runs to propel the A's (8-1) over the Angels, 16-1. Mike Lane hit his first home run of the season, and Steven Parry had three hits and scored twice. Gary Hurst added a base hit and scored twice.

    Winning pitcher Raymond Garcia struck out four in two innings. Pitcher James Parry, Steven's twin, struck out eight in three innings. Outfielder Jonathan Greenstein performed well. The Angels are 6-3.

  • Winning pitcher Brian Ecklund hit a two-run home run as the Cardinals edged the Phillies, 7-5. Ecklund -- who struck out eight in three innings -- had two hits and scored twice. Pitcher Marc Quinones earned the save, striking out three in two shutout innings of relief. Danique Henry had two doubles, including a two-run shot, and Eddie Gauna was two for two with a run.

  • The Tigers tied the White Sox, 4-4, in a surprise. Tigers' pitcher Kevin Hesse struck out 13 batters and allowed just two hits in six innings. He also had a triple. Right fielder Alonso Thomas had a two-run single and also threw a runner out trying to stretch a hit into a double.

  • The Indians won a three-hour, eight-inning marathon over the Red Sox, 15-11. The game had four lead changes. Chris Weeks pitched the final inning to seal the victory. Daniel Dunevant recorded the final out in the second straight close decision for the Indians. Tony Marhefka and Andrew Torres hit home runs to spark the offense.

  • The Re/Max Dodgers defeated the Braves, 5-1, April 21. Winning pitcher Jeremie Blount allowed one hit, walked six and struck out 14. Blount received solid defensive support.

    The River Rats defeated the Vipers, 4-1, to win the championship in the Senior Division (ages 13 and older) at Sunrise Ice Skating Center April 12. Michael Arnel scored two goals, and Vince Grenier and Scott Wondrely had one goal each. Greg Waxman scored the Vipers' lone goal.

  • The Bulls placed third with an exciting 6-5 victory over the Wolverines. Mike Gagnon led the Bulls with three goals. Jeff Grayboyes added two, and Matt Marcoux scored one. For the Wolverines, James Ryder scored twice, and Khoung Phan, Josh Rimer and Matt Swerdlow each had a goal.
    Steven Willoughby had a hat trick (three goals), and Noah Pennebaker scored two goals with three assists to lead the Weston Wildcats over Hollywood, 9-1, in the Mites Division (ages 9 and younger) April 23. Zachary Willis added two goals with one assist, and Beau Brudzinski had one goal and an assist.
