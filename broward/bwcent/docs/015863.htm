Plantation revamps recycling bag packaging

By ALEXIS CHIU
Herald Writer

T
he Plantation City Council wrapped up discussions on repackaging city garbage bags Wednesday, voting to shift to smaller packages and larger bags.

Instead of 18 blue garbage bags and four small recycling bags in every package, residents will soon be able to buy packages with 10 blue bags and three large recycling bags.

The current packages cost $29.20; the new packages will cost $16.45.

The change was prompted by the complaints of residents who said they wanted to buy fewer bags at a time. Others said they needed larger recycling bags.

In another change, the bags will no longer be sold in cardboard boxes. People were lifting the boxes' flaps and taking out one or two bags in the supermarket. Now, the bags will be packaged in clear, tamper-resistant plastic.

Small and large recycling bags still can be purchased separately at supermarkets.

  • Most glass, milk cartons, tin cans, soda cans and plastics labeled 1, 2 or 3 can be thrown out together in the clear bags.

  • Do not include cobalt blue glass, other plastics or paper products.

  • Rinse all containers.

  • Put newspapers and magazines, weighing 40 pounds or less, in brown paper bags.

  • Boxes should be broken down and tied in bundles not exceeding 40 pounds.

  • Tree trimmings and palm fronds should be tied in bundles no longer than four feet long and weighing no more than 40 pounds.
