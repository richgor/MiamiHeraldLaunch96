Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Davie considers development district to end dispute

By MARION CALLAHAN
Herald Staff Writer

T
he developer wants to build homes.

The town of Davie wants to end a lawsuit.

And Davie residents only want low-density homes built around them.

Former Town Administrator Irving Rosenbaum stepped up to the council podium Wednesday with a proposal to accomplish all three of those goals for the Belmont Lakes community in western Davie.

Rosenbaum's suggestion to create a community development district appealed to council members, especially after the landowner offered to drop the pending lawsuit against the town. Council members unanimously agreed to halt litigation and consider a new development district in Davie.

``This sounds like a really fine settlement,'' Mayor Harry Venis said.

In September, council members rejected a developer's proposal to rezone the 50-acre parcel in western Davie. In October, the landowner, SunBank of Ocala, filed a lawsuit in Broward Circuit Court appealing the decision against estate zoning, which would have allowed it to build homes on lots smaller than one acre.

The property, near Southwest 14th Street and Shotgun Road, borders Davie's Oak Hills and D&M Acres, where the majority of residents own animals.

``A lot of developers try to get density increases because it's not always economically feasible to build the one-acre lots in the country-style manner,'' Rosenbaum said. ``This kind of district allows Davie to maintain its rural character.''

The landowner could then afford to build homes on one-acre lots. By creating a district, it would allow the landowner to borrow money from a bank to build the infrastructure -- water and sewer fixtures -- and the residents would pay the principal and interest through fixed assessment fees.

``The town would be under no financial obligation and the homeowners are protected,'' said Rosenbaum, a municipal consultant and director of the Center for Entrepreneurship for Nova Southeastern University. ``Everyone walks away with what they want.''
