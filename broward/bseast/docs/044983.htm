Cities plan swimming lessons for kids, adults this summer

By MARSHA HALPER
Herald Staff Writer

W
ith summer -- and prime swimming season -- fast approaching, now's the time to start thinking about swimming lessons for your kids, or even yourself. Here's a sampling of what's available in Southeast Broward.

  • City of Hollywood: The city's aquatics program offers swimming lessons at Apollo Middle School and Attucks Middle School pools, for people 2 or older. Sessions are two weeks long and start June 17. Fee: $21. Registration will be from 9 a.m. to 2 p.m. June 1 at David Park, 108 N. 33rd Ct., Hollywood. For details, call the city's Parks and Recreation Department at 921-3460 or David Park at 967-4236.

  • Greater Hollywood YMCA: Lessons are available for people 6 months or older. New sessions start at the beginning of each month. Parent and tot sessions have four classes and cost $18 for members, $26 for nonmembers. Weekday sessions last one month and have two classes per week. Saturday sessions last two months. The cost for weekday or Saturday sessions is $30 for members, $35 for nonmembers. Registration for June sessions starts May 13, but Saturday sessions are booked until July. If you want to enroll in a Saturday session, wait until June. For details, call 989-9622.

  • Topeekeegee Yugnee Park: Lessons are available for people 3 or older in the swimming area of the park, 3300 N. Park Rd., Hollywood. Registration is ongoing for the first two-week session, which starts June 24. The cost is $40. For details, call 985-1980.

  • City of Dania: Swimming lessons will be offered at Modello Park Pool. The city's Parks and Recreation Department will announce the schedule when it is complete. For details, call 921-8700.

  • City of Hallandale: Lessons will be given at the Peter Bluesten Park (formerly City Park) pool for toddlers through adults. The city is fine-tuning its lesson schedule. It should be available May 18. For details, call the Parks and Recreation Department at 457-1450.
