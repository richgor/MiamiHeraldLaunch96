Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Pembroke Park commission to vote on longer club hours

By MARSHA HALPER
Herald Staff Writer

T
he last call for alcohol soon may change in Pembroke Park.

Town commissioners will vote on Wednesday whether to amend an ordinance regulating hours of alcohol consumption at nightclubs, lounges and any establishment that serves alcohol. Now, they must close between 6 and 7 a.m. Saturdays.

Under the proposed ordinance, businesses could remain open between those hours on Saturdays, paving the way for 24-hour service.

Vice Mayor Karen Janzer, who proposed the ordinance change, said the 6 a.m. closing time is causing business owners to lose patrons.

``All their business is shooting over to Hallandale, and then when our businesses reopen, people don't come back,'' Janzer said.

In 1994, Hallandale city commissioners approved 24-hour operations at nightclubs, and last year renewed licenses for six establishments.

Cafe Casablanca, 100 Ansin Blvd., one block east of Interstate 95, is open until 8 a.m. Saturdays. Ocean's 11 South Lounge, 800 N. Federal Hwy., opens at 11 a.m. Friday and stays open until 6 a.m. Tuesday.

A few Pembroke Park establishments have expressed interest in the lengthened hours, including Casey's Nickelodeon, 5590 W. Hallandale Beach Blvd. It now closes at 6 a.m. Saturdays.

Despite reports of drunkenness and fights at some of the after-hours clubs in Hallandale, that city's commissioners voted unanimously to approve the late-closing licenses.

Janzer said she doesn't expect similar problems in Pembroke Park if the ordinance is amended.

``I don't think it will make a bit of difference for us, but it will help the businesses,'' she said. ``And there are some things we can do if there are problems, like limiting outdoor [waiting] lines to 40 people,'' at establishments such as Zippers, a nightclub one block west of Interstate 95.

Pembroke Park commissioners approved the amendment on first reading. After a 7:15 p.m. public hearing on Wednesday, they will vote again. If it passes, a motion can be made for a third and final reading, also on Wednesday. If that passes, the amended ordinance will be official.

Town Hall is at 3150 SW 52nd Ave. The Town Commission meeting is at 7 p.m. Wednesday.
