Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Owner removes boat from Intracoastal

By GREG BROWN
Herald Staff Writer

A
wrecked, partially submerged, 57-foot cabin cruiser that had floated in the Intracoastal Waterway since Oct. 1 is gone.

State and local marine officials had said in January that it would take at least eight months to find the money to remove James Schmale's 1947-vintage boat from the Intracoastal near Hallandale Beach Boulevard.

A state grant program to pay up to $30,000 for the removal wouldn't consider applications until August, Hallandale officers said.

Instead, the state acted sooner. Florida Marine Patrol issued a citation against Schmale, said Capt. Robert E. Rogers, head of the city's road patrol, which also oversees Hallandale's single-boat marine patrol.

A Broward County Court judge told Schmale to remove the boat at his own expense within 30 days. Last week, the vessel was removed, Rogers said.

All that's left in the water behind Manero's Steakhouse, 2600 E. Hallandale Beach Blvd. -- the boat's berth for four years -- is a makeshift foam marker splattered with red spray paint.

``The owner of the boat complied,'' Rogers said. ``He came down with a crane and divers and worked daily for a month and a half.''

Schmale, Rogers said, was unemployed and difficult to reach. Calls to his last address in West Palm Beach went unanswered.

Hallandale boater Sol Weiner, 70, said he is ecstatic the boat is gone. A regular fisherman, Weiner passed the wrecked cabin cruiser on his way north from his dock at Keystone Point in North Miami.

Weiner said he had been frustrated by the news in January that government agencies couldn't act sooner to remove the boat.

``Every group in every department was blaming it on everybody else,'' Weiner said.

The boat became partially submerged, Schmale said earlier, because a man broke into the boat on Sept. 19. Schmale said the man cut electrical cords to bilge pumps below deck, causing the boat to founder.

Hallandale police arrested George Tyson, a 25-year-old homeless man, on charges of burglary and possessing a sawed-off shotgun.

Schmale spent $2,600 trying to salvage the boat before giving up. Soon, chunks of the wooden vessel, furniture and boat parts began drifting down the waterway.

Unable to muster the public money to raise the boat, the Coast Guard drained its oil and gas tanks and left it alone.
