Published Monday, May 6, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Clinton pal set to pack for Panama

Fort Lauderdale trial attorney Jeffrey Orseck of TV ad fame finally received a presidential appointment from his college chum Bill Clinton -- three years, three months and a couple weeks after Clinton took office. The president has announced his intent to appoint Orseck to the Panama Canal Consultative Committee.

OK, so it's not quite the ambassadorship to Costa Rica that Orseck thought he'd get three years ago -- a ``lifelong ambition,'' he said at the time. But with the United States relinquishing operational control of the Panama Canal in 1999, the advisory committee post could at least be interesting.

Orseck earned a degree in foreign service with Clinton at Georgetown University, and raised more than $1 million in campaign money for his former classmate.

``I think it's a difficult assignment,'' Orseck said Sunday. ``It's not a popular decision, transferring the Panama Canal from the United States' possession to Panamanian sovereignty. I'm very interested in completing it successfully on behalf of the president and our country.''

RECUPERATING: Norma Howard, 69, director of the Broward County Bar Association for 31 years, is recovering at Holy Cross hospital after suffering a stroke -- in the middle of her retirement party. ``It was horrible,'' says Cynthia White, the bar's new director. ``Everything had been so lovely, the evening had been so wonderful, then all of a sudden. Dinner was basically over and she was about to get up and say her thank-yous but she couldn't stand, she fell over. At first everyone thought she had just fallen. It took a couple minutes to realize what had happened. Then we called 911.''

The stroke affected Howard's left arm and leg, but not her speech, and she's expected to return home soon, White said.

Responds Dave Corriveau: ``We've heard that before in other markets. But we have no interest in being in the gambling industry. WINGING IT: Among the accomplishments of state Rep. Jack Tobin, D-Margate, during the legislative session -- passage of a bill designating the zebra longwing butterfly as the state's official butterfly. House leaders told Tobin that Gov. Chiles' wife, Rhea, and her garden club were enamored of the insect and asked if he would sponsor the bill. Tobin figured he'd take some razzing from colleagues, but then remembered that the Butterfly World tourist attraction was in his district. So he agreed. ``It flew through the House,'' said Tobin. (State Sen. Howard Forman, D-Pembroke Pines, sponsored the measure in the Senate.) For the bill signing in Tallahassee, Butterfly World sent 15 zebra longwings sleeping peacefully in tiny cellophane pouches inside the dark, dark FedEx box.
