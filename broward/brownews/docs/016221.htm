Published Monday, May 6, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Women rescue teen from wrecked, burning car

By D. AILEEN DODD
Herald Staff Writer

Two women pulled an 18-year-old from a burning car Sunday morning after the teen, who was driving against traffic on U.S. 441, struck a utility pole.

The accident occurred just after 7:30 a.m. when Fabian Salgado, of 4189 Lakeside Dr., Tamarac, was heading south in the northbound lanes of U.S. 441.

Fatigued and under the influence of alcohol, Salgado was unaware he was traveling against traffic, police said.

He swerved to avoid an oncoming car, and smashed into a utility pole near Northwest 12th Street in Lauderhill. His car bounced five feet.

Motorists Karen Clementson of Lauderhill and Herma Rodgers of Coral Springs arrived on the scene seconds later as they traveled north on U.S. 441 .

When the two discovered someone was still inside the smoking car in their path, they stopped. Salgado sat, hunched in his seat with blood on his shirt and forehead.

``I got out and she got out, and we went to him. We thought he was in shock,'' Rodgers said. ``When we started to take him out, there were flames coming out of the engine. By the time we got him on the ground, the whole car was in flames. I thought, `Oh my God, this boy could have been killed.' ''

Paramedics arrived minutes later, and took Salgado to Broward General Medical Center in Fort Lauderdale.

The teen told police he fell asleep behind the wheel after consuming alcohol. Police and rescue workers said they smelled alcohol on Salgado's breath. A blood sample was taken for blood alcohol content screening.

Rodgers, a registered nurse, was on her way to see a client when she stopped to help save Salgado. ``It's my job, I'm a nurse,'' she said. ``I just did what I had to do.''
