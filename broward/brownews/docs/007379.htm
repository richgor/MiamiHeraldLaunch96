Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.


BROWARD REGIONAL TYPE

20-year-old killed near Lauderdale

A 20-year-old man was fatally shot in the driveway of a duplex apartment Monday night near Fort Lauderdale.

Shelton Jackson was standing outside the duplex at 2709 NW 15th St. about 8 p.m. when two men in a white car drove up, said Broward Sheriff's Office spokesman Jim Leljedal.

Witnesses told police the men got out of the car and approached Jackson. One of the men pulled out a gun and fired it at close range, hitting Jackson.

Jackson collapsed and was pronounced dead when paramedics arrived, Leljedal said.

After the shooting, the suspects hopped into their car and drove east on 15th Street and then north on Northwest 27th Avenue.

Detectives aren't sure what the motive for the killing was, but said it appeared the victim and the suspect knew each other and were involved in a dispute, Leljedal said.

Anyone with information about the shooting is asked to call BSO homicide detectives Jim Carr or Eli Thomasevich at 321-4210 or Crime Stoppers at 493-TIPS.
