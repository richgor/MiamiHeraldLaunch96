Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Man, 74, critically injured by gunshot at target range

By TRISH POWER
Herald Staff Writer

A 74-year-old man was on life support late Friday after shooting himself in the head in an apparent suicide attempt at a Pembroke Park gun range, investigators said.

The man, Melvin Kahn of Hallandale, may have tried to commit suicide, said Jim Leljedal, spokesman for the Broward Sheriff's Office.

Kahn arrived at the American Indoor Gun Range at 3130 SW 19th St. at about 6:55 p.m. A woman was driving the car that dropped him off. Witnesses heard him say he'd call her when he was done, detectives said.

Kahn registered with the range master, then took up a position on the firing line with the .38-caliber revolver.

``He was not a regular,'' Leljedal said. ``I think it was his first time. . . . According to the employee, there was nothing unusual. He acted normal.''

Three other customers and a range employee were on the line at the time, Leljedal said.

Witnesses said Kahn fired several shots at the target. A short time later, someone saw him on the floor and noticed his head was bleeding, Leljedal said.

Pembroke Park fire-rescue workers responded, treated Kahn and transported him to Memorial Regional Hospital in Hollywood, where he was listed in extremely critical condition at about 10:30 p.m. Friday, Leljedal said.

They determined the shooting was an attempted suicide, Leljedal said.
