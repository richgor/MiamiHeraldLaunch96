FORT LAUDERDALE

Man electrocuted while working on yacht

A 35-year-old man was electrocuted Thursday while he worked on an air-conditioning unit on a yacht at the Bahia Mar Marina in Fort Lauderdale, police said.

The accident happened about 10:20 a.m. when John Joseph Brown of Hollywood apparently leaned against a hot wire, fire Battalion Chief Tom O'Day said.

A man, identified as the owner of the 46-foot yacht, found the electrician below deck with his feet sticking out of a crawl space and pulled him to the deck, authorities said.

He suffered third-degree burns to his face and neck and was pronounced dead at the scene, O'Day said.

Summit set for youths of African descent

Youths of African descent will get a chance to learn a little more about each other and work out differences during a Community Youth Summit on Saturday.

The summit is scheduled from 8 a.m. to 3 p.m. at Fort Lauderdale High School, 1600 NE Fourth Ave.

Free and open to the public, the summit's primary goal is to foster positive and wholesome relationships among Haitian, Jamaican and African-American youths in Broward.

LAUDERHILL

Cops: Intruder stabbed by teenage girl

A 17-year-old boy who broke into a Lauderhill house was stabbed in the shoulder Wednesday by the teenage girl who lived at house, police said.

Hours later, the teen intruder was arrested when one of the teen girls recognized him as a former classmate, said Lauderhill police spokeswoman Lucy Crockett.

This is what police say happened:

The 15-year-old girl and a friend were hanging laundry outside the house about 1:45 p.m. Wednesday when two teen boys sneaked inside the house, police said.

When the two girls walked inside, they realized that the house, at 4810 NW 20th St., had been burglarized. The girls got a knife from the kitchen and checked the house to make sure no one was inside.

When the girls started straightening out one of the rooms, they heard someone walk in through the front door, Crockett said. The girls hid in the bedroom. When one of the youths walked inside the room, the 15-year old stabbed him, Crockett said. The boys ran away and the girls called police.

Officers found the injured youth at Broward General Medical Center in Fort Lauderdale and charged him with burglary. Police had not found the second youth late Thursday. Several pieces of jewelry were missing.

Anyone with information about the case is asked to call Lauderhill police at 497-4700 or Crimestoppers at 493-8477.

MIRAMAR

Race set to help celebrate city's birthday

A 10K Run will be held Saturday as part of Miramar Days '96, a celebration of the city's 41st birthday.

The race will begin at 7 a.m. at Shirley Branca Park, 6900 Miramar Pkwy. The public can register the day of the race, for $20. The price includes a T-shirt, and the winners will receive plaques.

For more information about this event or any of the Miramar Days events, call 962-5501.

NORTHWEST BROWARD

FHP plans toll crackdown at Sawgrass ramps

Authorities on Monday will step up enforcement against drivers who don't pay the toll on ramps that lead to and from the Sawgrass Expressway.

The Florida Highway Patrol will focus on the unattended ramps at seven interchanges along the 23-mile expressway. Not paying tolls can result in a $100 fine.

The toll crackdown will be at Lyons Road, U.S. 441, University Drive, Sample Road, Atlantic Boulevard, Commercial Boulevard and Oakland Park Boulevard.

PEMBROKE PARK

BSO sponsoring bicycle rodeo for kids

The Broward Sheriff's Office is sponsoring a bicycle rodeo, registration and free helmet giveaway, just in time for a new Florida law that requires kids under 16 to wear a helmet while riding in the street.

Children from first through fifth grades can participate in the event, which will begin at 9 a.m. Tuesday at Watkins Elementary School, 3601 SW 56th Ave., Pembroke Park.

The first 40 kids to get their bikes engraved with a special identification number will receive a bicycle helmet. The ID number will help police identify the bike if it's ever stolen. The rodeo will stress biking safety.

The bicycle helmet law, which was passed by the Florida Legislature and goes into effect in 1997, is expected to reduce biking head injuries by 85 percent.

TRAFFIC UPDATE

A1A to close at bridge over Hillsboro Inlet

Expect these traffic tie-ups during the next few days:

  • The State Road A1A bridge over the Hillsboro Inlet on the Pompano Beach-Hillsboro Beach border will be closed to road traffic from 4 to 6 p.m. Saturday and Sunday. The bridge will be in the up position in anticipation of heavy boat traffic. The nearest alternative routes are U.S. 1 to the Northeast 14th Street bridge in Pompano Beach to the south and the Hillsboro Boulevard bridge in Deerfield Beach to the north.

  • Dixie Highway near Pompano Beach will be closed to all traffic on both sides of the Sample Road intersection from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Saturday. At the same time, Sample Road will be restricted to one lane in each direction approaching Dixie Highway.

  • The Las Olas Boulevard Bridge over the Intracoastal Waterway in Fort Lauderdale will open for boat traffic only every 15 minutes between 10 a.m. and 6 p.m. today, Saturday and Sunday. The Coast Guard and the Florida Department of Transportation are testing the impact of the weekend schedule change on roadway and marine traffic until June 19. The bridge will be closed to vehicular traffic for the Shell Air & Sea Show from 9 a.m. to 10 p.m. Saturday and 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Sunday.

  • Traffic on Las Olas Boulevard from Southeast 16th Avenue to the Intracoastal Waterway in Fort Lauderdale has been shifted to the newly reconstructed pavement on the north side of the road. One lane in each direction is open at all times. The speed limit is reduced to 20 miles per hour. The mile-long section of Las Olas Boulevard is being rebuilt.
