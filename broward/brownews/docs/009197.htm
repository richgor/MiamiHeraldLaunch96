Parents resigned to half-day school

By SUE CORBETT
Herald Staff Writer

Karen Braverman has a one-word solution for crowding problems at her daughter's elementary school: Stuart.

``We lived there before and it was wonderful, like a small college town,'' said Braverman, as she sat at a cafeteria table at Country Isles Elementary in Weston, filling out kindergarten registration forms for Victoria, 5. ``If this keeps up, I'm going to have to move back.''

Not everybody thinks moving north is the answer, but it's a truth-in-jest remark you hear these days in Southwest Broward.

Braverman was one of about 100 Weston-area parents who came to their child's school early Tuesday to hear more about a new program most of them don't want: half-day kindergarten.

``I know this is not what you would have chosen,'' Principal Marci Smith told them. ``It's not what I would have chosen either.''

But faced with the irreconcilable equation of ever-increasing numbers of students and steadily diminishing money to build, three elementary schools -- Country Isles, Chapel Trail and Silver Palms -- are being forced to reduce their offerings.

Full-day kindergarten took the hit. In the fall, some kindergartners will go to class in the morning, others in the afternoon.

Penny Tannenbaum saw the bright side:

``Full-day kindergarten is a little too long for them, anyway,'' she said. ``It'll all work out.''

That's the approach Michele Balcerzak is recommending, too. She heads Silver Palms' school advisory committee.

``The kids will adjust,'' she says. ``It's the parents I worry about.''

Silver Palms' building, in Pembroke Pines, is still under construction, but the school set up camp this year at a portable site in Cooper City.

The school's attendance area covers one of the fastest-growing suburbs in the nation -- a swath of Pembroke Pines and Miramar where about 25 families close on new homes each week.

In January, Silver Palms -- though not technically open yet -- had to cap its enrollment because the portable site could not house any more students.

Students who have moved into Silver Palms' attendance zone since January have been temporarily assigned to a different Pembroke Pines school -- Pines Lakes Elementary. Since January, 120 new students have arrived -- five classrooms worth.

``Whatever child comes in, we find a place somehow,'' said Ann Fee, Pines Lakes principal.

Though the majority of parents who attended Tuesday's sessions understand the problem and seem grudgingly resigned to half-day kindergarten as a short-term solution, many worry that it is too little, too late.

At Country Isles, parents wondered aloud: What are we getting into?

``What are they going to do for first grade? Second grade?'' asked Cindy Martin, parent of Joseph, 5. ``Is this going to follow him every year? It's frustrating. I bought out here for the schools.''

Weston parents had hoped a proposed kindergarten/teen center would avert the need for half-day sessions, but the principal told them: Don't count on it. School district officials say it won't be built in time, if at all.

Chapel Trail Elementary had about 250 parents show up for the first day of kindergarten registration.

The principal had estimated a total kindergarten class of 250 to 300. Registration is open for three more months.
