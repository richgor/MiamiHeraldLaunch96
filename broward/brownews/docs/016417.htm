Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Officer stakes out site of attempted theft, nabs 2

Two Hollywood youths who allegedly returned to the scene of a crime in Lauderhill have been charged with burglary, police said.

Police said the juveniles, 14 and 15 years old, along with a third suspect who got away, tried to steal a truckload of sound equipment about 2:54 a.m. Thursday in the 2000 block of Northwest 46th Avenue.

Before the burglars were able to make off with the equipment, the truck's alarm sounded. The owner ran from a nearby apartment in time to stop the burglary and get a description of the suspects and the vehicle they were driving.

Suspecting that the burglars would return to finish the job, Lauderhill police officer Richard Dostie parked his patrol car out of sight. Within minutes, a dark Toyota matching the description provided by the truck owner pulled up behind the truck.

When three males got out of the car, Dostie approached them.

Though the unidentified driver ran, the other two suspects were detained and charged. Police said they also recovered stolen property from the Toyota.
