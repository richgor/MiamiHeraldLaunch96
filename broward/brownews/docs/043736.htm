Letter carriers to collect, deliver food

South Florida letter carriers hope to deliver more than a million pounds of nonperishable food to needy families in coming months. They're asking residents and businesses to leave donations for carriers to pick up Saturday, wherever mail normally is delivered.

Bags and cartons of food may be left beside residential mailboxes or near common mailbox areas in condominiums and apartments.

``We're looking for things people can make a meal out of,'' said Ezra M. Krieg, associate director of Daily Bread Food Bank in South Florida, which will distribute the goods. Suggested items are canned soups and vegetables, pasta, beans and rice. The donation of glass jars is discouraged.

The effort is part of a national drive that last year collected more than 40 million pounds of food. In South Florida, more than 800,000 pounds were collected and distributed to more than 1,200 South Florida groups that feed the needy.

Firms share goods, good will with schools

Several local companies involved with Broward schools are sharing resources this week with their partners.

On Tuesday, Florida Marlins players started visiting area schools, as well as distributing game tickets. Today, a software development company, Brighter Child Interactive, will donate 120 copies of software to teach students the basics in reading, phonics and math.

On Thursday, Motorola will have a celebration for youth motivators and the students they mentor. And on Friday, Partners in Education, Broward's business-education partnership, will have its annual Partnership of the Year Recognition Breakfast.

HOLLYWOOD

Cinderella Ball unites Scouts, their dads

Girl Scouts and Brownie Scouts throughout the West Hollywood and Pembroke Pines area will be attending the Eighth Annual Cinderella Ball May 18 at the Hillcrest Playdium, 1100 Hillcrest Dr. in Hollywood. The special event unites dads and daughters for an evening of music, dancing and refreshments. More than 250 Scouts and their dads are expected to attend. For more information, call 739-7660.

PORT EVERGLADES Port to be site of destroyer commissioning

Port Everglades has been selected as the commissioning site for the U.S. Navy's latest guided missile destroyer, which will be formally commissioned as the USS Cole on June 8.

Constructed by Ingalls Shipbuilding in Pascagoula, Miss., the Arleigh Burke Class warship is expected to arrive at Port Everglades on June 3 and depart on June 10.

Lee Perry, wife of U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry, is the ship's sponsor, and Secretary Perry is scheduled to be the principle speaker at the commissioning ceremony.

The ship is named for Marine Sgt. Darrell Samuel Cole, who was posthumously awarded the Medal of Honor for his heroism during the invasion of Iwo Jima in 1945.

While the commissioning ceremony will be open to invited guests only, the new ship will be open to the public from 2 to 5 p.m. June 9.

WESTON

Cocomo's may re-open as community center

Cocomo's, the Weston restaurant and bar best known for its closing, is once again about to go out of business. But this time, no one's protesting.

Cocomo's is an informal gathering place for some members of the Weston Athletic Club, which is in the same building at 2300 Arvida Pkwy. Last July, a small but vocal group convinced Arvida/JMB Partners to change its plans to close the watering hole. The protesters talked the developer into keeping the bar portion open. Since then, residents have been talking with Arvida about Cocomo's future.

Now, they have a plan: Close the bar May 31, then re-open all of Cocomo's as a community center. It would become a meeting place for all ages and all kinds of local groups, said Arvida's Claudia Troisi, who is in charge of commercial development in Weston.

Troisi said she hoped to hammer out the specifics in the next two weeks. In the meantime, she is looking for experienced food and beverage operators who want to bid on rights to serve food at the community center, using what was Cocomo's kitchen. Anyone interested can join Troisi on a tour of the facilities Monday. For a time and more details, call Troisi at 384-8000.

TRAFFIC UPDATE

Lanes to close on W. Hallandale Beach Blvd.

Expect these traffic tie-ups in the next few days:

  • A lane in each direction will be closed on West Hallandale Beach Boulevard between Bryan Road west of Interstate 95 and Edmund Road east of U.S. 441 in Pembroke Park from 9 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. today through Friday. A concrete traffic separator is being built, shoulders added and the entire roadway repaved.

  • The westbound emergency lane on Interstate 595 between U.S. 441 in Davie and Interstate 95 in Dania will be closed from 9 a.m to 4 p.m. today and Thursday. The I-595 bridge over the Pond Apple Slough just east of the 441 interchange will be inspected. Repairs will be made to the bridge decking.

  • In Carver Ranches, these roads will be closed through Friday: Southwest 40th Avenue between 17th and 21st streets; Southwest 44th Avenue between 17th and 21st streets and 23rd and 24th streets; the alley between Southwest 17th and 18th streets from 40th to 44th avenues; Southwest 19th Street between 40th and 44th avenues; and Southwest 20th Street between 44th and 48th avenues. Local residents will be provided access. New roadways, sidewalks, landscaping, sewers, drainage and a water main are being installed as part of the South County Neighborhood Improvement Project.

  • State Road A1A from Sunrise Boulevard to the 1100 block will be closed to traffic midnight Friday through Monday because of the Shell Air and Sea Show. Also closed during show hours from 9 a.m. to 10 p.m. Saturday and 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Sunday will be A1A from Vistamar Street to Northeast 19th Street; East Sunrise Boulevard from Northeast 26th Avenue/Seminole Drive to A1A; and East Las Olas Boulevard from Southeast 15th Avenue to A1A. Access to the beach will be limited and drivers will be able get to the beach only by the 17th Street Causeway or East Oakland Park Boulevard.

  • Two southbound lanes on the Palmetto Expressway/State Road 826 between Northwest 67th Avenue and Interstate 75 in Dade will be closed from midnight to 5:30 a.m. Thursday to remove barrier walls.
