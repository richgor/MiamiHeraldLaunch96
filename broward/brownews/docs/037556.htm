Broward School Board OKs waivers on policy

By JACQUELINE CHARLES
Herald Staff Writer

The Broward School Board on Tuesday approved more than 100 school waiver requests, including one that may reduce the school week for students at Pompano Beach's Ely High School.

According to the school calendar waiver requested by a school committee, Ely students would attend classes four days a week instead of five. The fifth day would be optional for field trips and special programs.

Ely is a medical/science and pre-engineering magnet school.

Under the recommendation approved by Superintendent Frank Petruzielo and voted on by board members, the school will have up to a year to map out a plan for altering the school week.

Among some of the waiver requests recommended by the superintendent and approved by the board: exam exemptions, the use of alternate referral forms, and the integration of health into the sixth-grade physical education curriculum.

All waivers pertaining to the grading scale were deferred for more discussion later this month at a School Board retreat.
