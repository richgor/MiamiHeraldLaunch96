Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Award recognizes student's good deeds

By FRANK FERNANDEZ
Herald Staff Writer

Eric Perlyn, a Fort Lauderdale student, didn't pay much attention to shoes. But that eventually changed and led to Perlyn's selection Monday as a national winner in the Prudential Spirit of Community Awards.

Perlyn, 18, has donated about 5,000 new shoes to needy children, many of whom had never owned a new pair of sneakers, he said.

``I call them magic shoes, really,'' Perlyn said. ``It does a lot more than protect their feet. It changes their attitude and their self esteem as well.''

Perlyn is among 10 honorees culled from an initial field of 7,000 across the nation, said Lew Armistead, program spokesperson for the Prudential Spirit of Community Awards. A middle school student near St. Petersburg also was honored, making Florida and Oklahoma the only states with two honorees each among the 10.

Perlyn will receive $5,000 and a gold medallion, Armistead said. Pine Crest Preparatory School in Fort Lauderdale, where Perlyn is a senior, will receive a crystal trophy, he said.

Perlyn is a thoughtful student, said Pine Crest Principal Lee Turner.

``He cares about other people,'' Turner said. ``You don't often find that in kids today. And he's a good student, too.''

Perlyn said he is thrilled with the honor.

``I'm still in shock from it,'' he said.

The urge to help people directly, rather than writing a check, is what started the project, called Stepp'n Up, Perlyn said. He started the program six years ago by walking into shoe stores and asking for donations of sneakers.

In the first year, he collected about 175 shoes. That grew to about 1,000 last year, he said. People need to help the less fortunate, he said.

``This project has taught me to see the real world,'' Perlyn said. ``My life is not necessarily the real world. I happen to be very fortunate. There are people who are far less fortunate than I am and we need to help them.''
