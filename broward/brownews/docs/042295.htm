Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Hollywood manager, attorney get raises

By CONNIE PRATER
Herald Staff Writer

Praised for their professionalism and ability to take risks, Hollywood's top administrator and attorney easily passed the City Commission's annual review Wednesday and got $5,000 raises each.

City Manager Sam Finz, 51, who is paid $108,000 a year, will make $113,000 starting June 20. That's a 4.62 percent increase.

City Attorney Jamie Cole, 32, currently receives a base salary of $95,000 and will go to $100,000 retroactive to April 30. He got a 5.26 percent raise.

Cole also gets $12,000 as legal counsel to the city's Community Redevelopment Agency. That compensation remains the same.

The package for each also includes a life insurance upgrade that will cost the city about $1,000 each. The term life insurance policies that each already receives will be upgraded from $350,000 to $500,000.

Commissioners unanimously approved the packages during a special meeting Wednesday.

``I think they do an outstanding job,'' Commissioner Ken Gottlieb said. ``They've done a lot for the city. I don't think we pay them enough. I think that what we've given them is very fair.''

Mayor Mara Giulianti said she liked the fact that Finz, who has been city manager since 1993, took risks in tackling new programs.

Among them: implementing a beach revitalization plan, preparing a five-year capital improvement plan for $86 million, reducing sanitation fees and city personnel costs and developing a tough anti-nepotism policy.

``We've had city managers who didn't risk anything because they were afraid to fail,'' Giulianti said. ``Not every single thing is going to succeed. We don't expect 20-20 vision that hindsight gives.''

Commissioner Richard Blattner commended Finz for ``keeping as many balls in the air as he does and being able to catch all of them.''

It wasn't all praise, though.

Giulianti said she wanted Finz to interact and network more with other city managers around Broward to share ideas and collaborate on projects that could help the city.

And Vice Mayor Cathy Anderson pointed out water billing problems that have generated calls this week from homeowners.

``I know that this occurred before you came, but you've got to straighten this stuff out,'' said Anderson.

She also said she was concerned about the high salaries and raises being given to department heads and how they will affect the retirement system. And Anderson cited as a problem allowing some property owners to amass huge fines, which are then waived by the city.

As for Cole, Commissioner Eleanor Sobel applauded the way he has explained legal issues and cases to the commission.

``Sometimes we have some very, very complicated cases. He has a way of reducing these to concise, clear meaning,'' she said.
