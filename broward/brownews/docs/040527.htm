Around Broward County

BONAVENTURE

Conference will deal with parenting issues

The second annual South Florida Conference on Parenting is set at the Bonaventure Conference Center in West Broward.

The daylong event on June 22 will bring together more than 30 experts. Topics will include such issues as ``parenting without anger,'' ``teaching your child to honor authority'' and ``creative storytelling.''

The conference is open to the public. Registration is $35 to $60 and includes lunch.

Child care and education credits are available.

Call (954)783-5299 for more information.

DAVIE

Town to hold summer activities for teens

The town of Davie will offer summer activities for teens 13 to 18 from June 24 to Aug. 16. Registration is under way for special events, including a trip to see a Florida Marlins game, summer movies, dances, pool parties, a day cruise and air boat rides. Sporting tournaments will be organized for bowling, tennis, paddleball, basketball and roller hockey.

For more information, parents can call Dan Massa at 797-1161.

FORT LAUDERDALE

Police seek brother of shooting victim

Homicide detectives continued looking for answers Monday in the weekend shooting death of Alejandro Lopez.

Lopez, 43, was found dead inside the bedroom in his home at 516 SW 14th Ct. in Fort Lauderdale early Sunday morning, said homicide Sgt. Tim Bronson.

Fort Lauderdale police got a call from Lopez's sister about 2:15 a.m. Sunday. She said another brother had called her and told her that Alejandro had been shot. Police went to the residence and found the bedroom door locked, Bronson said.

``They kicked the door down. He was dead in the room,'' Bronson said.

Police talked to the brother who made the call about the shooting, but they continued their search Monday for another brother, Antonio Lopez, and a nephew, Jaime Lopez, Bronson said.

``We believe a fight or argument ensued in the apartment,'' Bronson said. ``They were there. We certainly want to talk to them.''

Anyone with information is asked to call homicide detectives John King or John Curcio at 761-5700.

Computer equipment stolen from school
A burglar broke into the Fort Lauderdale Art Institute and stole about $9,000 worth of computer equipment, police said.

The burglar slipped into the institute between 4:30 p.m. Saturday and 6 a.m. Monday by breaking a window on the institute's ground floor, at 1799 SE 17th St., said police spokesman Clint Ward.

The thief stole a personal computer, a monitor, a laser printer and a scanner, which stores text and images on a computer, Ward said.

HOLLYWOOD

Car thefts and burglaries focus of meeting

Representatives of the Hollywood Police Department will meet with the Hollywood Three Lakes Civic Association at 7:30 tonight to counsel residents on how to avoid being victims of auto thefts and burglaries.

Club anti-theft devices will be given away through a lottery system through a grant to Hollywood's detective bureau.

The meeting will be at the Hollywood Beach Community Center, 1301 S. Ocean Dr.

Volunteers honored for helping Russians

Several South Floridians active in the Jewish Family Service of Broward's Russian resettlement program will be honored tonight at the Hollywood organization's 34th annual meeting.

Dorothy Sands, Dr. Charles Glaser and Wolf Taninbaum of Bari Associates will be recognized for their commitment to helping Russians who have moved into the Broward area.

The meeting, which includes a kosher buffet dinner, will begin at 6 p.m. at the Club at Emerald Hills, 4100 N. Hills Dr., Hollywood. Tickets cost $36. Call 966-0956.

POMPANO BEACH

Gunman robs bank, escapes with cash

Pompano Beach police are searching for a man who held up a teller at NationsBank, 100 E. McNab Rd., about 2:40 p.m. Monday.

The suspect pointed a large blue .45-caliber or 9mm handgun at the teller and demanded money. He ran away with a small amount of cash. No one was injured.

Anyone with more information is asked to call Pompano Beach police at 786-4200.

TRAFFIC UPDATE

Bridge work to close I-595 emergency lanes

Expect these traffic tie-ups in the next few days:

  • The eastbound emergency lane on Interstate 595 between U.S. 441 in Davie and Interstate 95 in Dania will be closed from 9 a.m to 4 p.m. today. The westbound emergency lane will be closed Wednesday and Thursday during the same hours. The I-595 bridge over the Pond Apple Slough just east of U.S. 441 will be inspected. Repairs will be made to the bridge's decking.

  • A lane in each direction on West Hallandale Beach Boulevard in Pembroke Park between Interstate 95 and U.S. 441 will be closed from 9 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. Wednesday through Friday. A concrete traffic separator is being built, shoulders added and the road repaved.

  • One southbound lane on the Palmetto Expressway/State Road 826 between Northwest 67th Avenue and Interstate 75 in Dade will be closed from 9:30 p.m. to midnight today; two lanes will be closed from midnight Tuesday to 5:30 a.m. Wednesday and again from midnight Wednesday to 5:30 a.m. Thursday. Barrier walls are being removed.

  • A lane in each direction on Biscayne Boulevard at Northeast 181st Street in Aventura will be closed from 9 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. daily until May 17. A gas line is being installed.

  • A southbound lane on the flyover ramp of Interstate 75 leading to Florida's Turnpike in West Dade will be closed from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. today. The bridge is being inspected.
