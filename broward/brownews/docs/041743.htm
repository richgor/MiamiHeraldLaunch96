Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Fake bomb found at Hollywood store

By DANNY ALVAREZ
Herald Writer

A small pipe that was thought to be a bomb shut down eight blocks of downtown Hollywood near 21st Avenue and Tyler Street early Monday after a store owner found the device in his mailbox and threw it into the street.

The Broward Sheriff's Office Bomb and Arson Squad retrieved the device and determined it was a fake. Hollywood police are investigating, but have no suspects.

``The investigation is in the very early stages,'' said Todd DeAngelis, a Hollywood police spokesman.

Harold Sokoloff, owner of Quality Restaurant and Bar Equipment, 117 N. 21st Ave., arrived at work about 9:20 a.m. and found an open manila envelope in the small black mailbox beside the front door. He saw a pipe inside it.

``It appeared to be a pipe bomb,'' said Sokoloff, 39. ``I've seen them before. I threw it out of my hands and on the road. I was scared. Wouldn't you be?''

Sokoloff and his fiancee, Becky Wayne, opened the store three months ago. It specializes in constructing and outfitting restaurants with cooking equipment.

The bomb squad's efforts forced traffic to be rerouted around Hollywood Boulevard for about an hour, though it caused no major traffic snarls.

Nor did it fluster Hollywood resident Kathy McCartney, 38. She was walking through downtown when police arrived on the scene.

``It's not so surprising. It was only a matter of time till it came over here to Hollywood. What's next?'' said McCartney, who lives nearby.

David Lee, 27, a worker at Murphy Beds and Custom Furniture, 2033 Hollywood Blvd., watched as the pipe was removed.

``It's normal in Florida. It didn't faze me,'' he said.
