Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN THE WORLD

From Herald Wire Services

MIDDLE EAST

U.S. arranges meeting to discuss cease-fire

The Clinton administration brought together diplomats from Syria, Lebanon, Israel and France on Friday to make arrangements for monitoring the cease-fire between Israel and Hezbollah guerrillas in Lebanon.

Dennis Ross, chief U.S. trouble shooter for the Middle East, presided at the three-hour meeting in Washington and reported all parties had expressed an interest in setting up an effective monitoring group.

Ross said they would meet again Tuesday.

ISRAEL

U.S. Embassy issues warning on terrorism

TEL AVIV -- The U.S. Embassy said Friday that Americans traveling in the Middle East should take extra precautions following a federal court ruling allowing an Islamic leader to be extradited to Israel.

The embassy reminded Americans who live in Israel or plan to visit the country of a standing warning not to take buses or go near bus stops.

A district court in New York ruled Wednesday that a Hamas leader, Mousa Mohammed Abu Marzook, can be extradited to Israel where he is accused of funding and ordering terror attacks.

GERMANY

American neo-Nazi could get long sentence

HAMBURG -- An American neo-Nazi on trial for mailing banned anti-Semitic literature to fascists in Germany faces up to 14 years in prison, not five years as previously reported, the judge said Friday.

Judge Guenter Bertram made a point of correcting the error on the second day of Gary Lauck's trial on charges of inciting hatred and distributing illegal anti-Semitic literature.

The rest of the day was spent reading aloud from the material Lauck published in Lincoln, Neb. Lauck mailed the hate literature to Germany for two decades, until he was arrested last year at a neo-Nazi convention in Denmark, where he was no longer protected by U.S. free-speech guarantees.

  • LUEBECK -- Hanna Krabbe, 51, a convicted Red Army Faction terrorist, was released from prison Friday after serving 21 years of a life sentence for her part in the 1975 killing of two West German diplomats in Stockholm, Sweden.
