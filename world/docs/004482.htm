Published Monday, May 6, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Security around Peres getting added attention

JERUSALEM -- While most Israelis worry about the possibility of a pre-election suicide bomb, police see another threat: an attack by Jewish extremists on Prime Minister Shimon Peres.

``There's no doubt the possibility of a murder of the prime minister is a central influence on our deployment,'' said national police commander Assaf Hefetz.

A right-wing religious Jew, Yigal Amir, assassinated the late Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin exactly six months ago Saturday.

The assassination caused ``very sizable changes'' in Peres' security, Hefetz said.

Peres often wears bulletproof vests, something Rabin refused. And with only a few exceptions, security guards prohibit him from walking among crowds.

A state inquiry into the Rabin assassination found that the Shin Bet security forces ignored ample warnings that a Jewish militant might try to kill Rabin.

Hefetz insisted security forces were not taking any threats lightly; Peres has said that intelligence has revealed hundreds of potential Jewish assailants.

-- JOHN DONNELLY
