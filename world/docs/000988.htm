Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

CARDINAL SUENENS

Belgian primate, Vatican II leader

BRUSSELS, Belgium -- (AP) -- Cardinal Leon-Joseph Suenens, the former primate of Belgium and a major power broker at the Vatican II Conference in the 1960s, has died. He was 91.

Suenens, who headed the Belgian Roman Catholic Church from 1962 to 1980, died Monday.

During the reform conference at the Vatican, which ended in 1965, he was one of four presidents to lead the debates. He urged greater contacts with other Christian religions and was a proponent of an increased role for women in the church.

In 1961, he was appointed archbishop of Brussels, and the next year became cardinal for Belgium, a post he held until he retired in 1980.
