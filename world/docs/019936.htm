SHORTER SUB

DOMINGUIN

Legendary bullfighter profiled by Hemingway

MADRID -- (AP) -- Luis Miguel Dominguin, a bullfighter whose rivalry with his brother-in-law was chronicled by Ernest Hemingway, died Wednesday. He was 69.

Dominguin had stomach cancer and died of a stroke at his farm near San Roque, Spain.

His steely nerves and dark, lean looks attracted
many women, including actresses such as Ava Gardner.

Hemingway wrote of the rivalry between Dominguin and Antonio Ordoñez in The Dangerous Summer.

``Bullfighting is worthless without rivalry. ``But with two great bullfighters, it becomes a deadly rivalry,'' he wrote, because the one-upmanship can force a matador to go beyond his abilities.

That dangerous summer of 1959 ended with Dominguin being gored in the thigh. He recovered and continued fighting until 1973.

For years, Dominguin had a relationship with Gardner. He was also reportedly involved with Brigitte Bardot, Rita Hayworth, Lana Turner, Olivia de Havilland and Zsa Zsa Gabor.

He befriended artist Pablo Picasso and playwright Jean Cocteau and went on hunting trips with Gen. Francisco Franco.

Hemingway disliked his showmanship: Dominguin sometimes would kiss a stunned, wounded bull before dispatching it. He also shocked critics in the 1940s by raising his right index finger in the air in the bullring to proclaim himself No. 1.

In 1955, Dominguin married Italian actress Lucia Bose. Their son Miguel is a pop star and actor.

After separating, Dominguin married Rosario Primo de Rivera. He is survived by his wife, three children and four grandchildren.
