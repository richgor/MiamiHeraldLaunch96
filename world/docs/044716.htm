LEBANON

Israeli artillery fires
on guerrillas in south
BEIRUT -- Israeli artillery fired on guerrillas attempting to infiltrate the Israeli-occupied border enclave in southern Lebanon on Saturday, security sources reported.

There was no immediate word on casualties. It was not known to which group the infiltrators belonged, although the Iranian-backed group Hezbollah has spearheaded the guerrilla war to drive Israel from south Lebanon.

It was the second time Israeli forces have been in action in the region since a U.S.-brokered cease-fire April 26 halted 16 days of Israeli air, naval and artillery bombardment that killed 164 people and wounded about 340.

The agreement does not preclude guerrillas from attacking Israeli forces in the enclave or the Israelis from firing back, provided civilians and population centers are not targeted.

ISRAEL

Peres delaying pullout of troops from Hebron

JERUSALEM -- Prime Minister Shimon Peres has decided to postpone withdrawing troops from the West Bank city of Hebron until after Israel's May 29 elections, Israel radio said.

The government does not want to risk increased friction between Jewish settlers and Palestinians in Hebron -- the last West Bank city under Israeli occupation -- in the sensitive period ahead of the vote, Foreign Minister Ehud Barak said.

Hebron's Palestinian mayor, Mustafa Natche, reacted angrily to the government's apparent decision to retain control over his city for at least another month.

``This is killing the peace process. The Palestinians are being deceived,'' Natche said. ``This is the second time he [Peres] is postponing redeployment.''

BOSNIA-HERZEGOVINA

Soldier evacuated after self-inflicted wound

TUZLA AIR BASE -- A U.S. soldier who wounded himself in the abdomen was evacuated to Germany.

Spc. Hector Rodriguez, 28, of Joliet, Ill., was flown to the Landstuhl Regional Medical Center, hospital officials said. A spokesman said Rodriguez remained in critical but stable condition.

Rodriguez suffered ``an apparent self-inflicted wound'' Thursday, according to military officials in Tuzla, U.S. headquarters in Bosnia.
