Published Monday, May 6, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN THE WORLD

Rebels down warplane, kidnap 11 police officers

GROZNY -- Chechen rebels shot down a Russian Su-25 warplane Sunday, killing its two pilots, and kidnapped 11 police officers of the Moscow-backed Chechen government.

Also Sunday, the leader of the rebels rejected a Russian offer for peace talks, but a standoff between rebels in Shali and Russian troops besieging the town for nearly two weeks was reportedly settled with Chechens handing over their weapons.

The Russian jet fighter was brought down Sunday morning during a reconnaissance mission near the eastern village of Mairtup, Defense Ministry officials told The Associated Press.

INDIA

Premier: Elections essential for peace

JAMMU -- Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao vowed Sunday to go ahead with this month's elections in rebellious Jammu-Kashmir despite ``interference by Pakistan,'' which supports Kashmiri separatists.

It was the first visit by a prime minister to the northern state since the Muslim insurgency began in 1989. India and Pakistan have fought two wars over Kashmir, a mainly Muslim territory that straddles their border.

Winding up an election campaign in Jammu for this month's general elections, Rao said he did not rule out ``more mischief by Pakistan in Jammu and Kashmir to thwart the poll.'' Militants have urged Kashmiris to boycott the election.

ITALY

Mafia boss accused in murder is arrested

PALERMO -- A reputed top Mafia boss accused in the murder of the top Sicilian Communist Party official was arrested Sunday after four years in hiding.

Police said Salvatore Cucuzza, 49, did not resist when they arrested him in a Palermo apartment.

Mob informers have identified Cucuzza as the boss of downtown Palermo and a close associate of the now-imprisoned Salvatore ``Toto'' Riina, the mob's ``boss of bosses.''

ETC.

  • TAJIKISTAN -- Rebels fighting Tajikistan's Moscow-backed government on Saturday ambushed a car carrying four Tajik Security Ministry officials, killing three of them and injuring one, the government said Sunday.
  • AUSTRALIA -- Martin Bryant, 28, accused of killing 35 people in Tasmania on April 28, was transferred to a prison Sunday, after medical workers at a public hospital received death threats because they were treating him.
  • NORWAY -- A strike by oil workers has reduced Norway's North Sea oil production by a third, costing the government $20 million a day. Norway produces an average of three million barrels of oil a day.
  • BAHRAIN -- At least nine bombs exploded early Sunday in the capital Manama, gutting four shops and damaging five others in the latest wave of unrest in the tiny state. No one was injured. Shiite activists were suspected.
