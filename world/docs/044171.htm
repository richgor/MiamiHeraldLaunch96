Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN THE WORLD

From Herald Wire Services

INDIA

Prime Minister Rao may step down today

NEW DELHI -- After guiding India's 50-year transformation from a colonial backwater to a rising Third World power, the secular Congress Party conceded Thursday that it lost a parliamentary election to Hindu nationalists.

Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao met President Shankar Dayal Sharma and told him he would resign. Rao is expected to submit his resignation after a Cabinet meeting today.

The biggest winner in three days of voting, the Bharatiya Janata Party, started seeking partners to form a coalition government.

NETHERLANDS

Muslim prisoner pleads not guilty

THE HAGUE -- The first Muslim taken into custody by the Yugoslav war crimes tribunal pleaded not guilty Thursday to murders, rapes and torture allegedly committed against Serb inmates at a prison camp he commanded.

Zejnil Delalic, 48, was handed over to the tribunal Wednesday by German authorities who arrested him in March on tribunal orders. He was brought Thursday before the same three-judge panel hearing the first war crimes trial of the Bosnian war, which opened this week.

GERMANY

American neo-Nazi won't testify at trial

HAMBURG -- An American neo-Nazi refused Thursday to testify at his trial for sending banned anti-Semitic material to fascists in Germany, saying the charges were illegal.

Gary Lauck mailed the hate literature from his Nebraska base for two decades, until he was arrested last year at a neo-Nazi convention in Denmark, where he was no longer protected by U.S. free-speech guarantees.

Lauck, who turns 43 on Sunday, is accused of being the German fascists' main supplier of anti-Semitic brochures, films and other propaganda. Such material is illegal in Germany.

ETC.

  • ISRAEL -- More than 30,000 workers seeking higher pensions held a one-day strike Thursday, disrupting air traffic, limiting hospital services and shutting the telephone information service. The Histadrut Trade Union Federation threatened to widen the strike if no agreement is reached.
  • BAHRAIN -- A gas leak, not explosives, caused a blast Tuesday that demolished a house in a Shiite village, killing a couple and their 3-year-old son, the official Gulf News Agency reported Thursday.
