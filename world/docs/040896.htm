Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

French police bust fake ID ring linked to Muslim militants

PARIS -- (AP) -- Anti-terrorist police arrested 46 Algerians Friday, breaking open what they said was a major ring that channeled fake IDs to Muslim militants around Europe.

Some 350 officers seized cash, arms and thousands of false documents they said would have enabled militants from Algeria, Germany and Italy to travel freely.

Police said the sweeps in a Paris suburb and the southern port of Marseille demonstrated how entrenched militants have become in France, which last year suffered a series of bombings that killed eight people and wounded 160.

Algerian Muslim militants claimed responsibility for some of the attacks. The militants are angry at France for its support of the government in its former North African colony.

A four-year drive to overthrow Algeria's secularist rule and replace it with an Islamic government has claimed more than 40,000 lives. The Armed Islamic Group -- the most violent of Algeria's factions -- has repeatedly threatened to carry out attacks on French soil.

On Thursday, French police arrested the alleged ringleader: Salem Nassah, 35, an Algerian-born junior high school physics instructor in the western suburb of Sartrouville.

Police told the Paris daily Le Monde that Nassah was suspected of leading the false ID ring for at least a year. Many of the falsified documents were sold for up to $2,000 apiece, authorities said.

The French government has arrested hundreds of Muslim suspects in scores of raids since last year's bombings.

Most of the suspects have been released and never charged, sparking criticism that authorities are using the terrorist threat as a pretext to harass Muslims.
