Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

U.N. unlikely to condemn Israel for firing on Lebanon camp

UNITED NATIONS -- (AP) -- Despite a report suggesting that Israel intentionally fired on a U.N. camp in Lebanon, Arab diplomats are finding little support on the U.N. Security Council for a strong condemnation.

On Wednesday, the day after the U.N. report was released, Lebanese Ambassador Samir Moubarak said he would submit a resolution denouncing Israel for the April 18 shelling of the U.N. garrison at Qana, which killed at least 91 civilians.

Israel has said the shelling was accidental, and that its fire was aimed at Hezbollah guerrilla positions near the camp.

The U.N. report said the pattern of artillery shell hits ``is inconsistent with a normal overshooting'' and that ``it is unlikely'' the barrage on the U.N. compound was an accident.

But after a full day of lobbying, an Arab diplomat speaking on condition of anonymity said that only four of the 15 Security Council members -- China, Guinea-Bissau, Egypt and Honduras -- were prepared to support Lebanon's resolution.
