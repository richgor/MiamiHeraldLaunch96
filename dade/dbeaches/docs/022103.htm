Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Here's one for the road: Tipsy revelers get free ride, tow

By RICK JERVIS
Herald Staff Writer

Beach Towing, traditionally a thorn in the side of Miami Beach residents and revelers, is trying to make life easier for them.

The company will pick up Beach residents and tourists who have had too much to drink and take them and their car home.

No questions, no charge.

``Around 75 percent of the people you go to pick up are drunk,'' said Tony Rivera, manager of the tow company at 1349 Dade Blvd. ``We're just trying to do something different, trying to help the people. If you drink and drive, you go to jail. We try to avoid that.''

The offer extends only to Beach residents or tourists vacationing at area hotels. Callers need to have a car, he said, and be willing to wait about 45 minutes on weekends.

Rivera said Beach Towing has been offering this service for years but had never publicized it. Thus, few people knew about it.

Recently, the company began posting signs of the service on the side of pay phones, handing out fliers.

``I think it's a great service,'' said Dave Kolstad, manager at Rose's Bar and Music Lounge, 754 Washington Ave. ``Any community could use it, but especially us, since we're so much into the club and bar scene. There's a lot of drunks on the road.''

Rivera said his company was prompted to offer the service after witnessing several accidents involving drunk drivers.

``If you call us, you won't go to jail, you won't get into an accident and you don't pay for the towing,'' Rivera said. ``Just don't throw up in the truck.''
