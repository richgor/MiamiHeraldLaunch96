Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

City officials outraged by firm's request

Group under contract asks for extra $24,000

By RICK JERVIS
Herald Staff Writer

Miami Beach commissioners blasted a local architectural firm Wednesday for requesting an extra $24,000 in fees from the city.

Bermello, Ajamil and Partners was hired by the city last year to create a master plan of park improvements in a contract worth hundreds of thousands of dollars. Wednesday's request, placed on the agenda by the city manager's office, asked that the architects be paid an additional $24,000 for meetings and workshops they held for the public recently and for a tour it gave commissioners of city parks.

The request was unanimously shot down by commissioners.

``This project is too big for them to be hitting us with these petty cash bills,'' Mayor Seymour Gelber said. ``It shows a lack of professionalism to me.''

The architects were chosen to create a master plan that will outline use of $15 million in bond funds approved by voters in 1994 to improve Miami Beach's parks and greenspace.

The contract required the firm to hold 18 or 20 public workshops, the architects said, which in turn spawned more meetings and workshops. The $24,000 bill was mostly for meetings the firm had to attend ``above and beyond the contract.''

``We feel these were additional meetings,'' said Rai A. Fernandez, an architect with the firm. ``Not only meetings but incorporating the public's comments and redrawing plans.''

The tour the company gave commissioners earlier this year represented only a small portion of the costs, Fernandez said.

``In my opinion there must have been a misunderstanding,'' he said. ``We're in the process of clarifying that.''

Commissioners said they were caught by surprise by the request.

``It came out of left field for all of us,'' Commissioner Susan Gottlieb said. ``That money should go into the parks program not into the designers who are doing the plan. This should not have been an extra charge.''

Fernandez said his firm is currently justifying the request and hopes to hear from the city administration soon.

Getting it past the commission, however, may be difficult.

``This is a tremendous project that we're undertaking with them,'' Mayor Gelber said. ``To find additional sources of revenue for themselves just didn't seem appropriate.''
