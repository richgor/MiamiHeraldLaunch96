Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Villa Deli closes; former owner to reopen it Monday

By DEBRA FRANCO
Herald Staff Writer

Last week, the Villa Deli almost went the way of its erstwhile clientele.

Tuesday, the deli appeared to die quietly and peacefully, an old relic among hip new neighbors that had moved in along Lincoln Road.

A sign on the front door said the deli closed for ``economic reasons,'' but promised to re-open soon. Inside, the flickering neon sign advertising Hebrew National hotdogs glowed in the dark. Half-empty bottles of ketchup stood like lone sentinels, placed in the center of every table, awaiting the return of customers.

Although owners Stephen and Larry Schatzman tried to keep pace with the changes around South Beach, Villa Deli, 1608 Alton Rd., just wasn't attracting customers like it used to.

``All of my customers died or moved away,'' he said. ``The elderly Jewish population is gone completely.''

Also, competition among restaurants has grown stiffer. More than 200 restaurants in the South Beach area offer an array of food 24 hours a day, with more than 20 lining Lincoln Road alone.

``When we bought the restaurant, there were maybe 15 restaurants open on the Beach,'' Schatzman said. ``Now it's difficult to get what's left of the business because it's so diluted by the number of restaurants that are there.''

Former owner Phil Greenbaum disagreed. Greenbaum owned the deli for eight years and sold it to the Schatzmans in 1990.

``Obviously all the franchise operators who came to Miami Beach must think otherwise,'' he said. ``The location is prime. It was before all these new restaurants opened up, and it still is.''

When Greenbaum heard about the deli's demise, he stepped in to breathe new life into the business.

``I know what was here before and I'm going to try to reproduce it,'' he said. ``I'm coming back in to try to re-establish it as it was. It had always been rated one of -- if not the best -- delies in Miami. I hope to be able to reproduce that.''

The deli is scheduled to re-open at 6 a.m. Monday with just a few changes. The hours have been scaled back -- dinner will no longer be served. But the prices will be a bit less, Greenbaum promises.

To celebrate the deli's rebirth, Villa Deli's famous breakfast special will be re-instated. For $1.29 plus tax, early risers get two eggs with a choice of potatoes or grits, a bagel or toast, cream cheese or butter, and coffee or tea.

``We'll see how long we can hold out at that price without losing money,'' Greenbaum said.
