Into their helping hands, a token of thanks

Seven Dade volunteers win Golden Rule Awards

By BEA MOSS
Herald Staff Writer

They help the elderly, are advocates for children and work to help rebuild neighborhoods. They are volunteers who give of their time and talents to help make the entire community a better place.

They are the nominees, finalists and winners of the JCPenney Golden Rule Awards. Friends and relatives from across the county honored them during a breakfast April 26 at the Radisson Mart Hotel.

More than 500 people attended the ceremonies.

Maria Cao-Lopez, director of volunteer services at Jackson Memorial Hospital and the president of Dade County National Volunteer Week, said the judges had a tough time paring down almost 150 nominees to seven winners.

Each winner received a crystal sculpture and each winner's organization received $1,000.

The JCPenney Golden Rule Award recognizes local volunteers and organizations who do outstanding community service.
