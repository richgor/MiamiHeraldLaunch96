Published Sunday, May 12, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Area wants to be part of Coral Gables

Snapper Creek Lakes residents are thismuchcloser to becoming the newest Coral Gables citizens.

The Metro-Dade County Commission approved the annexation of the subdivision -- bordered by Southwest 57th Avenue, Snapper Creek Canal, Old Cutler Road and roughly 105th Street -- if a majority of the electors vote ``yes'' by June 25. Ballots are to be mailed in.

Snapper Creek Lakes -- which has 123 homes and an estimated population of 330 -- would be the second neighborhood to be annexed into the Gables this year. In April, Pine Bay Estates residents voted overwhelmingly to become part of the city. Almost 100 voters said yes; 21 said no.

Those living in Hammock Lakes will probably get to decide whether to join the Gables in August. The second public hearing before the annexation is June 11, when final County Commission approval is expected.

-- ELAINE DE VALLE
