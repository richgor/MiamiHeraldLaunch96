Published Sunday, May 12, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

House under construction robbed

Someone broke the sliding glass door of a home under construction in the 15700 block of Southwest 85th Street between 4:30 p.m. Friday and 7:15 a.m. Monday and stole a $300 dishwasher, an air conditioner worth $800, and an $800 air compressor.


CALUSA SHORES

SOMEONE CUT THROUGH the screen on a balcony of a residence in the 9200 block of Southwest 138th Place between 1 and 4:30 p.m. Monday and stole a Raleigh bicycle worth $400.


CORAL GABLES

A GABLES COUPLE stopped at a red light on Riviera Drive at South Dixie Highway were robbed by an occupant in the car behind them about 5:35 p.m. Sunday. The couple were driving north on Riviera in their 1993 Chrysler New Yorker when they stopped at the light. A man age 19 to 21 got out of a black specially outfitted 1996 Mitsubishi Eclipse behind them at the light, ran up to the passenger window, broke the glass and reached inside. He grabbed the 62-year-old woman's $30 purse which contained $40, her keys and several credit cards. The couple tried to follow the suspects, but lost them in the vicinity of Grand Avenue. When they returned home, the woman collapsed and had to be taken to Mercy Hospital.

SOMEONE SNATCHED a woman's $500 Louis Vuitton purse from her shoulder as she unlocked her car door in a swale in the 400 block of Alminar Avenue about 7 p.m. May 2. The robber also snatched the victim's $220 sunglasses off her head. Inside the purse: $100, identification, credit cards, checks and the victim's phone book.

BURGLARS KICKED down the front door of a house in the 200 block of Alesio Avenue between 10:30 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. May 3, stealing a $400 television and a $500 stereo. A neighbor said he had seen an old faded or rusted red vehicle parked in the driveway.

A 12-GAUGE SHOTGUN was stolen from a house in the 3900 block of Riviera Drive between noon April 28 and 2 p.m. April 29. The gun was valued at $100. A $5 piggy bank with about $90 in coins and bills was also stolen.

THIEVES PULLED out the left rear door window of a 1991 Honda Accord and reached in to unlock the door as it was parked in the 100 block of Sarto Avenue between 8:30 p.m. Monday and 7 a.m. Tuesday. They pried open the glove box and stole a $310 cellular phone that was inside.

SOMEONE BROKE into a 1994 Lexus parked in the 4200 block of Salzedo Street between 11 and 11:30 a.m. Wednesday, stealing a portable phone valued at $30 and three compact discs valued at a total of $45.

THIEVES SNEAKED over a low fence into the back yard of a house in the 1000 block of Asturia Avenue between 10 a.m. and 9 p.m. April 26. Stolen: a $300 barbecue grill cover and barbell weights valued at $200.

SOMEONE SMASHED the rear passenger vent window of a 1993 Toyota Corolla parked in the 200 block of Sidonia Avenue between 9:20 p.m. April 21 and 7:30 a.m. April 22. The interior was ransacked and a stereo with compact disc player valued at $300 was stolen.

WOULD-BE BURGLARS CUT the screen door at the rear of a residence in the first block of Santander Avenue and pushed in the southeast window at 9:26 a.m. April 22. The intruders set off the alarm and ran away without getting inside the house.

ABOUT $3,000 IN TOOLS, a $900 video camera and other items were stolen when thieves smashed the front right window of a 1993 Lincoln Villager van parked in the rear alley lot in the 4000 block of Aurora Street between 5:30 and 8:50 p.m. April 18. A gym bag with gym clothes, valued at $200, and a $100 Walkman were also taken.

SOMEONE STOLE a $100 cellular phone and its $15 case from the front seat of a 1995 Ford Ranger while the driver was paying for gas at the Mobil station, 390 Bird Rd., at 9 a.m. April 18.

VANDALS SMASHED the front passenger window of a 1994 Infiniti and stole a $300 cellular phone from inside as the car was parked in the 600 block of Fluvia Avenue between 8 and 10 p.m. April 22.

THE ALARM SCARED a would-be burglar from entering a home in the 5800 block of Riviera Drive where he or she had forced the double French doors open between 5 p.m. April 17 and 5:18 a.m. April 18.

BURGLARS ENTERED a house in the 200 block of Calabria Avenue between 8:45 a.m. and 6:45 p.m. April 24, stealing a $2,000 stereo system and 300 compact discs valued at $3,000.


THE CROSSINGS

SOMEONE ENTERED a home in the 11400 block of Southwest 133rd Court at 6 p.m. Monday through unknown means. A Citibank Visa card was stolen.


SOUTH MIAMI

SOMEONE STOLE a 1990 white Buick Century from a home in the 7400 block of Southwest 62nd Avenue at about 10 p.m. Tuesday.

A BURGLAR PRIED open the side kitchen door of a home in the 6600 block of Southwest 61st Terrace between 6:30 p.m. Monday and 5 p.m. Tuesday. Two African parrots valued at $1,200 were stolen.

SOMEONE BROKE into a shed in the back yard of a home in the 6700 block of Southwest 75th Terrace at about 5:30 p.m. Tuesday and stole a red 10-speed bicycle valued at $75.

A 1995 DODGE CARAVAN was stolen at the intersection of Southwest 73rd Street and 59th Court between noon and 1 p.m. Tuesday. The white caravan is valued at $10,000.


SOUTHWEST DADE
A BURGLAR REMOVED screws from the rear door of a home in the 15300 block of Southwest 70th Lane between 10 a.m. and 3 p.m. Monday. Stolen were a $500 remote control car and a $50 wheel.


WEST KENDALL

A BURGLAR ENTERED an open garage at a home in the 15600 block of Southwest 95th Street between 4 and 5 p.m. Monday and stole two Huffy bicycles worth $400.

A BURGLAR ENTERED a home in the 15900 block of Southwest 75th Street through a side window between 11:40 a.m. and 3:40 p.m. Monday and stole a $400 Sony VCR, $1,000 worth of jewelry and a $100 Walkman.


PRINCETON

FOUR ROBBERS DROVE up to a man when he pulled over at Southwest 117th Avenue and 240th Street to check a tire of his 1992 Acura Legend at 7:50 p.m. Wednesday.

``Need any help?'' they asked, and pointed guns at him. They got out of a tan Toyota Corolla and grabbed him, took his Rolex watch, portable phone, and wallet containing $300 cash, and threw him on the ground. They then got into both cars and drove away.


GOULDS

BURGLARS PRIED open the rear sliding glass door of a home on the 20800 block of Southwest 103rd Court between 8 a.m. and 3 p.m. Wednesday and stole jewelry worth $6,000, $500 cash and electrical appliances.


FLORIDA CITY

SOMEONE SHOT AT THE store front window of Farah Factory Store, 250 E. Palm Dr., No. 410, between 9:30 p.m. April 30 and 9 a.m. May 1, causing $300 in damage. A BB gun was found on the store front sidewalk.


COLONIAL DRIVE AREA

SOMEONE SMASHED the window of a car parked at a home on the 10800 block of Southwest 165th Street at about 3:40 a.m., but stole nothing.


CUTLER RIDGE

TWO MEN ARGUED with a jewelry salesman Wednesday at 12:05 p.m. in the McDonald's parking lot, 10851 Caribbean Blvd, about jewelry he had sold to a woman. When one punched the salesman in the head and tried to take his black fanny pouch that was filled with jewelry, the salesman pulled out a knife and stabbed him in the stomach. Police arrested James Henry Hall, 24, 410 NW 35th St., and charged him with strong-arm robbery and took him to Jackson Memorial Hospital. Police also arrested Ulysses Euline, 22, 28307 SW 141st Pl., and charged him with strong-arm robbery.
