Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Glass-door crashers take TV, stereo

Someone smashed the sliding-glass door of a home in the 6500 block of Southwest 54th Lane between 1 and 3:55 p.m. Tuesday. A $700 Panasonic 27-inch TV and a $1,100 Sony stereo system with speakers were stolen. Damage to the glass door was estimated at $200.


CORAL GABLES

A THIEF SNATCHED the purse of a housekeeper as she was reporting to work at a home in the 400 block of Bargello Avenue about 9:50 a.m. April 28. Someone approached her from behind as she took items out of the trunk and grabbed her $25 purse from her hand. There were no valuables inside.

BURGLARS BROKE into a home in the 400 block of Navarre Avenue through the rear French doors between 7:41 and 7:51 p.m. April 27, stealing a $500 VCR. A neighbor said he had seen a man, age 16 to 18, knock on the victim's door and go around to the back yard when nobody answered. Another neighbor saw a burgundy car, possibly a BMW, parked in front of the victim's home about 6:30 p.m.

SOMEONE STOLE a $200 oak-stained wood bench from the front porch of a home in the 800 block of Mariana Avenue between 11 and 11:15 p.m. April 26.

THIEVES ENTERED a home under construction in the 600 block of Destacada Avenue between 4 p.m. April 24 and 10 a.m. April 26, stealing a $1,500 stove top and a $700 microwave oven.

SOMEONE TOOK a dark gray 1991 Nissan truck from in front of a house in the 4400 block of Santa Maria Street between 12:15 and 12:20 a.m. April 25.

THIEVES USED A tool to remove the driver's side window of a 1995 Ford Explorer XLT parked in the driveway of a Pine Bay Estates house in the 5900 block of Southwest 116th Street just after 1 a.m. Monday. They stole a $350 Bazooka stereo speaker.

A MAN EXPOSED himself to a 15-year-old girl as she walked home from Coral Gables High about 2:50 p.m. April 25. She was walking in the 200 block of Alesio Avenue when she noticed the man, in his 20s, sitting in a Mazda MX3 and masturbating. She then crossed the street and continued walking. The man then approached her and said, ``I just want you to look at it.'' She then saw a fellow student who lived nearby and went over there to use the phone to call police. The man left the area. An officer took the girl home.

THIEVES STOLE 100 baseballs from the north side locker room at the Coral Gables Youth Center, 400 Anastasia Ave., about 2:15 a.m. April 28. A man who lives in an apartment across the street saw two teenagers removing the baseballs and a third driving a 1980s Monte Carlo around the block several times.
SOMEONE CUT a chain-link fence on the north side of Fairchild Tropical Garden, 10901 Old Cutler Rd., between 7 p.m. April 28 and 7 a.m. Monday and drove an unknown vehicle on the property. It was unknown if anything was stolen.


COUNTRY WALK

A 1995 TOYOTA CAMRY was stolen from the driveway of a home in the 14800 block of Southwest 139th Court between 4 and 7:30 a.m. Thursday.


DADELAND AREA

A THIEF STOLE some speakers from the open garage of a home in the 5400 block of Southwest 85th Street at 9:35 a.m. April 28. The property was valued at $600.


THE FALLS AREA

A THIEF STOLE flatware valued $4,000 from a home in the 7400 block of Southwest 142nd Terrace between 8:25 a.m. and 12:05 p.m. April 26. A door had been pried open.


THE HAMMOCKS

A ROCK WAS THROWN through the back window of a home in the 10000 block of Southwest 147th Place between 4 and 6:15 p.m. Wednesday. About $1,000 worth of jewelry is missing.

SOMEONE BROKE INTO a home through the garage door in the 10900 block of Southwest 146th Avenue between 3:40 and 4:15 p.m. Wednesday and stole $500 worth of cloth and $200 in jewelry.


KENDALE LAKES

SOMEONE BROKE INTO a 1989 Honda Accord at a home in the 14100 block of Southwest 87th Street between 11 p.m. April 24 and 8:30 a.m. April 25. Stolen were $200 worth of books, a $300 stereo, a Bazooka Roadmaster valued at $450, a $600 Sherwood amplifier and $300.


KENDALL

SOMEONE CLIMBED a wall to a second floor balcony of an apartment in the 10400 block of Southwest 88th Street between 10:30 p.m. April 28 and 12:30 a.m. Monday. A $500 memory disc, a $500 camcorder and a $75 watch were taken.

A BURGLAR KICKED in the door and ransacked a town home in the 10600 block of Southwest 76th Terrace between 8:30 a.m. and 5:25 p.m. April 27 and stole an unknown amount of jewelry. Damage to the home was set at $100.

A BURGLAR ENTERED an apartment in the 10800 block of Southwest 84th Street between 12:10 and 1 a.m. April 28 and stole a $900 necklace, a $300 wedding band, a $150 watch, a $250 diamond necklace and a $250 bracelet. The front door had been left unlocked.


PINE ACRES

A THIEF ENTERED a home in the 10800 block of Southwest 123rd Street between 3 and 5 a.m. Tuesday and stole two fishing rods valued at $500. The garage door had been left open.


RICHMOND HEIGHTS

A BURGLAR STOLE a man's and a woman's bicycle from the garage of a home in the 9400 block of Southwest 151st Street between 4:30 and 5 p.m. Friday. The bicycles are worth $359.


SOUTH MIAMI

SOMEONE BROKE into Sunrise Coin Laundry, 6228 S. Dixie Hwy. between 8 p.m. April 26 and 7:30 a.m. April 27. A $50 Pioneer cassette radio and $300 from the cash register were stolen. The burglar got inside by removing the front glass door panel from the frame. Damage to the frame was estimated at $100.

A BURGLAR BROKE into a home in the 6400 block of Southwest 63rd Street between 6:30 and 9:30 a.m. April 2. A $50 Craftsman electric drill was stolen. The burglar removed the glass window from the rear door of the house, pushed out the screen and unlocked the door.


SOUTHWEST DADE

A BURGLAR BROKE into a home in the 10400 block of Southwest 68th Street between 10:35 and 11:55 a.m. April 26 and stole an $8,000 computer, a $600 videocassette recorder, $600 in golf equipment, a $500 fax machine, a $300 gold chain, a $100 gun, a $40 wallet and $1,000.

This list is a sampling of crime in unincorporated Dade County and various cities. The information is taken from official police reports and compiled by Herald reporters.
