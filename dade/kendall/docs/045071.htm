Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

IN ACTION AT ITS REGULAR MEETING MONDAY, THE SWEETWATER COUNCIL:

  • Passed a resolution revising the city's annual budget to include revenue of $14,150 and spending it on computer upgrading ($4,150) in the finance department and city vehicle maintenance ($10,000).

  • Voted to spend $3,000 from the sale of several surplus police vehicles to fix two Harley Davidson police motorcycles.

  • Selected Humana to provide health insurance for city employees.

  • Approved a $380 payment from the Law Enforcement Trust Fund to Scott Sakin for services related to forfeiture cases.

  • Authorized $777.12 from the Law Enforcement Trust Fund to pay overtime for two Sweetwater detectives working surveillance in a narcotics investigation.

  • Entered into a sublease agreement -- for $1 -- with Don Pan and West Flagler Shopping Plaza to locate the ``Welcome to Sweetwater'' sign at Southwest 107th Avenue and West Flagler Street.

  • Adopted the evaluation and appraisal report of the city's comprehensive master plan.

  • Upgraded parks and recreation employee Jose Padron from part-time to full-time status.

  • Entered into a mutual aid agreement with Opa-locka Police.

  • Adopted an agreement with the Florida Department of Motor Vehicles to allow self-inspection of city vehicles for emissions testing.
