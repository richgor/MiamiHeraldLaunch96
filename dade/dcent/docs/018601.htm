Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

NEW BUSINESSES

DOT BRADY GALLERY: See the work of more than 20 artists at 201 N. Krome Ave. in Homestead.


CALIFORNIA COLORTIME: This `paint your own pottery' studio opened in Coconut Grove.


EAGLE ARMS & MILITARY COLLECTIBLES: Get your gun repaired or shop for military memorabilia here.


HAMPTON INN COCONUT GROVE/SOUTH MIAMI: Rent one of the 137 rooms at 2800 SW 28th Ter.


DOT BRADY GALLERY

A new art gallery has opened at 201 N. Krome Ave., Homestead. Owners Dot Brady, Ann Pelt and Lucy Hawkins offer works by more than 20 artists including paintings, sculptures, ceramics, metal works and much more. Design and art consultation are available at the gallery. Hours are 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Tuesday through Saturday. Call 242-0250.
CALIFORNIA COLORTIME

California Colortime is an alternative ``paint your own pottery'' studio now open at 3133 Commodore Plaza, Coconut Grove. Owner Caroline Dadlani and her three employees can help customers choose from a wide array of unpainted bisque pottery pieces. Customers can use a variety of brushes and paints at one of 12 work stations. The pieces are glazed and fired overnight in a kiln. All are ready within 24 hours. Hours are 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. Sunday through Wednesday, 10 a.m. to midnight Thursday through Saturday. Call 446--3960.
DDD SPORTS, INC.

DDD Sports is a home-based business licensed to sell official wear for umpires or referees of baseball, basketball, football, softball and soccer. Distributor Steven Briner has Elbeco shirts, umpire plate shoes, Spotbill shoes, Pony shoes, NFL and NCAA socks, shin guards, face masks, chest protectors, zebra shirts, knickers, hats and other items to complete the officials' uniform. Call 385-5062. Hours are 5:30 to 10 p.m. daily. Call 1-800-637-6796 any time.
EAGLE ARMS & MILITARY COLLECTIBLES

Now open under new ownership, the Eagle Arms & Military Collectibles at 14123 S. Dixie Hwy. is owned by Hank Zalkin and Barbara Austin. This full service gun shop also does repairs, rebluing and chroming. It sells military memorabilia, metal detectors, security and police products, including bulletproof vests. Merchandise is accepted on consignment. Special orders and export are available. Concealed weapons and private courses are offered. Hours are 10 a.m. to 6:30 p.m. weekdays, 9 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Saturday. The phone is 234-8446. The fax is 234-8155.
HAMPTON INN COCONUT GROVE/SOUTH MIAMI

The new 137-room Hampton Inn has opened at 2800 SW 28th Ter. is less than a block from the Coconut Grove Metrorail Station just off U.S. 1. It offers a free continental breakfast, free local telephone calls and free parking. Telephones are equipped with computer data ports and voice mail. An exercise room, swimming pool, whirlpool and meeting facilities are available. Developed and owned by Coral Gables lawyer Bernard Wolfson, the inn employs 30 people. Call 448-2800 any time. Fax 448-1513.
MOORE & SAADE VIOLINS

This store is at 6915 Red Rd., Suite 209, Coral Gables. Owners Thomas D. Moore and Jorge M. Saade buy, sell and repair violins, violas, cellos and bass. They carry everything from Suzuki-sized instruments through the finest of Italian instruments and offer music and accessories for all stringed instruments. They have a repair department and are affiliated with Bein & Fushi from Chicago. Hours are 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. weekdays and 10 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. Saturday. Call 665-9802 for an appointment or information. Fax 665-7803.
PERFUMES, ETC...

This new shop has opened in the Kendall Mall at 8819 SW 107th Ave. It offers name brand fragrances, cosmetics, bath products and sunglasses. A cosmetologist is available by appointment. Owners are Belkys and Robert Neuman. Hours are 11 a.m. Monday through Saturday, noon to 5 p.m. Sunday. Call 596-7011 or 596-5676.
SOUTH BEACH
HEALTH FOOD

The fourth South Beach Health Food store has opened at 8855 SW 107th Ave., at the Kendall Mall. The store occupies 3,500 square feet and sells vitamins, health foods, food supplements and books on diet and nutrition. Licensed and trained nutritionist are on staff. Owned by the Pharmed Group founded by three Kendall residents. Hours are 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. Saturday, 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sunday. Call 596--4533.
JAMES F. TRACY, D.P.M
OLGA GARCIA LUEPSCHEN, D.P.M.

Drs. James Tracy and Olga Garcia Luepschen have opened this practice at 8900 Coral Way, Suite 209. Specialties include children's foot deformities and sports medicine in addition to foot and ankle care and surgery. House calls can be arranged for debilitated or incapacitated patients. Hours are 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday through Friday. Evening or Saturday hours by appointment. Call 552-5545. Available 24 hours a day for emergencies.
WILD THINGS ACADEMY

An in-home animal training company that offers obedience training for dogs and other species. Owners Marcia Henton, C. Graham Coords and Elaine Allen have five trainers on staff. Members of the staff have past training experience at Miami Seaquarium, Parrot Jungle and Metrozoo. They apply positive reinforcement techniques for training and will work with any animal at any age. A six week, $50 canine obedience workshop covers basic behavior such as sit, down, stay, come and an introduction to leash work. In-home service can deal with breaking problem behaviors or teaching new behaviors according to need. Hours are 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. Monday through Saturday. Call 261-3129.
