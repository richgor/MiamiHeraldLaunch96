Sweetwater voters getting say in September on how city runs

Sweetwater voters get say on how city runs

By ELAINE DE VALLE
Herald Staff Writer

In September, voters in Sweetwater will have more to say about how their city government is run.

The City Council agreed Monday to put three charter changes on the ballot that will determine:

  • How council vacancies are filled.

  • If a city employee who is fired can appeal to the City Council.

  • If council members be elected by group seats.

    All are amendments to the city charter and must be approved by a majority of voters.

    Councilwoman Prisca Barreto moved to change how council vacancies are filled. Currently, if a council member leaves before a term expires, the council must chose someone from the pool of unsuccessful candidates in the last election to serve out the term.

    Barreto's proposal would automatically grant the seat to the unsuccessful candidate who had the next highest number of total votes.

    ``This way, the voters of Sweetwater will have a direct effect on who becomes a council member,'' Barreto said.

    Councilman Manuel Duasso was the only no vote.

    Councilman Manuel L. Maroño presented the change to let employees appeal their terminations to the council.

    ``I bring this up so that employees won't feel their position has anything to do with politics,'' Maroño said. ``So they are not terminated due to politics and so they know we value them for their work.''

    Mayor Gloria Bango, who has the authority to fire anyone at will, did not recommend the change. She said employees already approach council members about their bosses or co-workers in an effort to get their sympathy.

    ``This will be opening it up to more problems,'' Bango said.

    If passed, the amendment would allow the City Council to overrule any termination by the mayor as long as five of the seven members agree.

    Council President Cecilia Holtz Alonso and Councilman Jesus Mesa voted no.

    Councilman Jose Diaz suggested the group elections amendment.

    Currently, council members run at large and the top vote-getters are elected. Diaz's proposal would require candidates to file for a particular group and run against only those in the same group. Those running unopposed would automatically be elected.

    That resolution passed 4-3 with Holtz Alonso, Duasso and Mesa voting no.
