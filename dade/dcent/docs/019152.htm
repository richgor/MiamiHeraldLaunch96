Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Jewish community on `heightened alert' after threat

By CHARLES RABIN
Herald Staff Writer

Kendall's Jewish community is taking Sunday's FBI warning to beef up security very seriously.

Late last week, the FBI advised Jewish organizations to be on ``heightened alert'' because of a threat to kill 1,200 Jewish executives and doctors in the United States unless Israel withdrew its military forces from Lebanon and paid $12 billion in compensation by last Sunday.

To whom the compensation was to be paid wasn't clear.

The FBI said it learned of the threat through information it received through a private party, which then turned it over to the FBI.

``The Alper JCC, along with all other local Jewish agencies and synagogues, has been asked to review security measures,'' said Jacob Solomon, executive vice president of the Greater Miami Jewish Federation.

``The truth is we've been on heightened security for the past several months. The letter really doesn't make much difference. All the organizations are just making sure their security is adequate.''

The Jewish Federation was host to a number of Jewish groups and the FBI for a briefing at the Alper Center Wednesday morning.

Pinecrest's Temple Beth Am, like the Alper Center, has hired some Metro-Dade police officers to make extra rounds.

``We're just trying to make people cognizant of the fact that there's someone out there that doesn't like us,'' the temple's executive director, Gerald Taub, said of his 1,400-family congregation.

``There's a risk everywhere you go,'' said Stacey Stieg, who belongs to Kendall's JCC with her husband, Kenny, and 2-year-old son, Jacob. ``But I'm not that worried about it. It won't stop me from doing what I want to do.''
