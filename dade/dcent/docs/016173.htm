Gables FBLA members take awards

These students from Coral Gables High attended the Future Business Leaders of America Florida State Leadership Conference April 21-24 in Haines City: Luke Dixon, first place Business Mathematics; Alfredo Delgado, first place Computer Concepts; Juan Ramirez, second place Computer Concepts; Deborah Egurrola, third place Economics. Dixon, Delgado and Ramirez will represent Florida in their respective divisions at the National Leadership Conference July 8-11 in Washington, D.C. The Coral Gables Chapter also received the Gold Seal Chapter Award, the State Project Award and the March of Dimes Project Award.

Poster winners

Edwin Rivas of W.R. Thomas Middle won first place in the state and local ``Drop Savers'' poster contest for the middle school division.

Jose Gonzalez of Palmetto Elementary won the county contest in the third-through-fifth grade category and placed third in the state. Both boys will receive a $50 U.S. savings bond, a trophy and a framed copy of their artwork. The contest was sponsored locally by the Miami-Dade Water and Sewer Department.

SCHOOL SCENE

Braddock High: The PTSA will meet and honor the Challenger Patrol at an award ceremony at 7:30 p.m. Tuesday in the media center. New PTSA officers will be elected. The school is at 3601 SW 147th Ave. Call 220-9400.

Carver Middle: The book fair will be from 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. today through Wednesday and from 7 to 9 p.m. Tuesday. There will also be a PTSA meeting Tuesday. The school is at 4901 Lincoln Dr. in Coconut Grove. Call 444-7388.

Coral Park/Sunset High: The schools are cosponsoring a dinner and music event from 4 to 9 p.m. May 19 at the Gables Banquet Hall, 7360 SW 24th St. Entertainment will be provided by the jazz bands of both schools. The cost is $7.50 per adult and $5 per child. Proceeds will benefit the music programs at each school. For tickets call 388-0857.

Emerson Elementary: The Spring Book Fair will be Mondaythrough Fridayin the library. The school is at 8001 SW 36th St. Call 264-5757.

International Student Exchange: ASSE International Student Exchange is seeking local host families for 15- to 18-year-old students from various countries. Students are fully insured and brings their own spending money. Families may select their exchange student from applications including biographical essays, photos and transcripts. For more information, call Joan Soderqvist at 341-9389 or at 1-800-473-0696.

Olympia Heights Elementary: There will be a parent meeting for new kindergarten students at 9 a.m. Wednesday. The first grade meeting will be at 10 a.m. The school is at 9797 SW 40th St. Call 221-3821.

Ponce de Leon Middle: A general PTSA meeting will be at 7 p.m. Tuesday at the Coral Gables Police Department, 2801 Salzedo St. Refreshments will be served at 6:30 p.m. David Williams, Dade County School Board candidate, will speak about his involvement with the PTA. Anne Thompson, Florida PTA President, will speak on leadership roles. Reservations required. Call Sheila Lawless at 661-7082 or Debbie Korge at 670-2069.

Royal Green Elementary: The kindergarten orientation will be at 2:30 p.m. May 22 in the school's kindergarten wing. The school is at 13047 SW 47th St. Call 221-4452.
