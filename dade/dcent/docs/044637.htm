Coral Gables senior wins $500 scholarship

Sharu Mundell-Williams, a senior at Coral Gables High, won first place in the National Conference of Christians and Jews First Annual Media Awards Contest. Senior Manny Navarro from Braddock High placed second in the contest. Sharu will receive a $500 scholarship and Manny will receive a $200 scholarship. Both students will be honored at a luncheon later this month.
MAST scientists

The following MAST Academy students earned distinction at the State Science Fair: Lance Hall, Seth Mondschein and Maria Spielman, first place; Edward Prieto, second place; Noel Mack, third place; and David Bernstein, fourth place. Edward Prieto's research on asthma in adolescents has been published in the April 1996 American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine.
Cypress scientists

Cypress Elementary Science Fair winners are: First place -- Iris Herrera's kindergarten class, Thomas Rio, Keandra Cordero, Emanuel Ribas, Ericka McKenna, Carly Paulen, Bryan Whittington, Yamely Carbot and Anne Perez. Second place -- Josephina Bonet's kindergarten class, Matthew Landau, Gabriella Seara, Regina Bermudez, Jessica Pagan, Lindsey Cruz, Jonathan Addison, Marta Pagan, Kristen Purvis, and Mario Radillo. Third place -- Mayelin Perez's kindergarten class, Stormy Rains, Christian Villamor, Paul Moore, Alexander Iglesias, Christina Cougil, Kristy Phillips and Christina Villamor. An Honorable mention was awarded to Marilyn Seara's kindergarten class.
Leisure City winners

These students at Leisure City Elementary were recognized for participating in the National and State Radiant Peace Foundation's Peace Essay Contest: Gilbert Rosales, David Anguiano, Yulianna Alcala, Sintia Vazquez, Jose Avalos, Gustavo Garcia, Alma Olvera, Maria Garcia, David Garcia, Jorge DeAnda, Juanita Morales and Isaac Gonzalez.
Hammocks Middle helps

More than 500 trees and plants were purchased and planted by Hammocks Middle students during Earth Week. Rose Bailey and her EcoAction Science Club sponsored and organized the event.
Rockway makes music

Rockway Middle's Signing Singers will perform at the America Sings! festival in Washington D.C. Saturday, May 11 under the direction of Carmen Garcia. The group will also bring donations of food, clothing, toys and funds to be given to the Washington area Community Food Bank and Children's Defense Fund.

SCHOOL SCENE

Belen Jesuit Preparatory: The Ramon Guiteras Memorial Library will have a Book Fair from 8 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. today and Friday. The school is at 500 SW 127th Ave. Call 223-8600.

Braddock High: The PTSA will meet and honor the Challenger Patrol at an award ceremony at 7:30 p.m. Tuesday in the media center. New PTSA officers will be elected. The school is at 3601 SW 147th Ave. Call 220-9400.

Carver Middle: The Book Fair will be from 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. today through Wednesday and from 7 to 9 p.m. Tuesday. There will also be a PTSA meeting Tuesday. The school is at 4901 Lincoln Dr. in Coconut Grove. Call 444-7388.

International Student Exchange: ASSE International Student Exchange is seeking local host families for 15- to 18-year-old students from various countries. Students are fully insured and brings their own spending money. Families may select their exchange student from applications including biographical essays, photos and transcripts. For more information, call Joan Soderqvist at 341-9389 or at 1-800-473-0696.

Ponce de Leon Middle: A general PTSA meeting will be at 7 p.m. Tuesday, May 14 at the Coral Gables Police Department, 2801 Salzedo St. Refreshments will be served at 6:30 p.m. David Williams, Dade County School Board candidate, will speak about his involvement with the PTA. Anne Thompson, Florida PTA President, will speak on leadership roles. Reservations required. Call Sheila Lawless at 661-7082 or Debbie Korge at 670-2069.

Rockway Elementary: Career Day will be today. Officers from the Miami-Dade Police Department's Doral District, Sweetwater Police Department and other community organizations will be recognized as part of Police Appreciation Week. The school is at 2790 SW 93rd Ct. Call 221-1192.

Royal Green Elementary: The kindergarten orientation will be at 2:30 p.m. May 22 in the school's kindergarten wing. The school is at 13047 SW 47th St. Call 221-4452.
