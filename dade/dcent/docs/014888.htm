Published Sunday, May 12, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

W. Miami arrests male prostitutes

More than 15 male prostitutes were arrested in West Miami last weekend in a sting operation conducted by the city's Police Department in conjunction with Miami and Metro-Dade police.

Between 9 p.m. May 3 and 1 a.m. May 4, undercover officers arrested 17 men near the corner of Southwest Eighth Street and 62nd Court and charged them with soliciting to commit prostitution.



CORAL GABLES

A GABLES COUPLE stopped at a red light on Riviera Drive at South Dixie Highway were robbed by an occupant in the car behind them about 5:35 p.m. Sunday. The couple were driving north on Riviera in their 1993 Chrysler New Yorker when they stopped at the light. A man age 19 to 21 got out of a black specially outfitted 1996 Mitsubishi Eclipse behind them at the light, ran up to the passenger window, broke the glass and reached inside. He grabbed the 62-year-old woman's $30 purse which contained $40, her keys and several credit cards. The couple tried to follow the suspects, but lost them in the vicinity of Grand Avenue. When they returned home, the woman collapsed and had to be taken to Mercy Hospital.

SOMEONE SNATCHED a woman's $500 Louis Vuitton purse from her shoulder as she unlocked her car door in a swale in the 400 block of Alminar Avenue about 7 p.m. May 2. The robber also snatched the victim's $220 sunglasses off her head. Inside the purse: $100, identification, credit cards, checks and the victim's phone book.

BURGLARS KICKED down the front door of a house in the 200 block of Alesio Avenue between 10:30 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. May 3, stealing a $400 television and a $500 stereo. A neighbor said he had seen an old faded or rusted red vehicle parked in the driveway.

A 12-GAUGE SHOTGUN was stolen from a house in the 3900 block of Riviera Drive between noon April 28 and 2 p.m. April 29. The gun was valued at $100. A $5 piggy bank with about $90 in coins and bills was also stolen.

THIEVES PULLED out the left rear door window of a 1991 Honda Accord and reached in to unlock the door as it was parked in the 100 block of Sarto Avenue between 8:30 p.m. Monday and 7 a.m. Tuesday. They pried the glove box and stole a $310 cellular phone inside.

SOMEONE BROKE into a 1994 Lexus parked in the 4200 block of Salzedo Street between 11 and 11:30 a.m. Wednesday, stealing a portable phone valued at $30 and three compact discs valued at a total of $45.


CORAL PARK

INTERNATIONAL GARDENS

A BURGLAR SMASHED a window of a home in the 10200 block of Southwest 24th Street between 9 p.m. Monday and 8:20 a.m. Tuesday and stole a $400 radio, a $140 pair of in-line skates, a pair of $100 sunglasses and $200 worth of tools.


LITTLE HAVANA

TWO MEN STOLE a woman's $20 purse while she was walking in the 300 block of Southwest Ninth Avenue sometime after 1 p.m. April 22. The victim said she struggled to keep her purse, but the men suppressed her. The thieves then left in a white car. The purse contained $3 and an identification card.


MIAMI

A WOMAN'S 1995 Toyota Corolla parked in the 3400 block of Southwest Eighth Street was stolen between 10:30 p.m. May 4 and 3:30 a.m. May 5. The car, valued at $13,00, contained a $1,500 gold ring, an $800 watch, a $1,500 necklace, miscellaneous clothing and a set of keys.

A NISSAN SENTRA parked in the 1300 block of Coral Way was stolen between 2:30 and 4 p.m. May 3. It was valued at $18,000.

A MAN SAW three juveniles breaking into a car parked at Flagler Street and 21st Avenue at 2 p.m. April 29. Before the witness approached them, they ran away. Nothing was reported stolen, but signs of tampering were found on the steering column and the left rear window was broken. Repairs for the window are estimated at $60.

A $22,000 TRUCK was stolen from a parking lot at 3540 SW Coral Way sometime after 5 p.m. April 25. A $300 cellular phone was inside.


SOUTHWEST DADE

PRINCETON

FOUR ROBBERS DROVE up to a man when he pulled over at Southwest 117th Avenue and 240th Street to check a tire of his 1992 Acura Legend at 7:50 p.m. Wednesday.

``Need any help?'' they asked, and pointed guns at him. They got out of a tan Toyota Corolla and grabbed him, took his Rolex watch, portable phone, and wallet containing $300 cash, and threw him on the ground. They then got into both cars and drove away.
