Published Sunday, May 12, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

MORE PUBLIC SCHOOLS THAN EVER ARE PLANNING TO OPEN SUMMER CAMPS THIS YEAR, COSTING $60 TO $85 A WEEK, TO HELP PARENTS WHOSE CHILDREN ARE NOT ADMITTED TO ACADEMIC SUMMER SCHOOL. SO FAR, 11 SCHOOLS HAVE COMMITTED TO RUN CAMPS. AT LEAST A DOZEN OTHER ELEMENTARY AND MIDDLE SCHOOLS ARE NEGOTIATING TO OPEN THEM.

These schools have firmed up plans to offer camps from 7 a.m. to 6 p.m. from June 17 to Aug. 16, for $85 a week:

  • Calusa Elementary, 9580 W. Calusa Club Drive, 385-0589

  • Charles Hadley Elementary, 8400 NW Seventh St., 261-3453

  • Coral Gables Elementary, 105 Minorca Ave., 448-1731

  • Cutler Ridge Elementary, 20210 Coral Sea Road, 235-4611

  • David Fairchild Elementary, 5757 SW 45th St., 665-5483

  • Devon Aire Elementary, 10501 SW 122nd Ave., 274-7100

  • Miami Lakes Elementary, 14250 NW 67th Ave., 822-7757

  • North Beach Elementary, 4100 Prairie Ave., 531-7666

  • Ojus Elementary, 18600 W. Dixie Hwy., 931-4881

  • Rockway Elementary, 2790 SW 93rd Ct., 221-1192

  • Sunset Elementary, 5120 SW 72nd St., 661-8527
