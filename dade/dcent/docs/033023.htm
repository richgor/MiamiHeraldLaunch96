Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Belen Jesuit Preparatory wins social studies bowl

The Social Studies Knowledge Bowl team from Belen Jesuit Preparatory won the County Championship at the 17th annual Dade County Social Studies Knowledge Bowl County Championship March 13. Members are Jorge Fortun, Jose Ceide, Ivan Santa-Cruz, Jorge Sanchez, Pedro Carrillo, Arturo Balandra, and Ahmed Riesgo. Patrick Collins is department chairman and Jorge Martinez is coach. Jorge Sanchez is the team captain.
These are the South Miami Music Club scholarship winners for 1996, and their schools: First place, Jessica Victores, Florida Christian School; second place, Kelly Austin, Palmetto High, third place, Natalia Luckyanova, Ransom Everglades; fourth place, Christina Rosas-Guyon, Palmer Trinity; honorable mention: Audrey Siu, Palmetto High; Karly Kaplan, New World School of the Arts; Anthony Seepersad, School for the Advanced Studies; and Holli White, home school.
These students from Fairchild Elementary made the Principal's Honor Roll for the third grading period: William Chow, Kelvin Limonte, Eddy Ruano, Anahi Vada, Bet Zoufal, Denisa Mohammed, David Warfel, Siobhan Dearr, Kassandra Dunn, Julie-Ann Lavilla, Leonardo Lombardo, Mary Payan, Luis Perna, Rachael Starbuck, Carolyn Anderson, Christopher Castro, Monica de la Vega, Brian Garcia, Alexa Kuhn, Rebecca Skipp, Lauren Vega, Ariel Vidalrey, Alessandra Cruz, Veronica Diaz, Monica Kobelinski, Carina Massa, Nicolette McClendon, Robbin Moore, Alexander Robbie, Amy Scholl, Morgan Tinney, Bibi Wilson, Jonathan Blandon, Janet Lam, Taylor Dearr, Dina Figarola, Kelly Fitzpatrick, Katelyn Holsclaw, Eva Levy, Homero Nambo, Lynette Perna, Lauren Rey, Katherine Warfel, Alex Zabaleta, Jessica Cutrone, Christopher Davis, Elizabeth Harden, Jacqueline Reinante, Vanessa Zucker, Luis Castellanos, Kenneth Disalvo, Nicole Figuerola, Erika Garcia, Alexander Harden, Karina Lopez and Cristian Davis.
These students from Coconut Grove Elementary were accidentally omitted form the Principal's Honor Roll list for the third grading period: Meredith Naron and Brandon VanTuyn.

These students from Cypress Elementary are winners in the 1996 Science Fair: Keandra Cordero, primary grade winner, and Anne Perez, intermediate grade winner. The two will represent their school in the annual Elementary and Science and Mathematics Fair in Miami International Mall May 22.
These students from Coral Park High received first place in the Dade County Youth Fair Arts and Crafts Sculpture Division: Eva Veeris, Juan Rojas, Gizelle Blanco, Jose Garcia, Arleen Perez, Andrea Malo, Lynda Espana, Lury Delgado, Migual Pastrana, Luis Caballero and Angela Guyton. Guyton also won first place in the Fine Arts Division and second place at The Meaning of Music Contest sponsored by Spec's in the Grove during the Coconut Grove Arts Festival. Alejandro Martinez and Luis Cantin received third place in The Food and Nutrition poster contest, sponsored by Dade County Public Schools.
Senior Reza Adam Moghadam of New World School of the Arts is a semifinalist in the 1996 Presidential Scholars Program. Semifinalists were chosen on the basis of superior achievements, leadership qualities, personal character and community and school involvement.
These students from Belen Jesuit Preparatory have been named high scorers by National Merit Scholarship Program based on their 1995 PSAT/NMSQT scores: Carlos Artime, Arturo Balandra, Pedro Carrillo, Alexis Mantecon, Jose Menendez, Albert Palarde, Luis Patino and Ivan Santa-Cruz.

SCHOOL SCENE

Belen Jesuit Preparatory: The Literary Evening and Coffee House will begin at 7 p.m. Wednesday. Poet, art critic and Belen alumnus Ricardo Pau-Llosa will present his works and autograph copies of his books. Proceeds from the Book Fair will be applied toward the Library Automation Fund. The Ramon Guiteras Memorial Library will have a Book Fair from 8 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. Monday through Friday. The school is at 500 SW 127th Ave. Call 223-8600.

Braddock High: The PTSA will meet and honor the Challenger Patrol at an award ceremony at 7:30 p.m. May 14 in the media center. New PTSA officers will be elected. The school is at 3601 SW 147th Ave. Call 220-9400.

Carver Middle: The Book Fair will be from 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Thursday through May 15 and from 7 to 9 p.m. May 14. There will also be a PTSA meeting May 14. The school is at 4901 Lincoln Dr. in Coconut Grove. Call 444-7388.

Coral Gables High: The PTSA will meet at 7:30 p.m. Monday,in Room 702. The school is at 450 Bird Rd. Call 443-4871.

Coral Park High: The PTSA will meet at 7:30 p.m. Wednesday in the auditorium. The school is at 8865 SW 16th St. Call 226-6565.

Marjory Stoneman Douglas Elementary: The Spring School Art Show will be from 9 a.m. to 2:45 p.m. Monday in the library. The school is at 11901 SW Second St. Call 226-4356.

International Student Exchange: ASSE International Student Exchange is seeking local host families for 15- to 18-year-old students from various countries. Students are fully insured and brings their own spending money. Families may select their exchange student from applications including biographical essays, photos and transcripts. For more information, call Joan Soderqvist at 341-9389 or at 1-800-473-0696.

Palmetto High: Education the Key to AIDS Prevention will begin at 7:15 p.m. Tuesday in the auditorium. Representatives from Positive Connection, a resource and support center for HIV-positive heterosexuals, will speak and the musical group Legacy will perform music that promotes AIDS awareness. Students will present a quilt panel to be added to the AIDS Memorial Quilt. THe school is at 7460 SW 118th St. Call 235-1360.

Royal Green Elementary: The kindergarten orientation will be at 2:30 p.m. May 22 in the school's kindergarten wing. The school is at 13047 SW 47th St. Call 221-4452.

Youth Crime Watch: The Dade County chapter will have a Roll Call for Youth Crime Watch for elementary school members from 6 to 8 p.m. Tuesday at Hot Wheels, 12265 SW 112th St. Admission is free for students, parents and club advisers. Call Gwen Cooper at 470-1670.
