Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Gables murder tip could bring $21,000 reward

By ELAINE DE VALLE
Herald Staff Writer

The family and friends of Clifton Pawley -- a Gables man slain in his driveway April 24 -- have raised $20,000 to go toward a reward for anyone who can lead police to his killer or killers.

They hope someone will like the idea of having that much money more than protecting a friend or relative.

The money is added to $1,000 offered by Crimestoppers.

Metro-Dade Police don't have many clues in the murder case. A tip would be welcome, said detective Dean Sermon.

``We're looking for some guy to rat out whoever he knows did it,'' Sermon said. ``It's like the tip in the UM case. That one piece and then, `Boom,' it falls together.''

On Wednesday, police arrested a suspect in the brutal beating death of Marlin Barnes and Timwanika Lumpkins at Barnes' apartment on the University of Miami campus. That arrest was made after somebody called Crimestoppers.

Pat Sessions, a friend of the victim and his family, hopes police get the same kind of call in the Pawley case.

``They are really counting on that money for somebody to turn somebody in,'' he said.

More than half the reward money was raised by Pawley's neighbor Charles George, who went door to door asking for pledges in the neighborhood, near the Riviera Country Club.

``We asked people to give anything they felt was indicated,'' George said. About 40 neighbors chipped in with pledges ranging from $50 to $1,000.
