Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

LOCAL 4B

Bobby Paul, 50, marine carpenter, yacht captain

By HENRI E. CAUVIN
Herald Staff Writer

So long as he had a waterfront nearby and work on a house or a boat awaiting him, Bobby Paul would go almost anywhere.

A self-made shipwright, Paul parlayed his passion for rebuilding boats into a buoyant, busy career wherever he went.

For more than a decade, his home was Miami, where he lived at Dinner Key Marina and in Coconut Grove, and where he worked as a marine carpenter and a yacht captain.

His latest stop was St. Petersburg, where he moved five years ago with his wife, former Herald library director Nora Paul. There, he was the sole proprietor of Bayou Boatworks.

Injured in a motorcycle accident last week, Paul died Thursday at Bayfront Medical Center in St. Petersburg. He was 50.

``He was truly a self-taught jack-of-all-trades who could really see all sorts of interesting things to be done with almost anything that he saw,'' his brother-in-law, Herald Associate Editor Stephen K. Doig, recalled Saturday.

Paul practiced much of his craftsmanship in the four-car garage that he added onto his family's St. Petersburg home.

``Bobby was great for buying something and putting a few months' work into it and selling it for 20 times the amount he had paid,'' Doig said.

Born Aug. 3, 1945, in New York City, Robert Nathan Paul was raised in Houston, where he met his future wife, a research librarian.

They moved to Miami more than 15 years ago, when Nora came to work for The Herald. They were married a short time later at a family gathering in Tallahassee at Christmas.

As his wife took research technology to new heights at The Herald, Paul rebuilt and restored boats at Merrill Stevens Dry Dock in Miami, where he was an accomplished marine carpenter.

In the course of his work there, Paul met restaurateur Monty Trainer and later became the captain of Trainer's yacht.

Five years ago, his wife, by then an international expert in news and computer research, was offered a prestigious position at the Poynter Institute for Media Studies in St. Petersburg. Paul was game to go.

``Bobby was happy to go off to another adventure,'' Doig recalled.

Besides his wife, Paul is survived by his sons, Nathan, 11, and Spencer, 9; his daughter from an earlier marriage, Mercedes Paul; his mother, Ann Paul of St. Helena, Calif.; and his sisters, Patricia Perini, also of St. Helena, and Elizabeth Avedon of New York.

The family will hold a memorial celebration today in the Bahama Shores Drive South Park on the bay in St. Petersburg. Donations can be sent to the AIDS Coalition, 1180 Cleveland St., Clearwater, Fla. 34615.
