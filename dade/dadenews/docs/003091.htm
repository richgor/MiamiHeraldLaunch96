Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Distraught owner of dead dog accused of killing man

By FRANCES ROBLES
Herald Staff Writer

Wanted: Ulises Herrera, a 24-year-old man who police say gunned down someone in the street over a dead dog.

Murdered: Jose ``Buddha'' Bottino, 32, a Hialeah resident whose mistake was standing in front of Herrera's house when Herrera found out his beloved pooch was dead.

Police issued an arrest
warrant for Herrera, but as of Friday night he had managed to elude police. The slight, five-foot five-inch man was last seen in North Central Dade riding a green bicycle.

Police say Herrera charged out of his home at 9375 NW 30th Ct. about 2 p.m. Wednesday in a fury. His dog was dead, and he was determined to make someone pay for it.

First, Herrera buried his pet.

Then, police said, he beat his girlfriend, who is five months pregnant. He struck her stomach with his fists and a can of potato chips.

After the beating, police said Herrera discovered cash missing from his wallet, which infuriated him even more.

``He told his girlfriend that somebody was going to pay for his dog's death,'' said Metro-Dade police spokesman Rudy Espinosa. ``He was irate. He looked outside and said, `As a matter of fact, Buddha will die.' ''

Herrera, witnesses said, stormed out and headed for Bottino. He allegedly shot him several times, then turned the handgun on another man.

The gun jammed.

``Buddha was standing over there, doing nothing to nobody,'' said the would-be victim, who did not want to be identified. ``Ulises comes out of his house saying he's no good; he's this; he's that. He just shoots him in the neck and chest. He shot him like a dog for nothing. Nothing led up to it.''

Espinosa said Herrera hopped on a bicycle and rode off. Police would like anyone who knows Herrera's whereabouts to call Crimestoppers at (305) 471-TIPS.
