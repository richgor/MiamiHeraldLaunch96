Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

In Brief

ON THE WATER

Father drowns on lake outing with son

A father drowned Sunday evening while playing with his 12-year-old son in a West Kendall lake, police said.

The man, identified as Mufid Jabr, 38, of The Hammocks, was first reported missing about 8 p.m. at the man-made beach and lake at 9709 Hammocks Blvd.

``He was playing with his kid near the buoy,'' said Rudy Espinosa, a Metro-Dade police spokesman. ``He went under for no apparent reason near a drop-off in the lake. According to responding officers, the family said he didn't know how to swim.''

Once he disappeared, his family could not locate him and they called for help.

Police divers and rescue workers searched for more than an hour in the dark before locating his body.

IN CUBA

Six migrants repatriated after rescue

Six Cuban migrants were repatriated to Bahi de Cabanas, Cuba, on Sunday, four days after being rescued from a sinking tugboat 54 miles southeast of Andros Island.

The migrants were spotted by a Coast Guard plane Thursday, said Petty Officer Scott Carr. The Coast Guard cutter Padre was dispatched to meet the 35-foot tugboat, where they gave the migrants food, water and medical attention.

``They immediately took them on board when they saw the boat was taking on water,'' Carr said.

The rescue brings the total number of Cuban migrants rescued at sea to 95 this year, Carr said.

ON THE STREETS

Gables jewelry store robbed at gunpoint

Two men, at least one with a gun, on Monday robbed the Mayor's jewelry store on Miracle Mile in Coral Gables and stole merchandise worth more than $20,000, police said.

The gunman whipped out a firearm when an employee was showing jewelry to a customer and stole several items. No one was injured during the incident.

The store manager would not comment.

GOVERNMENT IN ACTION

Metro rescinds self-imposed port fine

When Dade County building officials discovered earlier this year that the Port of Miami had built two passenger terminals without proper building permits, the county fined itself $17,159 for violating the South Florida Building Code. But Port Director Carmen Lunetta won't have to pay.

Guillermo Olmedillo, acting director of Metro's Department of Planning Development and Regulation, said Monday he rescinded the fine after determining that former Metro building official Carlos Bonzon authorized the construction.

``I couldn't very well go out and fine someone who had this verification,'' Olmedillo said.

The state Department of Business & Professional Regulation launched an investigation of the case in March. Olmedillo said he was interviewed last week by an investigator. Bonzon contends he acted within his authority.

AROUND TOWN

South Florida history course offered

Final registration is under way for Paul George's ``Miami and South Florida History'' course to be taught during the spring term at Miami-Dade Community College Wolfson Campus.

Classes will meet from 5:30 to 8:30 p.m. Tuesdays and Thursdays, from today through June 13.

The course (AMH 2079) is open to anyone with an interest in local history and includes lectures, guest speakers, videos, slide presentations, visits to historical exhibits and George's walking tours of Miami. For information, call (305) 237-3723.

IN BROWARD

No plans to fill sinkhole

There were no immediate plans Monday to fill the Miramar sinkhole, as homeowners and developer Lennar reached a standoff on a plan to test for debris under homes.

A third plan of action submitted by Lennar late Friday still did not call for testing under every house in Hampshire Homes -- something homeowners are adamant about. They don't want the pit filled in until Lennar agrees to the testing.

The Department of Natural Resource Protection also found Lennar's latest plan lacked one detail: a record of how and when the garbage was buried on the site.
