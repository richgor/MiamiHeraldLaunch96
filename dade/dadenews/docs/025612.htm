Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

LOCAL 2B

Exile claims he has orders to assassinate Cuban leaders

Mas Canosa


A Cuban exile went to the offices of the Cuban American National Foundation in Miami last week and said he had orders from Cuban President Fidel Castro to assassinate leaders of the foundation, said a source familiar with the case.

The source said that several days ago, a Cuban who had been in Miami for some time went to foundation offices at 7300 NW 35th Ter. and said he had orders from Havana to kill several foundation leaders, including Jorge Mas Canosa, chairman; Francisco ``Pepe'' Hernandez, president; and Ninoska Perez, director of La Voz de la Fundacion, the organization's radio station.

The foundation staff contacted the FBI after hearing the man's account, the source said. The foundation declined to comment on the matter.

The source did not know the name or the current location of the self-declared Castro agent.

The FBI was tight-lipped about the incident. ``We are not going to make any comment,'' FBI spokesman Paul Miller said.

But a law enforcement source who asked not to be identified told The Herald on Saturday that the supposed plot was ``craziness'' and that the man who confessed is not considered credible.
