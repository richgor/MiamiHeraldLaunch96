Published Thursday, May 9, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Latest suggestion: Let parking fees finance new arena garage

By DON FINEFROCK
Herald Staff Writer

Dade County is mapping a new game plan for building a basketball arena, parking garage and retail development on Biscayne Bay in downtown Miami.

The latest wrinkle: The county may rely on parking fees to finance the construction of a parking garage for Miami Heat fans.

The county has promised to build 2,500 parking spaces next to the new arena, but Metro managers have had to scramble to find enough money to deliver that pledge.

The Metro Commission approved a $210 million financial package March 29, but that plan included state grant money that can't be used for the project.

The county is now working to plug that gap. Details haven't been made public, but the broad outlines of the plan are known.

County managers say the retail development -- which accounted for $30 million of the original $210 million -- may be financed by a private developer, in partnership with the Heat.

The county would then use cash produced by the development -- parking fees and rent payments -- to pay for a portion of the arena project.

Details of that plan may be available later this week.

Negotiators for the Heat and the county continue to work on four agreements -- management, development, licensing and assurance -- that will define how the arena is built and operated. The entire package may be submitted to the commission for a vote May 21.

As of Wednesday, however, Metro officials were still exploring whether those parking fees and rent payments could replace the state grant money, which totaled $5.1 million a year.

Two reports by outside consultants appear to support the county's latest financing plan -- but officials cautioned the numbers are preliminary.

In a draft report delivered this week to Port of Miami director Carmen Lunetta, Arthur Andersen estimated a retail and entertainment center of about 200,000 square feet could generate payments of at least $2.4 million.

Lunetta has said the county needs $2 million to $2.5 million a year to round out the financing for the $165 million arena.

Arthur Andersen suggests that's feasible, but the county has yet to identify a developer or describe the project in detail.

Nevertheless, a separate report released last week suggests the development could draw enough shoppers and tourists to support a parking garage.

Desman Associates of Chicago estimated visitors would pay $2.9 million a year to park in the arena's garage -- leaving $2 million after expenses.

That assumes a typical customer would pay $2 to park during the day and $6 at night. Desman did not count parking fees paid by basketball fans, since those revenues are earmarked for the arena.

If Desman's estimate is accurate, that $2 million could be used to pay for the garage. Lunetta has said the county would need about $2.2 million to cover the debt service on 2,500 parking spaces.

Extra money to cover the debt and provide a small cushion for lenders could come from parking fees at the Port of Miami.

The county has agreed to provide 2,000 parking spaces at the port for basketball fans, who would be shuttled to the arena. Those spaces could generate $762,000 a year, Desman said.

If the county needs a larger cushion to obtain financing, Lunetta suggested the Heat could pledge some of the parking revenue that will flow to the arena.

Eric Woolworth, general counsel for the team, said that option had been discussed but that no decision had been made.

Lunetta also said the Miami Parking System, the agency that operates city parking meters and garages, may agree to issue bonds to build the garage.

Executive Director Clark Cook said the agency typically requires an income stream of 1 1/2 times the debt payment for its bonds.
