Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Writer Ruben Dario is subject of lecture

The life of Nicaraguan writer Ruben Dario will be the topic of a lecture tonight, sponsored by the Ruben Dario International Foundation and the Pan-American Roundtable.

The creator of literary modernism, Dario -- born Felix Ruben Garcia Sarmiento -- is best known for works such as Blue, Profane Prose and Songs of Life and Hope.

Dario scholar Armando Luna-Silva will deliver the lecture at 7 p.m. at the South Miami Hospital Auditorium, 6200 SW 73rd St. Admission is $15. For more information or reservations, call Eduardo Vilchez at (305) 227-5881 or Mauricio Neret at (305) 665-7169 or (305) 325-1907.
