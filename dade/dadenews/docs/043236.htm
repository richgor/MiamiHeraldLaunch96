ON THE STREETS

Burglary suspects surrender at house

Two suspected burglars hid in a West Kendall house for more than two hours Sunday while police surrounded the place.

It started at 10 a.m. when a neighbor called saying there appeared to be a break-in in the 10000 block of Southwest 144th Avenue. Police arrived to discover someone had broken in through the back.

Metro-Dade police dogs had another discovery: Someone was in the attic.

The SWAT team was called out to force the burglars out, but after several hours, the suspects came out willingly. A 16-year-old and an 18-year-old were charged with burglary. Their names were not available Sunday.

Police spokesman Juan Del Castillo said SWAT was called because police saw rifles among the possessions the alleged burglars had stacked up at the door to steal. ``We didn't know if maybe they had a gun there that they took and slipped into their waistband,'' Del Castillo said.
