A wildlife group needs funds, so it's throwing . . .

A party for the birds

By SUSANA BELLIDO
Herald Staff Writer

KEY WEST -- All eyes turn to the little table in the middle when someone new arrives -- raccoon eyes, rabbit, chicken, hawks, owls and prairie dog eyes.

That's where volunteers of Wildlife Rescue of the Florida Keys take blood samples, wrap wings or give physical exams to the 1,000 injured animals that require their help every year.

``It would be nice not to be in the middle of 150 animals staring at the animal you're trying to calm down,'' said Becky Barron, the group's director. ``It's like if the doctor is trying to check you out in the emergency room and you're in the middle of the waiting room. It would be unnerv
ing.''

For the past year and a half, Wildlife Rescue has run its little animal hospital from a chicken-wire building at McCoy Indigenous Park in Key West. It's much better quarters than those the group had before at the Key West bight.

But volunteers believe they could do a lot more for their patients with a few improvements. They hope a Wildlife Wingding party next weekend will help them raise the $15,000 they figure they need to build a treatment room, office and rehabilitation cage.

``They're just going to be a little fancier shed,'' Barron said.

Although the existing prefabricated building gives animals shade and rain protection, no parts of it are totally enclosed. The office now is a plastic storage closet a few steps away from the multipurpose table. Once a week, volunteers must drive the injured birds to a flight table in Tavernier for the exercise they need to be eventually released into the wild.

``It doesn't make any sense,'' Barron said.

The group, which operates on private donations only, has already raised $3,000 for construction.

An auction, music and other activities are planned for the fund-raiser at Indigenous Park at the corner of White Street and Atlantic Boulevard.

All for the birds.

``They'll come whether we build it or not,'' Barron said.




For more information or if you find an injured animal, call 294-1441 in Key West, 743-7373 in Marathon and 852-4486 in Tavernier.
