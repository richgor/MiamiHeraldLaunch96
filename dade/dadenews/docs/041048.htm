Published Tuesday, May 7, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

5 Miami residents charged with defrauding Medicaid

Associated Press

Four members of a Miami family were arrested Monday on charges they defrauded Medicaid of nearly $135,000 in the rental of wheelchairs, artificial limbs, and oxygen tanks and tents.

A fifth Miami man was also charged.

The arrests were the latest in a four-month investigation by Florida's 13th statewide grand jury into Medicaid fraud.

The five people arrested Monday face five to 10 years in prison if convicted.

The latest people arrested:

  • Teresita Hernandez, 38, was charged with 73 counts of Medicaid provider fraud in cases involving $14,297 at T&T Medical Rental Equipment. She was charged with one count of organized fraud and 64 counts of Medicaid provider fraud in cases involving $62,920 at G&T Medical Supplies.

  • Her brother, Gabriel Hernandez, 40, was charged with 149 counts of Medicaid provider fraud for cases involving $37,878 at Royal Medical.

  • His wife, Lourdes Yanes, 26, also faces 149 counts of Medicaid provider fraud for cases involving $37,878 at Royal Medical. She also faces one count of organized fraud and 64 counts of Medicaid provider fraud in cases involving $62,920 at G&T Medical Supplies.

  • Alejandrina Mirella Fantony, 57, was charged with 99 counts of Medicaid provider fraud in cases involving $19,652 at Tamiami Medical. She is Hernandez's aunt.

  • Jesus Acevedo, 24, was charged with 99 counts of Medicaid provider fraud in cases involving $19,652 at Tamiami Medical.
