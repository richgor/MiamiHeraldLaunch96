Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Police cadet guilty in car repossession case

By MANNY GARCIA
Herald Staff Writer

Metro-Dade police cadet Juan Suarez, who once planned to put criminals behind bars, is going to prison.

After deliberating about six hours, a jury convicted him Tuesday afternoon of robbery and burglary. He faces a likely sentence of three to five years in prison, although Dade Circuit Judge Martin Kahn could sentence him to life.

Suarez, 25, lowered his head in disbelief as the jury verdict was read. Hours earlier, he testified that he never aimed a shotgun at a night watchman last year or stole his white 1988 Ford Mustang convertible, which had been repossessed.

``It's a shame,'' said prosecutor PercyMartinez. ``This young man destroyed a promising career.''

Suarez was 10 days shy of graduating from the police academy, which lasts nine months, when he was arrested.

On Monday, Miami detective Rudy Herrera told jurors that the defendant ``loved his car,'' and that is why he stole it from the repo yard Sept. 10.

But Suarez and his lawyer, Michael Voigt, challenged that assertion. Suarez said he was not infatuated with the Mustang. He merely ``loved all sports cars.''

They also challenged the credibility of the state's star witness, Manuel Medina, the watchman who testified that Suarez pointed a shotgun at him to steal the car. Voigt told jurors that Medina suffers from psychiatric problems, takes Zanax for anxiety and needed a magnifying glass to identify his client.
