Published Saturday, May 11, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Convicted killer from Dade commits suicide on Death Row

By MANNY GARCIA
Herald Staff Writer

Lazaro Martinez, a former Alamo rental car agency employee who was on Death Row for killing two managers, is dead.

Martinez, 35, killed himself early Sunday at the Union Correctional Institute, a maximum-security prison in the North Florida town of Starke.

``It's a suicide,'' said Gene Morris, a spokesman for the Florida Department of Corrections. ``And he left a suicide note.''

Morris said he did not know the contents of the note or how Martinez killed himself.

Morris said a guard found Martinez dead in his cell just before 6 a.m. Martinez was last seen alive 30 minutes earlier. Like all Death Row inmates, he had a cell to himself.

Union Correctional is across the road from Florida State Prison, home of the electric chair. Men on Death Row are housed in both facilities.

Two years ago, Dade Circuit Judge Thomas Carney sentenced Martinez to death for shooting two Alamo supervisors in 1991. During the trial, prosecutor Sally Weintraub told jurors that Martinez took a taxi to the rental agency two days after he was fired for fighting.

Weintraub said he came out of the cab armed with a pistol and shotgun and began firing. Scores of tourists and Alamo employees dived for cover around the busy agency, east of Miami International Airport.

Martinez killed Ciro Sigler, 31, and Chester Scheel, 49, wounded a third manager, and then started shooting at a police officer. Employees tackled Martinez when he ran out of ammunition.

At the trial, defense lawyer Arthur Carter argued that his client was insane at the time of the shooting.
