Published Friday, May 10, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Eight convicted on drug charges

Gang sold cocaine in Miami

By GAIL EPSTEIN
Herald Staff Writer

For much of the past decade, the Saldana brothers -- Francisco, Jose and Henry -- sold hundreds of pounds of cocaine from behind the barred windows and locked doors of their elderly grandmother's house in Miami's Design District.

Now Henry Saldana is dead, shot in a drug-related homicide; the grandmother also has died, and Francisco and Jose face spending the rest of their lives in federal prison.

The Saldanas were among eight members of an inner-city crack cocaine gang convicted by a federal jury Wednesday. Prosecutors said the group, called the Buena Vista Boys, locked members inside crack houses with guns and ordered them to distribute drugs through barred windows in a bid to prevent rip-offs.

All the defendants were convicted on conspiracy charges. Additionally, Jose and Francisco Saldana were convicted of firearms violations, and Francisco Saldana was convicted under the federal ``drug kingpin'' statute.

Police investigated the group for two years. They executed nine search warrants at various sites, seizing more than 20 firearms, $100,000 and crack-processing paraphernalia.

Prosecutors said the main crack house was at 22 NE 43rd St., which belonged to Delia Varela, the Saldana brothers' 83-year-old grandmother.

Varela went for groceries Feb. 20, 1995, and collapsed dead in the 4100 block of Northeast Second Avenue. Her body lay unclaimed in the Dade County morgue for 5 1/2 months before her grandsons saw a Herald article on unidentified cadavers and recognized one of them as their grandmother.

Varela's body was held at the morgue at the same time her grandson Henry Saldana, 28, wound up there. He was shot to death July 28, 1995, in a drug-related homicide. One week later, the remaining Saldana brothers claimed their grandmother's body.

Sentencing is pending for all those convicted. Besides the Saldana brothers, they are Andre Scales, Jesus Morejon, Quamil Haywood, Frederick Knight, David Velez and Stephen Harris.
