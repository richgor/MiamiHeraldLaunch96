Published Sunday, May 12, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

KEEPING THE SCORE

The Herald's eight selected observers -- Dominic Calabro, president of Tallahassee-based Florida Tax Watch; Bill Cotterell, capital bureau chief for The Tallahassee Democrat; Pete Dunbar, former legislator from Pinellas County and general counsel to ex-Gov. Bob Martinez, now a lobbyist and lawyer; Casey Gluckman, environmental and hospital lobbyist; Victoria Hernandez, lobbyist for Miami-Dade Community College; Steve MacNamara, FSU communications professor, lawyer, former lobbyist and former secretary of Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation; Kelvin Robinson, Florida League of Cities lobbyist and former bureau chief for the state Department of Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles; and Mark Silva, capital bureau chief for The Herald -- rated the effectiveness of local members of the 1996 Legislature.

House and Senate members were ranked separately. Each chamber is divided into quarters. Legislators who made the top quarter are listed in numerical order. Legislators in the bottom three quarters are listed alphabetically within their respective quarters. Those in the highest-ranked quarter received a four-star rating. The next quarter was assigned a three-star rating. The next-to-last quarter gets two stars. Bottom-quarter lawmakers get one star.

Legislative profiles were written by Dade political writer Karen Branch, with the exception of the profiles of Rep. Steve Geller and Sen. Howard Forman, which were written by Broward political writer Steve Bousquet.
