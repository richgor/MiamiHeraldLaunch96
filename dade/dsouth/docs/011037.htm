Belen Jesuit Preparatory wins social studies bowl

The Social Studies Knowledge Bowl team from Belen Jesuit Preparatory won the County Championship at the 17th annual Dade County Social Studies Knowledge Bowl County Championship March 13. members are Jorge Fortun, Jose Ceide, Ivan Santa-Cruz, Jorge Sanchez, Pedro Carrillo, Arturo Balandra, and Ahmed Riesgo. Patrick Collins is department chairman and Jorge Martinez is coach. Jorge Sanchez is the team captain.
These are the South Miami Music Club scholarship winners for 1996, and their schools: first place, Jessica Victores, Florida Christian School; second place, Kelly Austin, Palmetto High, third place, Natalia Luckyanova, Ransom Everglades; fourth place, Christina Rosas-Guyon, Palmer Trinity; honorable mention: Audrey Siu, Palmetto High; Karly Kaplan, New World School of the Arts; Anthony Seepersad, School for the Advanced Studies; and Holli White, home school.

SCHOOL SCENE

Alexander Montessori: A slide presentation on the toddler program for children 2 and 3 will be at 8 p.m. Tuesday. Parents can visit the Toddle Children's Houses. The school is at 14400 Old Cutler Rd. Call 665-6274.

Belen Jesuit Preparatory: The Literary Evening and Coffee House will begin at 7 p.m. Wednesday. Poet, art critic and Belen alumnus Ricardo Pau-Llosa will present his works and autograph copies of his books. Proceeds from the Book Fair will be applied toward the Library Automation Fund. The Ramon Guiteras Memorial Library will have a Book Fair from 8 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. Monday through Friday. The school is at 500 SW 127th Ave. Call 223-8600.

Coral Gables High: The PTSA will meet at 7:30 p.m. Monday in Room 702. The school is at 450 Bird Rd. Call 443-4871.

Cutler Ridge Middle: The PTSA will meet at 7 p.m. Tuesday. Coral Reef High magnet school principal Greg Zawyer will discuss the new school. Cutler Ridge Middle is at 19400 SW 97th Ave. Call 254-3746.

International Student Exchange: ASSE International Student Exchange is seeking local host families for 15- to 18-year-old students from various countries. Each student is fully insured and brings their own spending money. Families may select their exchange student from applications including biographical essays, photos and transcripts. For more information, call Joan Soderqvist at 341-9389 or at 1-800-473-0696.

Leisure City Elementary: The Bright Horizon Resource Center Project meets from 3 to 4 p.m. Monday through Thursday. Students can get homework assistance in English and Spanish. The school is at 14950 SW 288th St. Call 247-5170.

Naranja Elementary: Parents of entering prekindergarten students will meet at 9:15 a.m. Wednesday in the cafeteria. Kindergarten registration is open from 9 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. The school is at 13990 SW 264th St. Call 258-3401.

Palmetto High: Education the Key to AIDS Prevention will begin at 7:15 p.m. Tuesday in the auditorium. Representatives from Positive Connection, a resource and support center for HIV-positive heterosexuals, will speak and the musical group Legacy will perform music that promotes AIDS awareness. Students will present a quilt panel to be added to the AIDS Memorial Quilt. THe school is at 7460 SW 118th St. Call 235-1360.

Youth Crime Watch: The Dade County chapter will have a Roll Call for Youth Crime Watch for elementary school members from 6 to 8 p.m. Tuesday at Hot Wheels, 12265 SW 112th St. Admission is free for students, parents and club advisers. Call Gwen Cooper at 470-1670.

Edward Whigham Elementary: This new school opens in September. There will be a meeting for parents of students who will attend Whigham at 7 p.m. May 14 in the Gulfstream Elementary cafeteria, 20900 SW 97th St. Other parents who live within the new school's boundaries also are invited. Call 235-6811.
