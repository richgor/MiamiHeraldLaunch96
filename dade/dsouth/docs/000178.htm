Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Museum hosting special events for Family Day

The Historical Museum of Southern Florida will kick off Family Day Saturday.

The free event -- part of the ongoing city of Miami's 100th birthday celebration -- will be at the Metro-Dade Cultural Center, 101 W. Flagler St., from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m.

``It's a celebration of Miami's diverse heritage,'' said Rebecca Eads, marketing coordinator for the museum. ``It will focus on the different cultures from different communities in South Florida.''

Visitors will be treated to a variety of events, including the unveiling of the official Miami Centennial Quilt. The collaborative work of art represents the many images of Miami's history and cultures.

Miami-based groups, including The Miami Jackson High School Jazz Ensemble, the 21st Century Steelband, Sosyete Koukouy and the Ife-Ile Dance and Music Ensemble, will entertain.

The 21st Century Steelband will perform calypsos, and the Sosyete Koukouy will concentrate on Haitian dances, like the Kongo and Rara.

Other family activities include a craft workshop, storytelling and a petting zoo. Chony Gutierrez will demonstrate Nicaraguan festival crafts, and Haitian crafts, paintings and other art pieces will be on sale.

Guided tours of the Historical Museum's Miami Centennial exhibition will be available. The collection includes rare artifacts and photographs detailing Miami's transition from a wilderness to the city that exists today.
