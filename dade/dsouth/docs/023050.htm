Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

FIU students win first place at United Nations Conference

A team of students from Florida International University received a first-place award for Outstanding Delegation at the recent 1996 National Model United Nations Conference competition in New York City.

The 22-member model U.N. team competed against more than 2,000 students from 152 colleges and universities in North and South America, Europe, the Middle East and Asia.

The weeklong competition provides students the opportunity to portray U.N. delegates. Participants worked together to resolve international conflicts and remedy global problems concerning the environment, population, disease and human rights through universal cooperation and peaceful resolution.

The FIU delegation, which represented Romania, spent four months gathering research and preparing position papers for the competition.

Victoria Cervantes of the Little Havana area, a graduate student studying political science at Florida International University, recently received a 1996 Florida Excellence in Service Award.
Cristina de Zarraga is studying at Cross Cultural Center in Seville, Spain, for the spring semester. A student at Trinity College in Hartford, Conn., de Zarraga is a member of the class of 1997 and majoring in psychology. Danielle Lyon of Kendall recently was named to the dean's list at Barry University. Lyon, an elementary education major, also has pledged Kappa Delta Pi, an international honor society in education and will be inducted in the fall. She is the daughter of Michalineand Jose Perez of Kendall.
Melissa Moreno, a 1992 graduate of Our Lady of Lourdes Academy, will graduate magna cum laude from Butler University in Indianapolis, Ind. She recently was selected as the recipient of an outstanding student recognition award for character, scholarship and leadership during her four years at Butler. She will attend Purdue University in the fall where she has received a master's graduate opportunities fellowship to continue her studies in speech pathology.
David Reese, a 1995 graduate of Coral Gables High School, recently was selected to take part in an honors program for study abroad. He will take 12 credits in Holland for the fall semester. Reese recently completed his first year at the University of Florida at Gainesville.
Jorge Edgardo Reynardus of Southwest Dade, was one of 20 winners of the 1996 Louis L. Redding Minority First Year Law Student Summer Fellowships presented by the Redding Fellows Committee of the Delaware State Bar Association. Reynardus is a graduate of Florida International University and currently attends the University of Pennsylvania Law School.
Eric Sapp, a sophomore at Davidson College in North Carolina, recently received one of three Edward Crosland Stuart Scholarships awarded at the school this year. Sapp, a political science major and 1994 graduate of Gulliver Preparatory School, is the son of Dr. Stephen and Dr. Mary Sapp of Kendall. The award includes $10,000 for the student's junior year, renewable for the senior year.
