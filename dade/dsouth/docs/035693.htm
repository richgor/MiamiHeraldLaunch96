Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Hialeah benefit calling all Bogies, Bergmans

Hialeah high rollers can try their luck at a May 18 casino benefit for the Hialeah Foundation.

``Casablanca'' -- a night of gambling complete with a Humphrey Bogart look-alike who will greet patrons at the door -- begins with dinner at 7 p.m. and dancing at 9 p.m. in the Doral Beach Hotel, 4833 Collins Ave.

Tickets start at $150 a person. Men are encouraged to dress like Bogart, women like Ingrid Bergman. Fez and Fedora hats will be supplied.

Lola, a '40s jazz singer, will perform with her band and ``Sam'' at the piano. Each patron will get $1,000 in casino chips, and prizes will be given away at night's end.

``This year's Morocco-type theme is very nostalgic,'' said Maria Diaz, executive director of the foundation. ``It should be a lot of fun.''

About 600 people attended last year's fund-raiser, ``A Night in Monte Carlo,'' which generated more than $35,000. The foundation donated the bulk of that money to Hialeah Hospital for equipment and supplies at Hialeah High's new Student Health Center, which officially opened April 19.

The center provides primary care, counseling and health education to 3,000 teenagers.

Those interested in attending or sponsoring the event should call Diaz at 696-8110.
-- JACK REJTMAN
