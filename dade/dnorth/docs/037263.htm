Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Mariachis and margaritas mark Cinco de Mayo

By RICK JERVIS
Herald Staff Writer

On May 5, 1862, Mexican forces, outnumbered 3-to-1, fended off an invading French army in the Battle of the Puebla.

The date became etched in history as Cinco de Mayo -- the Mexican day of independence.

Miami Beach will help celebrate Mexico's freedom Sunday with boisterous, tequila-splashed parties all around town.

The loudest cheer for victory will probably be heard from Moe's Cantina, 616 Collins Ave. The 10,000-square-foot restaurant will host a Cinco de Mayo celebration complete with a new brunch menu, a mariachi band and 24 different brands of tequila.

``There's something about margaritas that make people have just that much more fun,'' co-owner Conor Lumpkin said.

Tequila Girls will rove the premises, offering straight shots or ``upside down margaritas,'' where the mixture is poured into the client's mouth, then the head shaken. These are $3 each.

Opened four months, Moe's was designed to look like a genuine Mexican compound. One bar was pulled out of a Juarez, Mexico cantina. Another bar is a replica of a Mexican mission.

``The idea was to have it feel like it's been here for 150 years,'' Lumpkin said.

Other Cinco de Mayo celebrations around the beach:

  • Mrs. Mendoza's Tacos Al Carbon restaurant, 1040 Alton Rd., will slash its beer prices and give away T-shirts. Also, Mexican sodas called ``Jarritos'' will be imported just for the day.

  • El Rancho Grande Restaurant, 1626 Pennsylvania Ave., will open at 3 p.m. Sunday, three hours earlier than usual. Margaritas will be $3, beer $2.

    ``Normally for Cinco de Mayo, it's well packed,'' owner Manuel Ortiz said. ``It's a good way to celebrate Mexico. And it's good for business.''

  • Mex Mess Cantina Mexicana, 1522 Washington Ave., will also celebrate with 10 different Mexican beers and decorations.
