Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Pair robs pizza delivery man, 1 accidentally gets shot

Two hungry gunmen would have gotten away with the dough if one of them hadn't been clumsy.

After the teens stole two large pizzas, two six-packs of Pepsi, a set of car keys and $50 from a delivery man at gunpoint, the gunman accidentally shot himself in the leg.

The robbers held up Fernando Galindo, who was delivering pizzas to an apartment at 926 79th Ter. at 11:10 p.m. Tuesday. Police say one of them pointed a gun at Galindo and ordered him to ``give up the pizza.'' The other man then grabbed the two large pizzas and two six-packs of Pepsi from Galindo.

When the gunman demanded money and the keys to the car, Galindo handed over $50 in cash.

As the robbers ran away, the gunman dropped his gun. The .380 caliber handgun fired when it hit the pavement, shooting the gunman in the right leg.

The teens ran home to an apartment building in the 700 block of 82nd Street, where the gunman's mother called 911.

Police quickly identified the teens as those who robbed Galindo. The teens were charged with armed robbery and use of a firearm during the commission of a felony.

--DEBRA FRANCO
