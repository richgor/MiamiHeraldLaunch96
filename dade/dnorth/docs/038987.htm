Published Sunday, May 5, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

Ride, race even without mountains

Bike competition is at Oleta park

By CHERIE HENDERSON
Herald Staff Writer

Oleta River State Recreation Area, perhaps the best kept secret for recreation in Northeast Dade, is hoping to raise its profile with this weekend's Bud Light Mountain Bike Festival.

``This will give people a reason to come out to the park who don't come out here,'' said Warren Poplin, assistant park manager.

The festival, which began Saturday and runs most of today, features races for all experience levels, from first timers to experts, on a new path built over the last several months by a volunteer crew.

Fans can watch parts of the race that cut through open areas, and can learn more about mountain biking.

Kids will have a race of their own, with parents allowed to lend a steadying hand to help their offspring conquer the hills. A charity race for waiters and bartenders will benefit the Children's Home Society.

Next year -- if there is a next year for this first-time event -- Poplin hopes the 854-acre park itself will be a beneficiary. This year, festival fans will be paying the usual park entrance fee of $3.25 per car, but that money goes back to the state.

The park is getting lots of exposure, though, race promoter Steve Tebon said. Its name has been on everything from entry forms to posters to squeeze bottles.

The weekend festival, the first stop on a four-city tour that next travels to Georgia, suffered one black eye last month when the city of North Miami withdrew its offer of support as a local sponsor after its name failed to show up on promotions.

Tebon said the error was an oversight caused because the city had committed its support so early in the planning.
