Published Wednesday, May 8, 1996, in the Miami Herald.

WALTER O. BENTON JR.

Chemical salesman

By ANNE MARTINEZ
Herald Writer

Walter O. Benton Jr. loved the outdoors. Whether working as a chemical salesman servicing area golf courses or fishing, he was at home outside.

Benton, 70, died Sunday at North Ridge Medical Center in Fort Lauderdale of complications related to a blood clot.

A graduate of Miami Edison High School, Benton, a Miami native and Cooper City resident, enlisted in the Coast Guard during World War II even before the school's graduation ceremony.

``His mother didn't want him to go because he hadn't yet graduated,'' said his wife, Elyse, who works in The Herald's classified advertising department. ``She signed a consent form so that he could join, and he graduated without finishing off the school year.''

Benton worked 25 years for W.R. Grace Co. He retired in 1988. He was a member of the Florida Golf Course Superintendents Association.

Besides his wife, Benton is survived by a son, Tom; daughters Terri Zell and Tammy Massaro; sister Mary Morgan of Lincoln, Del.; and four grandchildren.

Services will be held at 11 a.m. today at Fred Hunter's University Drive Funeral Home, 2401 S. University Dr., Davie. Burial will follow at Fort Lauderdale Memorial Park.

In lieu of flowers, the family requests that donations be made to the American Heart Association or the American Cancer Society.
